## Scope of the practical

This post covers ESR resonance, Zeeman and hyperfine splitting, photoelectric effect, semiconductor energy gap, X-ray spectra, lattice parameter of cubic crystals, and frequency-dependent dielectric constant.

## Standard experiment titles used in this record

The broad syllabus terms are interpreted as the usual experiments: ESR resonance and $g$-factor; Zeeman effect and hyperfine splitting; verification of Einstein's photoelectric equation; energy gap from a semiconductor temperature characteristic; X-ray intensity variation with tube voltage and current; lattice parameter of a cubic crystal by Bragg diffraction; and dielectric constant as a function of frequency.

## Apparatus

ESR spectrometer, microwave source, electromagnet, optical spectrometer, photoelectric cell, semiconductor diode, X-ray unit, crystal sample, and LCR meter.

## Essential relations

ESR resonance follows $h\nu=g\mu_BB$. Einstein's photoelectric equation is $eV_s=h\nu-\phi$. Bragg's law is $2d\sin\theta=n\lambda$. For a cubic crystal, the lattice parameter follows from the measured interplanar spacing and Miller indices.

## Results

The resonance, quantum, diffraction, and band-structure relations are verified using the corresponding fitted graphs and spectral readings.

## Precautions

Use radiation shielding, never bypass interlocks, avoid direct laser or UV exposure, calibrate the field and wavelength scales, and record background signals.

## Viva Questions

1. **What is ESR?** Resonant absorption of microwaves by unpaired electron spins in a magnetic field.
2. **What is stopping potential?** The reverse potential needed to stop the most energetic photoelectrons.
3. **Why does X-ray intensity increase with tube current?** More electrons strike the target per unit time.
4. **What is lattice parameter?** The characteristic unit-cell length of a crystal lattice.
