## Aim

To determine the complex dielectric constant and plasma frequency from surface-plasmon-resonance measurements and to determine the refractive index of a dielectric layer.

## Standard experiment titles used in this record

The usual experiments meant here are: determination of the complex dielectric function and plasma frequency from an SPR reflectance curve, and determination of the refractive index of a dielectric overlayer from the SPR resonance-angle shift.

## Apparatus

SPR prism assembly, metal film, laser, photodetector, rotation stage, dielectric sample, and computer interface.

## Theory

The resonance angle depends on the dielectric function of the sample and the thickness and optical constants of the metal film. From the measured reflectance curve, the resonance angle and linewidth give the real and imaginary parts of the dielectric response. The plasma frequency is related to the carrier density by $\omega_p^2=ne^2/(\epsilon_0m^*)$.

## Observations

| Sample | Resonance angle | Linewidth | Refractive index |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Air reference | $44.2^\circ$ | $1.8^\circ$ | 1.000 |
| Liquid sample | $51.6^\circ$ | $2.4^\circ$ | 1.333 |
| Dielectric layer | $56.1^\circ$ | $3.0^\circ$ | 1.510 |

## Result

The resonance shift gives the refractive index of the dielectric layer as

$$\boxed{n=1.510}.$$

The resonance width indicates the absorptive, or imaginary, part of the dielectric response.

## Precautions

Keep the prism and metal film clean, remove air bubbles, maintain fixed polarisation, and record the complete reflectance curve around resonance.

## Viva Questions

1. **What is surface plasmon resonance?** Resonant excitation of collective electron oscillations at a metal-dielectric interface.
2. **Why is p-polarised light used?** It has an electric-field component normal to the interface.
3. **What does resonance-angle shift measure?** A change in the refractive index near the metal surface.
