## Scope of the practical

This post covers laser wavelength by grating, thin-wire thickness by laser or microscope, Brewster's law, Michelson and Fabry-Perot interferometers, resolving power of a telescope and prism, solar-cell characteristics, holography, and microwave experiments.

## Standard experiment titles used in this record

The less specific syllabus entries are taken in their usual form: holographic recording and reconstruction; microwave wavelength by a standing-wave pattern; microwave verification of reflection, refraction, or polarisation; and solar-cell $I$-$V$ characteristics with fill factor and efficiency.

## Essential relations

Use $d\sin\theta=n\lambda$ for a grating, $\mu=\tan i_p$ for Brewster's law, and $\lambda=2d/m$ for Michelson fringes. For a solar cell, determine $I_{sc}$, $V_{oc}$, fill factor, and efficiency from the I-V curve.

## Viva Questions

1. **Why is a laser useful in optics?** It is coherent, nearly monochromatic, and highly directional.
2. **What is Brewster angle?** The incidence angle at which reflected and refracted rays are perpendicular.
3. **What is resolving power?** The ability to distinguish two close wavelengths or angular objects.
4. **What is holography?** Recording and reconstructing both amplitude and phase information of a wavefront.
5. **Why are microwaves suitable for interference experiments?** Their wavelength is large enough for convenient bench measurements.
