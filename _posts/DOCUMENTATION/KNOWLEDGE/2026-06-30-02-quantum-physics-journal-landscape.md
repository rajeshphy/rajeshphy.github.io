Choosing a journal is an act of **scientific classification**. Before looking at a metric, identify what the paper contributes, which community can evaluate it, what level of advance it claims, and what form of argument carries that claim. A short, field-changing quantum result, a rigorous spectral theorem, a long QFT calculation and a careful modified-gravity analysis do not have the same natural destination.

This module maps the main journal communities relevant to quantum and theoretical physics. It deliberately describes **scope, readership, editorial threshold and manuscript style**, not a frozen prestige ranking. Journal metrics, quartiles, fees, editorial policies and even scopes change. All external links here were checked on **30 June 2026**, but every submission requires a fresh live check.

<div class="note-box" markdown="1">
**The governing question is not:** “What is the highest-Q journal that might accept this?”

**Ask instead:** “What claim have we established, which physicists need it, what evidence will convince them, and which trustworthy journal gives the work the right test and readership?”
</div>

## 1. Why there is no universal list of top journals

“Top” can mean several different things:

- most selective across all physics;
- most influential inside one specialty;
- most mathematically rigorous;
- most widely read by a particular community;
- most appropriate for a letter, full article, methods paper or review;
- highest under one current citation indicator; or
- most valuable for the actual scientific conversation started by the paper.

Those meanings do not produce the same ordering. A journal with a lower broad citation metric may be the journal that every expert in a narrow subject reads. Citation rates also differ between quantum information, particle phenomenology, mathematical physics and gravitation. Review-heavy journals normally attract more citations than original-research journals. Older and larger journals accumulate a higher journal h-index more easily. None of these effects measures whether one manuscript is correct, original or well placed.

A better map uses two axes.

| Axis | Questions to ask |
|---|---|
| **Contribution level** | Is this a physics-wide landmark, an exceptional subfield advance, a substantial specialist result, a rigorous theorem or method, or a sound incremental result? |
| **Natural audience** | Is the paper primarily for quantum information, foundations, mathematical physics, AMO, HEP/QFT, gravity, cosmology, or a broad physics audience? |

Only after locating the paper on both axes should one compare journals. Even then, the “best” destination is the one whose editors, referees, authors and readers are equipped to recognize and use the result.

### Selectivity is not the same as quality

A broad landmark journal must reject many technically excellent papers because it promises unusually wide significance to its readers. A respected specialist journal may accept a sound and useful advance without requiring a change in the direction of all physics. Both can use rigorous peer review. The difference is mainly the **editorial significance threshold**, not a division between “good” and “bad” science.

### Scope fit is the first editorial gate

At desk review, an editor commonly asks:

1. Does the topic fall inside the journal's present scope?
2. Is the advance large enough for this journal's editorial promise?
3. Is the paper written for this readership?
4. Is the article type and length appropriate?
5. Is the claim supported strongly enough to justify external review?

A technically correct paper can fail questions 1–4. That is why an unsuitable submission does not become suitable merely because its equations are correct.

## 2. Learning from three representative journal types

**Annals of Physics**, **International Journal of Modern Physics A**, and **The European Physical Journal Plus** provide useful examples of different editorial homes. They should not be arranged as three rungs on one ladder.

### Annals of Physics

[Annals of Physics](https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/annals-of-physics) covers basic theoretical physics and is particularly natural for a substantial, fully developed treatment built from first principles. It gives room for a long conceptual or mathematical narrative, complete derivations and a result whose value lies in depth rather than letter-length urgency.

A paper placed here normally demonstrates a sustained theoretical argument. It is a sensible candidate when the work benefits from completeness and coherence. A mature 30-page derivation should not be compressed into a letter merely to pursue a more famous title.

### International Journal of Modern Physics A

[International Journal of Modern Physics A](https://www.worldscientific.com/worldscinet/ijmpa) serves particles and fields, with gravitation and cosmology also represented. It is an established specialist destination when the main readership is in QFT, particle theory, gravity or adjacent modern-physics communities.

A paper placed here normally addresses a specialist community. A later paper should go there because its subject and readers fit—not because an earlier group publication creates an automatic sequence.

### The European Physical Journal Plus

[The European Physical Journal Plus](https://link.springer.com/journal/13360/aims-and-scope) has a broad remit across fundamental, applied and interdisciplinary physics, including theoretical and computational work. It can suit a technically complete study whose interest spans more than one narrow specialty or whose principal value is a useful analysis, model, method or cross-field connection.

This venue can add breadth to a group's publication portfolio. It should not be described as interchangeable with a specialist HEP journal or a mathematical-physics journal.

### The productive next step

Build a **topic-dependent portfolio** rather than chasing a single upward line. For example:

- develop a quantum-information route through Physical Review A, Quantum, Quantum Science and Technology or more selective QIST venues when warranted;
- develop a mathematical-physics route through Journal of Mathematical Physics, Journal of Physics A, Letters in Mathematical Physics, Annales Henri Poincaré or Communications in Mathematical Physics according to rigor and threshold;
- develop a HEP/QFT route through Physical Review D, JHEP, European Physical Journal C, Nuclear Physics B or a concise-letter venue according to the claim; and
- develop a gravitation/cosmology route through Physical Review D, Classical and Quantum Gravity, JCAP, General Relativity and Gravitation or a mathematically oriented venue.

The goal is not to collect logos. It is to make the work visible in the communities that should test and extend it.

## 3. Landmark journals across physics

These are possible destinations only when the central result has unusually broad consequence. “Technically difficult” and “many pages of calculation” do not by themselves establish that threshold.

| Journal | Editorial promise and likely threshold | Manuscript style that fits |
|---|---|---|
| [Nature Physics](https://www.nature.com/nphys/aims) | Research of exceptional quality and significance across physics; the result must matter beyond a narrow specialist circle | A clean conceptual story, decisive evidence, restrained technical detail in the main narrative, and an explicit explanation of broad consequence |
| [Physical Review X](https://journals.aps.org/prx/about) | Highly selective, fully open-access research representing a major advance, breakthrough or new direction | A complete article with unusually strong claims, transparent validation, and a physics-wide or cross-subfield case for importance |
| [Physical Review Letters](https://journals.aps.org/prl/about) | A concise, influential result of broad interest or exceptional importance within a major field | One principal message, letter-length discipline, immediate motivation, decisive evidence and no dispensable derivation in the main text |
| [SciPost Physics](https://scipost.org/SciPostPhys/about) | Selective community-led venue for breakthrough or genuinely important advances across physics | A self-contained, technically transparent paper that makes the field-level reason for publication explicit; open peer-witnessed process should be understood before submission |

### Before claiming a landmark threshold

Require the authors to answer, without adjectives:

- What belief, capability, bound, classification or experimental/theoretical possibility changes because of this result?
- Which researchers outside the immediate niche will alter their work after reading it?
- What direct comparison shows that the advance is not merely another parameter choice or formal variation?
- Can the main result be stated in two sentences and defended with one decisive figure, theorem or calculation?

If those answers are weak, choose the strongest specialist journal that genuinely fits. A respected field journal is not a consolation prize.

## 4. Quantum information, quantum science and foundations

“Quantum physics” is too broad to be a journal category by itself. Separate quantum information and technology, AMO/quantum optics, many-body physics, and conceptual foundations.

| Journal | Community and threshold | Manuscript emphasis |
|---|---|---|
| [PRX Quantum](https://journals.aps.org/prxquantum/scope) | Highly selective QIST; milestone or exceptional multidisciplinary advance | Demonstrate why the result changes capability or understanding across a substantial part of quantum science, not only one implementation detail |
| [Quantum Science and Technology](https://publishingsupport.iopscience.iop.org/journals/quantum-science-technology/about-quantum-science-technology/) | Selective quantum computing, simulation, communication, sensing, thermodynamics and enabling science | Connect theory to a significant QST question; benchmark against leading methods and state operational implications |
| [npj Quantum Information](https://www.nature.com/npjqi/journal-information) | Multidisciplinary quantum information, communication and computation with substantial field interest | Make information-theoretic significance and accessibility across quantum subcommunities visible |
| [Physical Review A](https://journals.aps.org/pra/about) | Core venue for AMO, quantum science, foundations, quantum information, optics and related theory | A rigorous, complete subfield advance; write for specialists and explain the physical content of formal results |
| [Quantum](https://quantum-journal.org/about/) | Nonprofit open-access journal for rigorous, high-quality quantum science; closely connected to arXiv quant-ph | Transparent technical contribution, reproducibility where relevant, and clear placement relative to current quantum literature |
| [Foundations of Physics](https://link.springer.com/journal/10701/aims-and-scope) | Conceptual and foundational problems in modern physics, including measurement, quantum theory, spacetime and information | A precise physics problem and a defensible advance; avoid speculation unsupported by formal or empirical consequences |
| [Quantum Studies: Mathematics and Foundations](https://link.springer.com/journal/40509/aims-and-scope) | Interface of quantum theory, mathematical methods, foundations and philosophy of science | Explicit definitions, mathematical control and a clear account of what foundational problem is resolved or sharpened |

### A common mistake in foundations

A new interpretation, terminology or philosophical preference is not automatically a physics result. A credible foundations manuscript should identify the formal problem, distinguish itself from existing interpretations, and derive a new theorem, prediction, no-go result, operational distinction or conceptual resolution that experts can evaluate.

## 5. Mathematical and theoretical physics

In this area, “importance” and “rigor” must be separated. A beautiful proof of a very narrow lemma may be rigorous but below the editorial threshold of a leading mathematical-physics journal. Conversely, a physically important calculation may not satisfy a theorem-centred journal if definitions, hypotheses and proofs remain informal.

| Journal | Community and threshold | Manuscript emphasis |
|---|---|---|
| [Communications in Mathematical Physics](https://link.springer.com/journal/220/aims-and-scope) | Very high mathematical standard for problems motivated by modern physics; substantial conceptual or mathematical advance | State precise hypotheses and theorems, supply complete proofs, and demonstrate why the result matters to mathematical physics rather than pure formalism alone |
| [Annales Henri Poincaré](https://link.springer.com/journal/23/aims-and-scope) | High-standard analytical theoretical and mathematical physics, including QFT, GR, quantum information and quantum gravity | Combine mathematical depth with a clear physics problem; establish novelty against the closest rigorous literature |
| [Journal of Mathematical Physics](https://publishing.aip.org/publications/journals/special-topics/jmp/) | Broad mathematical physics and mathematical methods useful to theoretical physicists | Define the physical framework carefully, prove or derive the result fully, and keep the mathematics legible to physicists |
| [Journal of Physics A: Mathematical and Theoretical](https://publishingsupport.iopscience.iop.org/journals/journal-of-physics-a-mathematical-and-theoretical/about-journal-physics-mathematical-theoretical/) | Significant advances in mathematical and theoretical physics | Emphasize the new method, structure or theoretical insight and demonstrate it on a nontrivial physical problem |
| [Letters in Mathematical Physics](https://link.springer.com/journal/11005/aims-and-scope) | Important original developments communicated concisely | A focused result with complete logic; short format must not hide essential proof or assumptions |
| [Annals of Physics](https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/annals-of-physics) | Deep and fully developed basic theoretical physics | A coherent long-form treatment, first-principles development, complete derivations and clear physical interpretation |

### Choosing among them

- Choose a theorem-centred venue when the main contribution is a mathematically rigorous result with meaningful physical motivation.
- Choose Journal of Mathematical Physics or Journal of Physics A when a method, formalism or mathematical structure is the main contribution and the specialist audience is appropriate.
- Choose Annals of Physics when a full theoretical construction needs space and the value lies in physical depth and completeness rather than theorem status alone.
- Choose a letter only when the central result remains convincing without concealing indispensable proof.

## 6. High-energy physics, QFT and particle theory

HEP has strong preprint culture and unusually developed open-access infrastructure. Journal choice still matters for editorial certification, specialist readership and the form in which the work enters the record.

| Journal | Community and threshold | Manuscript emphasis |
|---|---|---|
| [Journal of High Energy Physics](https://jhep.sissa.it/jhep/help/JHEP/JHEP_about.jsp) | Major specialist venue for QFT, gauge theory, strings, gravity, phenomenology and mathematical methods | Establish a substantial HEP advance, compare directly with the closest arXiv literature, and make conventions and calculational checks reproducible |
| [Physical Review D](https://journals.aps.org/prd/about) | Core journal for particles, fields, gravity, cosmology and astrophysics | Complete specialist article with clear physical consequence, systematic comparison and appropriate phenomenological or theoretical validation |
| [European Physical Journal C](https://link.springer.com/journal/10052/aims-and-scope) | Experimental, theoretical and computational HEP, including QFT, cosmology, gravity and mathematical relativity | A solid, relevant field contribution with transparent methods and connection to the active HEP community |
| [Physics Letters B](https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/physics-letters-b) | Concise important results in particle and nuclear physics | One timely, well-supported result that benefits from rapid letter-length communication; do not fragment a full study artificially |
| [Nuclear Physics B](https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/nuclear-physics-b) | Full-length HEP, QFT, statistical systems and mathematical physics | Detailed development, nontrivial calculations and a complete account that exceeds a letter but remains sharply motivated |
| [International Journal of Modern Physics A](https://www.worldscientific.com/worldscinet/ijmpa) | Established specialist readership in particles, fields, gravitation and cosmology | Technically complete specialist work with its physical significance stated plainly and literature coverage kept current |

### A sensible HEP sequence

- A concise result with genuinely wide consequence may justify Physical Review Letters or Physics Letters B, depending on breadth and community.
- A substantial HEP/QFT article naturally belongs in JHEP, Physical Review D or European Physical Journal C when its threshold and audience fit.
- A detailed QFT or mathematical treatment may fit Nuclear Physics B, Annals of Physics, Journal of Physics A or Journal of Mathematical Physics better than a phenomenology-centred venue.
- A sound specialist article can fit International Journal of Modern Physics A without pretending to have a physics-wide claim.

There is no honest permanent rule that JHEP is “above” Physical Review D or vice versa. The correct comparison depends on subfield, paper type, reader community and current editorial practice.

## 7. Gravitation, cosmology and quantum gravity

Gravity papers often sit between relativity, cosmology, field theory, astrophysics and mathematical physics. The manuscript must declare which conversation it is entering.

| Journal | Community and threshold | Manuscript emphasis |
|---|---|---|
| [Physical Review D](https://journals.aps.org/prd/about) | Gravity and cosmology alongside particles and fields | A substantial physical result with clear tests, limiting cases and relation to current field questions |
| [Journal of Cosmology and Astroparticle Physics](https://jcap.sissa.it/jcap/help/helpLoader.jsp?pgType=about) | Cosmology and particle astrophysics, including dark matter, early universe, structure and gravitational physics | Connect theory to cosmological observables, data, falsifiable consequences or a major theoretical issue |
| [Classical and Quantum Gravity](https://publishingsupport.iopscience.iop.org/journals/classical-and-quantum-gravity/about-classical-quantum-gravity/) | Gravitational physics, spacetime, cosmology, mathematical relativity and quantum gravity | State the gravitational problem and invariant physical content; include analytical, numerical or observational checks appropriate to the claim |
| [General Relativity and Gravitation](https://link.springer.com/journal/10714/aims-and-scope) | Broad modern gravitation, GR, cosmology, gravitational waves and quantum gravity | Show physical or geometric significance; a new exact solution without meaningful interpretation is generally insufficient |
| [European Physical Journal C](https://link.springer.com/journal/10052/aims-and-scope) | Gravity and cosmology at the interface with HEP and field theory | Make the particle/field/cosmology connection central rather than incidental |
| [Annales Henri Poincaré](https://link.springer.com/journal/23/aims-and-scope) | Rigorous mathematical relativity, QFT and geometric analysis | Precise mathematical result with high analytical standard and strong physical motivation |

### Modified-gravity warning

A recurring weak pattern is: choose a new model, obtain a solution, plot parameters and declare viability. Before submission require:

- a nontrivial motivation beyond algebraic novelty;
- recovery of the appropriate established limit;
- stability, consistency and dimensional checks;
- comparison against existing constraints or observables;
- explanation of what is learned that was not known for closely related models; and
- honest treatment of degeneracies and limitations.

Without these, moving to a lower-ranked journal does not fix the scientific weakness.

## 8. Broad, reputable research venues

Broad journals can be appropriate when the paper crosses conventional categories or when a sound result has more than one natural audience. “Broad” does not mean “accepts anything.”

| Journal | Community and threshold | Manuscript emphasis |
|---|---|---|
| [Physical Review Research](https://journals.aps.org/prresearch/about) | Fully open-access research across physics, aligned with the quality standards of the established Physical Review family | A complete, technically sound and significant advance whose audience may cross conventional Physical Review boundaries |
| [New Journal of Physics](https://publishingsupport.iopscience.iop.org/journals/new-journal-of-physics/about-new-journal-physics/) | Fully open-access journal across all physics for significant results of broad interest | Explain both specialist contribution and why readers outside the immediate niche should care |
| [SciPost Physics Core](https://scipost.org/SciPostPhysCore/about) | Community-led, diamond open-access venue for significant research across physics | Self-contained, transparent work meeting field standards; understand its public editorial process before choosing it |
| [The European Physical Journal Plus](https://link.springer.com/journal/13360/aims-and-scope) | Broad fundamental, applied and interdisciplinary physics | A thorough theoretical, computational or cross-field contribution whose value and audience are explicitly identified |
| [Annals of Physics](https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/annals-of-physics) | Broad basic theoretical physics with depth | A mature first-principles article whose full development is part of the contribution |

Use a broad journal because the paper genuinely crosses or serves communities—not because its specialist home was not investigated.

## 9. Review-led venues are a different publication route

Review journals often have very high citation indicators because authoritative syntheses are cited frequently. That does not make them ordinary targets for an original paper.

| Venue | Normal route |
|---|---|
| [Reviews of Modern Physics](https://journals.aps.org/rmp/authors) | Most reviews are invited or begin with a detailed proposal. The work must critically synthesize a mature field for a broad physics audience. |
| [Reports on Progress in Physics](https://publishingsupport.iopscience.iop.org/journals/reports-on-progress-in-physics/about-reports-progress-physics/) | Authoritative, accessible reviews of major progress; inspect the current article types and proposal route before preparing a manuscript. |
| [Physics Reports](https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/physics-reports) | Long, authoritative reviews designed to organize and evaluate a substantial field rather than report one new calculation. |

A literature-heavy manuscript is not automatically a review. A serious review needs a transparent scope, critical synthesis, competing viewpoints, methodological judgment, an organizing framework and a clear statement of open problems. Contact or propose to the journal before investing months in a review-led submission when its policy calls for that route.

## 10. The reach–target–fallback method

Prepare three defensible options **before** the first submission.

### Reach

The paper fits the scope, but acceptance requires the strongest reasonable interpretation of its significance. The reach choice must still be plausible. A physics-wide journal is not a lottery ticket for a narrow parameter study.

### Target

This is the best balance of audience, threshold, article style, trust and logistics. If the manuscript is as strong as the group believes, it should receive a fair hearing here. The target is usually the most important choice.

### Fallback

This is a reputable journal whose readers still need the result and whose editorial threshold the manuscript clearly satisfies. It is not a predatory shortcut or a random lower-metric title. If the paper is rejected for a correctable scientific reason, revise before using the fallback.

### Example shortlist logic

| Paper | Reach | Target | Fallback |
|---|---|---|---|
| Major quantum-error-correction theorem with operational gain | PRX Quantum | Quantum Science and Technology or Quantum | Physical Review A |
| Rigorous spectral result with strong physical implication | Communications in Mathematical Physics | Annales Henri Poincaré or Journal of Mathematical Physics | Journal of Physics A, depending on emphasis |
| Full QFT calculation of clear HEP relevance | Physical Review Letters only if concise and broadly consequential | JHEP or Physical Review D | European Physical Journal C, Nuclear Physics B or IJMPA according to style |
| New gravity result tied to cosmological observables | Physical Review Letters only at landmark threshold | Physical Review D or JCAP | Classical and Quantum Gravity, EPJ C or General Relativity and Gravitation according to audience |

The names can move between columns after the evidence audit. The method is stable; the classification is manuscript-specific.

## 11. The 20-paper fit audit

Never select a journal from its homepage sentence alone. Audit recent practice.

### Step 1: collect the closest literature

Find approximately 20 papers from the previous two years that are closest in **problem, method and contribution level**, not merely papers containing the same keyword. Use references in your manuscript, INSPIRE-HEP, NASA ADS, Web of Science, Scopus, arXiv and journal searches as appropriate.

### Step 2: record where the conversation occurs

For each paper record:

| Field | What to capture |
|---|---|
| Bibliographic identity | Title, year, DOI or arXiv identifier |
| Scientific similarity | Same problem, method, dataset/model, or consequence |
| Contribution level | Landmark, major subfield advance, specialist advance, incremental result, review |
| Journal and article type | Letter, regular article, rapid communication, review, methods paper |
| Evidence style | Theorem, numerical benchmark, experimental validation, phenomenological constraint, conceptual argument |
| Relationship | Does your manuscript extend, contradict, unify, sharpen or merely repeat it? |

### Step 3: inspect candidate journals directly

For each candidate, read at least 10 recent relevant abstracts and several complete papers. Ask:

- Does the journal actually publish this topic now, not only historically?
- What claim size survives desk review?
- How much mathematical or computational detail appears in the main article?
- Are papers written for specialists or a broad audience?
- Is the result normally a letter or a full article?
- Are the editors and likely referees part of the intended community?
- Does your bibliography already participate in this journal's conversation?

### Step 4: write a one-sentence fit statement

> This journal's readers need our result because ________, and recent articles A, B and C show that it publishes this problem at this level of advance.

If the sentence requires vague phrases such as “highly reputed” or “high impact,” the scientific fit has not been demonstrated.

## 12. A weighted journal-selection matrix

The matrix disciplines judgment; it does not replace judgment. First apply non-negotiable gates, then score the surviving journals from 1 (poor) to 5 (excellent).

### Non-negotiable gates

A journal is removed from consideration if any of these fails:

- authentic identity and trustworthy editorial process;
- scope includes the manuscript;
- no simultaneous-submission conflict;
- acceptable publication ethics, licence and repository policy;
- a feasible cost route or confirmed waiver; and
- compliance with current university and funder requirements.

### Recommended weights

| Criterion | Weight | Evidence required |
|---|---:|---|
| Exact scientific scope fit | 25 | Official scope plus recent comparable papers |
| Intended readership and community visibility | 20 | Citation network, authors, editors and field databases |
| Editorial threshold fit | 15 | Recent accepted papers at a comparable contribution level |
| Article type and manuscript-style fit | 10 | Current author guide and examples |
| Editorial trust and quality of review | 10 | Publisher/society identity, policies, group experience and transparent history |
| Indexing and discoverability | 5 | Live Web of Science, Scopus, INSPIRE-HEP, ADS or DOAJ verification as relevant |
| Open-access and licence fit | 5 | Official journal policy and funder requirement |
| Total author cost | 5 | Official fee page, waiver and institutional agreement |
| Practical timing and workflow | 5 | Current process information and realistic—not guaranteed—group experience |
| **Total** | **100** | |

For each criterion calculate:

**weighted contribution = score ÷ 5 × weight**

The maximum total is 100. Do not let a high metric compensate for a scope or trust failure. Scores within a few points should be treated as effectively tied; use audience and editorial threshold to decide.

## 13. Open access, APCs and SCOAP³

Open access is an access and financing model, not a quality grade.

| Model | Reader pays? | Author necessarily pays? | Typical situation |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| Subscription | Usually reader or library | Often no mandatory publication fee | Article remains behind access control unless a repository route applies |
| Hybrid | Subscription by default | Only if optional gold OA is chosen, subject to other charges | One journal contains both subscription and OA articles |
| Gold OA | No | Often an APC, waiver, agreement or sponsor | Version of record is openly available |
| Diamond/sponsored OA | No | No author APC | Society, consortium, institution or sponsor funds publication |
| Green OA | Repository copy is free | Usually no OA APC | Author deposits an allowed manuscript version under the journal's policy |

### HEP and SCOAP³

[SCOAP³](https://scoap3.org/faqs/) is a consortium that centrally funds eligible high-energy-physics open access. It supports full-journal open access for titles including JHEP, European Physical Journal C, Physics Letters B and Nuclear Physics B, and supports eligible HEP content in participating hybrid journals. [APS explains its SCOAP³ eligibility route](https://journals.aps.org/authors/scoap3), including the importance of an eligible primary arXiv HEP category for covered APS articles.

Before assuming that publication is free:

1. check the current journal and SCOAP³ participation page;
2. confirm that the article and primary arXiv category are eligible;
3. check whether page, colour, overlength or other charges exist separately;
4. verify whether the institution has a transformative or read-and-publish agreement; and
5. save the dated policy evidence with the submission record.

Some excellent journals are diamond OA, such as SciPost venues; others charge APCs; many hybrid journals permit ordinary subscription publication without a mandatory OA fee. APC size, or the absence of an APC, does not establish editorial quality.

### Preprints

Physics preprints are valuable for rapid dissemination, priority and community scrutiny, but they are not journal certification. Check the target's current preprint and licence policy. Keep the preprint, accepted manuscript and version of record clearly labelled and linked.

## 14. When not to submit yet

Delay submission when any of the following is true.

### The science is not ready

- The main claim changes when reasonable parameters, initial conditions or conventions change.
- Essential limiting cases, stability checks, dimensional checks or error estimates are missing.
- The closest competing method or paper has not been compared.
- Numerical code, symbolic algebra or data cannot be reproduced inside the group.
- A formal novelty has no identified physical consequence.
- The abstract claims more than the results demonstrate.

### The manuscript is not ready

- Notation changes across sections or important assumptions are hidden.
- Figures cannot be understood without reconstructing the analysis.
- The literature review omits obvious recent work or contains unverified references.
- The central result cannot be stated clearly by every author.
- Language editing would change the scientific meaning because the argument itself is unclear.

### The authorship and ethics record is not ready

- Authors disagree about order, contribution or accountability.
- Related manuscripts, conference papers or preprints have not been disclosed.
- Permissions, data rights, conflicts of interest or AI-use declarations are unresolved.
- The manuscript is active at another journal.

### The journal has not been verified

- Selection is based on an unsolicited email, an invented metric or a logo on the journal website.
- Exact ISSN, publisher, indexing coverage and submission domain do not match.
- Fees or waiver terms are hidden until after acceptance.
- No recent comparable papers can be found.
- The only reason for choosing it is a claimed quartile.

Stopping for one week of internal audit is cheaper than months lost to desk rejection, a weak review process or an integrity problem.

## 15. Worked manuscript-to-journal examples

These examples demonstrate reasoning, not guaranteed outcomes.

### Example A: a new quantum-error-correction theorem

**Manuscript:** A theorem proves a tighter threshold for a physically motivated noise model, accompanied by simulations showing a practical decoding advantage.

**Audit:** The closest 20 papers cluster in quantum information journals; the result has operational meaning and a reproducible benchmark. It is important within QIST but not yet shown to change all quantum architectures.

**Decision:**

- **Reach:** PRX Quantum, only if the improvement is exceptional and broadly consequential.
- **Target:** Quantum Science and Technology, npj Quantum Information or Quantum depending breadth, access model and community.
- **Fallback:** Physical Review A for a complete, rigorous quantum-science contribution.

**Writing change:** Lead with the capability gained, then state the theorem and benchmark. Do not lead with pages of formalism before explaining the operational consequence.

### Example B: a rigorous spectral theorem for an open quantum system

**Manuscript:** A complete theorem characterizes the spectrum of a non-self-adjoint generator under physically meaningful assumptions and resolves an open mathematical question.

**Audit:** The novelty is mathematical, the proof is complete, and the physical model is central rather than decorative.

**Decision:**

- **Reach:** Communications in Mathematical Physics if the result changes understanding across mathematical physics.
- **Target:** Annales Henri Poincaré or Journal of Mathematical Physics, depending depth and audience.
- **Fallback:** Journal of Physics A if the method and physical application fit its recent practice.

**Writing change:** Put definitions, assumptions and the main theorem early. Separate proof from physical interpretation, and compare precisely with the nearest rigorous results.

### Example C: a long QFT calculation

**Manuscript:** A 35-page calculation derives higher-order corrections, checks symmetries and limiting cases, and changes a phenomenological prediction measurably.

**Audit:** The paper is substantial and complete, but its main audience is HEP/QFT specialists. Compressing it would remove essential checks.

**Decision:**

- **Reach:** A broad letter only if one conclusion can stand alone and has truly broad consequence; otherwise do not force it.
- **Target:** JHEP or Physical Review D.
- **Fallback:** European Physical Journal C, Nuclear Physics B or International Journal of Modern Physics A according to the precise topic and article style.

**Writing change:** Give the physical consequence before the technical development, maintain a reproducible convention table, and include independent analytical or numerical checks.

### Example D: a modified-gravity cosmology paper

**Manuscript:** A modified-gravity model produces a late-time expansion history consistent with one observational dataset but has not been tested for perturbative stability.

**Audit:** The result is not ready. Agreement at background level does not establish viability; closely related models already reproduce the same history.

**Decision:** Do not submit. First test ghosts/gradients or the appropriate stability criteria, recover the accepted limit, compare models statistically, and state what distinctive observable separates this model.

After strengthening:

- **Target:** JCAP or Physical Review D if cosmological consequences become substantial.
- **Alternative target:** Classical and Quantum Gravity or European Physical Journal C if the gravitational or field-theory aspect dominates.
- **Specialist fallback:** General Relativity and Gravitation if the physical/geometric significance meets its scope.

This example shows that a fallback journal is not a substitute for missing science.

### Example E: a quantum-foundations proposal

**Manuscript:** A new interpretation of measurement is proposed, but it reproduces existing predictions and contains no theorem, operational distinction or resolution of a specific inconsistency.

**Audit:** The work presently offers terminology rather than a testable or formally decisive advance.

**Decision:** Do not submit as a major physics result. Identify the exact foundational problem and derive a no-go theorem, operational consequence, reconstruction result or formal clarification. Then consider Physical Review A, Foundations of Physics or Quantum Studies: Mathematics and Foundations according to the achieved contribution.

**Writing change:** Replace declarations of philosophical superiority with explicit definitions, comparisons and consequences.

### Example F: a genuinely cross-field theoretical method

**Manuscript:** A numerical tensor-network method solves a class of quantum many-body problems and is independently demonstrated in quantum simulation and statistical physics.

**Audit:** The technique serves multiple communities, benchmarks exceed existing methods, and code plus convergence tests are available.

**Decision:**

- **Reach:** Physical Review X if the method opens a major new capability across fields.
- **Target:** Physical Review Research, New Journal of Physics or SciPost Physics/Core depending significance threshold and editorial model.
- **Specialist alternative:** Physical Review A or Journal of Physics A if one community is clearly dominant.

**Writing change:** Use the cross-field demonstrations to prove breadth; do not merely claim that the method is “general.”

### Example G: an invited or proposed review

**Manuscript idea:** A synthesis of ten years of quantum-information approaches to black-hole information, with competing frameworks and open tests.

**Audit:** The literature is mature enough, the authors have relevant expertise, and the proposed review would organize rather than merely list the field.

**Decision:** Read the proposal instructions for Reviews of Modern Physics, Reports on Progress in Physics or Physics Reports and contact the appropriate editorial route before writing a full review. If the intended paper is instead a new original calculation with an extended introduction, submit it to an original-research journal.

## 16. Verify quartiles and metrics live

No quartile or impact-factor number in a static post should be treated as current. Quartiles can change annually; journals may occupy several categories; JCR, CiteScore and SJR use different datasets and methods. A journal can therefore be Q1 under one system or category and Q2 under another without contradiction.

### JCR/Web of Science workflow

1. Search the exact journal title or ISSN in the [Web of Science Master Journal List](https://mjl.clarivate.com/home) and confirm active coverage.
2. Open the licensed Journal Citation Reports record.
3. Select the intended **data year** rather than assuming the newest screen represents the paper's publication year.
4. Record every relevant category separately.
5. Record Journal Impact Factor, category rank, number of journals in the category, percentile and JIF quartile.
6. Save the access date and, where permitted, a screenshot or exported record.

### Scopus/CiteScore workflow

1. Search the exact title or ISSN in [Scopus Sources](https://www.scopus.com/sources).
2. Confirm active coverage years and subject categories.
3. Record the CiteScore **year**, score, rank, percentile and quartile for the relevant category.
4. Keep CiteScore quartile separate from JCR JIF quartile and from SCImago SJR quartile.

### Policy and fee workflow

1. Navigate to the journal through the verified publisher or society domain.
2. Read the current aims and scope, author guide, peer-review model, article types and preprint policy.
3. Check the official OA/APC page, waiver rules and institutional agreement tool.
4. For HEP, confirm current SCOAP³ eligibility rather than assuming it from an old paper.
5. Date every observation.

## 17. A supervisor's final pre-submission conversation

Ask the scholar to answer these questions aloud:

1. What exactly is new?
2. What is the strongest evidence for the claim?
3. Which result would fail first if an assumption is wrong?
4. Who are the five researchers or groups most likely to use or challenge this work?
5. Which recent papers make this journal a natural home?
6. What editorial threshold does the journal promise, and have we met it?
7. Why is our target stronger than our fallback and more realistic than our reach?
8. What will we revise if a desk rejection says the significance is too narrow?
9. Are scope, indexing, policy, fees and access route verified from live official sources?
10. Can every author defend the entire paper and take responsibility for their contribution?

If the discussion produces evidence rather than prestige slogans, the group is ready to submit intelligently.
