The journal landscape in quantum and theoretical physics is not a single ladder. It is a network of partially overlapping communities, each with its own central questions, article forms, standards of evidence and citation culture. A short result with physics-wide consequences, a rigorous theorem, a long quantum-field-theory calculation and a cosmological parameter analysis may all be excellent research, yet they belong to different parts of that network.

This chapter is an **awareness map**, not a journal-ranking or submission guide. It describes what major journal families publish, who reads them, how their editorial thresholds differ, and why their apparent positions change across databases and years. Scope statements and policy links were checked on **30 June 2026**. Journal scopes, article types, access arrangements, fees, indexing and metrics remain time-dependent facts.

<div class="note-box" markdown="1">
**Central distinction:** journal standing has several dimensions—breadth, field authority, selectivity, mathematical rigor, readership, article type, editorial trust and citation position. No one number combines them into a universal measure of quality.
</div>

## 1. What “top journal” can mean

The phrase “top journal” is common but incomplete. In physics it can refer to any of the following:

| Meaning of “top” | What it describes | Why it produces a different list |
|---|---|---|
| **Broad prestige** | Recognition across many areas of physics | Physics-wide journals require significance beyond a narrow specialty |
| **Field authority** | Centrality to one research community | A specialist journal may be read more closely by the relevant experts than a broader title |
| **Selectivity** | A high editorial threshold and low acceptance proportion | Selectivity reflects the journal's editorial promise, capacity and breadth requirements, not merely correctness |
| **Mathematical rigor** | Depth of formal definitions, hypotheses and proofs | The most rigorous mathematical-physics venues are not necessarily the most cited across physics |
| **Citation position** | Rank under a named metric, category and year | Database coverage, citation window and subject classification alter the result |
| **Article-type authority** | Leadership in letters, full articles or reviews | Review journals and rapid-letter journals serve fundamentally different purposes |
| **Community influence** | Ability to shape the literature actually used by a subfield | A lower-volume specialist venue can be decisive inside a small community |

These meanings overlap but are not interchangeable. **Physical Review Letters** and **Physical Review X** represent broad significance and high selectivity. **Physical Review A** is a core field journal for atomic, molecular, optical and quantum science. **Communications in Mathematical Physics** represents a particularly high standard of mathematical physics. **JHEP** and **Physical Review D** are central to high-energy theory. **Reviews of Modern Physics** is associated with authoritative synthesis rather than routine original articles.

Consequently, statements such as “Journal A is above Journal B” are usually meaningful only after specifying a subfield, article type, time period and criterion.

### Selectivity and scientific quality

Selectivity describes an editorial filter. Broad selective journals reject many sound papers because the results are too specialized for the readership promised by the journal. Specialist journals can apply rigorous peer review while accepting substantial work that matters mainly to one community. A difference in editorial threshold is therefore not a simple division between good and bad research.

Editorial thresholds commonly range from:

- **physics-wide landmark significance**, where the result is expected to influence several communities;
- **exceptional subfield significance**, where the result changes an important direction within a major field;
- **substantial specialist advance**, where experts gain a new theorem, method, prediction or explanation;
- **sound field contribution**, where the result is original, technically reliable and useful without being transformative; to
- **review or synthesis authority**, where value comes from organizing and critically evaluating an established body of knowledge.

The threshold also interacts with article form. A letter demands one concentrated message. A full article supports a more complete derivation. A mathematical paper may be organized around formal results and proofs. A review is judged by coverage, critical synthesis and authority rather than by one new calculation.

## 2. Journal families and article forms

Quantum and theoretical physics journals can be viewed as several broad families.

| Journal family | Typical readership | Characteristic editorial identity |
|---|---|---|
| **Broad selective physics** | Physicists across multiple fields | Results with unusually wide significance or long-term influence |
| **Core subfield journals** | Specialists in quantum science, AMO, HEP, gravity or cosmology | Complete and important advances judged mainly within a defined community |
| **Mathematical-physics journals** | Mathematical physicists and theory-oriented mathematicians | Formal precision, analytical depth and connection between mathematics and physical problems |
| **Broad research journals** | Several physics communities | Significant, technically sound work that crosses or does not fit one narrow specialty |
| **Letters journals** | Readers seeking concise, timely advances | One main result presented in a compressed form |
| **Review-led journals** | Specialists and non-specialists entering or reassessing a field | Critical synthesis, mature perspective and organized access to a large literature |

### Common article forms

**Letters** center on one concise result; **regular articles** support complete derivations and validation; **long-form theory articles** give extended development an important role; **mathematical communications** may be organized around definitions and proofs; and **reviews, reports and perspectives** synthesize a literature. These forms have different standards and citation patterns.

## 3. Three illustrative journals with different identities

**Annals of Physics**, **International Journal of Modern Physics A**, and **The European Physical Journal Plus** illustrate why journals cannot be understood as positions on one ladder.

| Feature | Annals of Physics | International Journal of Modern Physics A | The European Physical Journal Plus |
|---|---|---|---|
| Official scope | [Basic theoretical physics](https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/annals-of-physics) | [Particles and fields, with gravitation and cosmology](https://www.worldscientific.com/worldscinet/ijmpa) | [Broad fundamental, applied and interdisciplinary physics](https://link.springer.com/journal/13360/aims-and-scope) |
| Principal readership | Theoretical physicists across several foundational areas | Particle, field-theory, gravitation and cosmology specialists | A broad physics audience, including theoretical and computational communities |
| Characteristic article | Fully developed theoretical treatment, often with long derivations | Specialist modern-physics research article | Complete theoretical, computational, applied or interdisciplinary study |
| Editorial emphasis | Depth, coherence and first-principles development | Relevance to the particles-and-fields community and adjacent areas | Broad scope, technical completeness and cross-field or general physics interest |
| Access structure | Hybrid/subscription and open-access routes | Publisher access options under current World Scientific policy | Hybrid journal under current Springer policy |

Annals of Physics is associated with a sustained theoretical narrative; International Journal of Modern Physics A with a concentrated particles-and-fields conversation; and EPJ Plus with a wider fundamental, applied and interdisciplinary portfolio. They therefore represent **depth-oriented theoretical visibility**, **specialist field visibility**, and **broad physics visibility** rather than successive prestige levels.

## 4. Broad, highly selective physics journals

Broad selective journals publish a small subset of results considered important beyond the immediate niche. Their editorial assessment includes accessibility and significance for a wide readership as well as technical validity.

| Journal | Scope and readership | Selectivity and article character | Access model in June 2026 |
|---|---|---|---|
| [Nature Physics](https://www.nature.com/nphys/aims) | High-significance research across physics, including quantum physics, high-energy physics and cosmology | Strong editorial selection for results of exceptional quality and broad consequence; concise conceptual presentation | Hybrid |
| [Physical Review X](https://journals.aps.org/prx/about) | Multidisciplinary physics readership | Highly selective complete articles reporting a major advance, breakthrough or new direction | Fully open access |
| [Physical Review Letters](https://journals.aps.org/prl/about) | Broad physics audience organized around short communications | Concise influential results of broad interest or exceptional importance within a major field | Hybrid, with optional OA routes |
| [SciPost Physics](https://scipost.org/SciPostPhys/about) | Whole-physics community under a scholar-led editorial system | Selective research meeting stated breakthrough or major-advance expectations; open peer-witnessed process | Diamond open access |

The phrase “broad interest” does not mean that every physicist must use the result. It indicates that the conceptual consequence can be understood beyond the narrow group that performed the calculation. In these journals, an advance confined to another parameter set, a routine extension or an isolated formal variation generally lies below the stated editorial identity even when technically correct.

## 5. Quantum information, quantum science and foundations

Quantum science now contains several partially distinct communities: quantum computation and information, communication, sensing, simulation, thermodynamics, control, AMO and quantum optics, many-body physics, and conceptual foundations. Journals differ in how much of this spectrum they cover.

| Journal | Main community | Characteristic threshold and article style | Access model in June 2026 |
|---|---|---|---|
| [PRX Quantum](https://journals.aps.org/prxquantum/scope) | Multidisciplinary quantum information science and technology | Highly selective milestone or exceptional QIST advance with significance across a substantial part of the field | Fully open access |
| [Quantum Science and Technology](https://publishingsupport.iopscience.iop.org/journals/quantum-science-technology/about-quantum-science-technology/) | Quantum computing, simulation, communication, sensing, metrology and enabling science | Selective work with clear importance to QST and interest beyond one implementation detail | Hybrid |
| [npj Quantum Information](https://www.nature.com/npjqi/journal-information) | Quantum information, computation and communication | Multidisciplinary research with substantial information-science relevance | Fully open access |
| [Physical Review A](https://journals.aps.org/pra/about) | AMO physics, quantum science, foundations, quantum information and optics | Core specialist articles reporting complete and significant subfield advances | Hybrid |
| [Quantum](https://quantum-journal.org/about/) | Quantum science, especially the arXiv quant-ph community | Rigorous, high-quality theoretical and experimental work under a nonprofit community model | Open access with a publication-fee and waiver structure |
| [Foundations of Physics](https://link.springer.com/journal/10701/aims-and-scope) | Conceptual and foundational questions in modern physics | Formal, conceptual or interpretive work tied to a precise physics problem | Hybrid |
| [Quantum Studies: Mathematics and Foundations](https://link.springer.com/journal/40509/aims-and-scope) | Quantum theory at the interface of mathematics, foundations and philosophy of science | Explicit mathematical or conceptual analysis of foundational questions | Hybrid |

**Physical Review A** is an important example of field authority not captured by a simple broad-journal comparison. Its readership and editorial expertise are deeply embedded in AMO and quantum science. **PRX Quantum**, **Quantum Science and Technology** and **npj Quantum Information** emphasize QIST across theoretical, experimental and technological boundaries. **Quantum** adds a nonprofit community-led model linked closely to the quant-ph preprint culture.

Foundations journals occupy another intellectual space. Their subject matter includes measurement, interpretations, reconstruction of quantum theory, no-go results, quantum logic and the relation between formalism and physical meaning. Conceptual novelty in this literature is normally distinguished from a new name for an existing interpretation by formal consequences, operational distinctions, theorems or a demonstrable resolution of a recognized problem.

## 6. Mathematical and theoretical physics

Mathematical physics contains a spectrum from theorem-centered work to physically motivated formal methods and long theoretical constructions. Mathematical rigor is necessary in some journals but is not the only criterion: the result must also have sufficient conceptual reach or physical relevance for that journal's readership.

| Journal | Main identity | Characteristic threshold and article style |
|---|---|---|
| [Communications in Mathematical Physics](https://link.springer.com/journal/220/aims-and-scope) | Mathematical physics at very high analytical standards | Major results motivated by modern physics, with precise hypotheses, theorems and complete proofs |
| [Annales Henri Poincaré](https://link.springer.com/journal/23/aims-and-scope) | Analytical theoretical and mathematical physics | High-standard work in areas including QFT, GR, geometric analysis, quantum information and quantum gravity |
| [Journal of Mathematical Physics](https://publishing.aip.org/publications/journals/special-topics/jmp/) | Broad mathematical methods and structures in physics | Formal developments presented in a way accessible to theoretical physicists |
| [Journal of Physics A: Mathematical and Theoretical](https://publishingsupport.iopscience.iop.org/journals/journal-of-physics-a-mathematical-and-theoretical/about-journal-physics-mathematical-theoretical/) | Mathematical and theoretical physics across multiple specialties | Significant methods, structures and theoretical insights, often demonstrated on nontrivial physical problems |
| [Letters in Mathematical Physics](https://link.springer.com/journal/11005/aims-and-scope) | Concise original developments in mathematical physics | Focused formal results communicated in a shorter format without abandoning logical completeness |
| [Annals of Physics](https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/annals-of-physics) | Deep basic theoretical physics | Coherent long-form development whose derivations and physical interpretation form part of the contribution |

The distinction between **Communications in Mathematical Physics** and **Journal of Mathematical Physics** is not merely a citation rank. The former is associated with an especially high threshold for mathematical-physics significance and proof. The latter covers a wider range of mathematical methods and formulations valuable to theoretical physicists. **Journal of Physics A** combines mathematical structures with broad theoretical-physics problems, while **Annales Henri Poincaré** has a strong analytical and geometrical identity. **Annals of Physics** is less defined by theorem form and more by a fully developed theoretical account.

## 7. High-energy physics, quantum field theory and particle theory

High-energy physics has a strong arXiv culture and relies heavily on field databases such as INSPIRE-HEP. Preprints provide rapid public circulation, while journals provide editorial selection, peer-review certification and a stable version of record.

| Journal | Main community | Characteristic threshold and article style | Access setting in June 2026 |
|---|---|---|---|
| [Journal of High Energy Physics](https://jhep.sissa.it/jhep/help/JHEP/JHEP_about.jsp) | QFT, gauge theory, strings, gravity, phenomenology and mathematical methods | Major specialist venue for substantial full research articles | Gold OA through SCOAP³ |
| [Physical Review D](https://journals.aps.org/prd/about) | Particles, fields, gravitation, cosmology and astrophysics | Core field journal for complete specialist advances, from formal theory to phenomenology | Hybrid; eligible HEP content may be covered through SCOAP³ |
| [European Physical Journal C](https://link.springer.com/journal/10052/aims-and-scope) | Experimental, theoretical and computational HEP, QFT, cosmology and gravity | Broad HEP field journal for technically complete research | Sponsored OA through SCOAP³ |
| [Physics Letters B](https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/physics-letters-b) | Particle and nuclear physics | Concise, timely and important original results in letter form | OA through SCOAP³ |
| [Nuclear Physics B](https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/nuclear-physics-b) | HEP, QFT, mathematical physics and statistical systems | Full-length theoretical work with substantial calculations and development | OA through SCOAP³ |
| [International Journal of Modern Physics A](https://www.worldscientific.com/worldscinet/ijmpa) | Particles, fields, gravitation and cosmology | Established specialist articles and related modern-physics content | Publisher subscription/hybrid options |

**JHEP**, **Physical Review D** and **European Physical Journal C** overlap substantially, but their author communities, editorial histories and distributions across formal theory, phenomenology, experiment and gravity differ. **Physics Letters B** is distinguished by concise communication. **Nuclear Physics B** supports a fuller theoretical development and also reaches mathematical-physics and statistical-system topics. **International Journal of Modern Physics A** serves a broad established community within particles, fields and adjacent gravitation/cosmology.

There is no permanent context-free ordering of these titles. Citation position, category assignment, regional and subfield publication patterns, article volume and access arrangements all contribute to different apparent rankings.

## 8. Gravitation, cosmology and quantum gravity

Gravity research crosses several disciplinary boundaries. A paper on mathematical relativity, cosmological inference, gravitational waves, QFT in curved spacetime and quantum gravity may share vocabulary while addressing different expert communities.

| Journal | Main community | Characteristic threshold and article style |
|---|---|---|
| [Physical Review D](https://journals.aps.org/prd/about) | Gravity and cosmology in a particles-and-fields setting | Substantial theoretical, phenomenological, numerical or observationally connected research |
| [Journal of Cosmology and Astroparticle Physics](https://jcap.sissa.it/jcap/help/helpLoader.jsp?pgType=about) | Cosmology and particle astrophysics | Early-universe theory, dark matter, cosmic structure, neutrinos, gravitational physics and links to observables |
| [Classical and Quantum Gravity](https://publishingsupport.iopscience.iop.org/journals/classical-and-quantum-gravity/about-classical-quantum-gravity/) | Gravitational physics, spacetime and quantum gravity | Broad theoretical, mathematical, computational and observational work centered on gravitation |
| [General Relativity and Gravitation](https://link.springer.com/journal/10714/aims-and-scope) | General relativity and modern gravitation | GR, cosmology, gravitational waves, QFT in curved spacetime and quantum gravity; physical or geometric significance is central |
| [European Physical Journal C](https://link.springer.com/journal/10052/aims-and-scope) | Gravity and cosmology at the HEP/QFT interface | Field-theory, particle and cosmological connections |
| [Annales Henri Poincaré](https://link.springer.com/journal/23/aims-and-scope) | Rigorous mathematical relativity and geometric analysis | High-level analytical results with mathematical and physical significance |

The same broad topic therefore appears in different forms. JCAP is strongly associated with cosmology and astroparticle questions. Classical and Quantum Gravity centers the gravitational problem itself. Physical Review D integrates gravity with particles, fields and cosmology. General Relativity and Gravitation has a specialist relativity identity. Annales Henri Poincaré represents the rigorous mathematical end of the landscape.

## 9. Broad, reputable research journals

Broad research journals occupy the space between highly selective physics-wide titles and narrow specialty journals. Their breadth can include interdisciplinary work, methods crossing subfields and sound research without a single obvious specialist home.

| Journal | Scope and identity | Article character | Access model in June 2026 |
|---|---|---|---|
| [Physical Review Research](https://journals.aps.org/prresearch/about) | Research across all physics within the Physical Review family | Significant complete articles across established and emerging areas | Fully open access |
| [New Journal of Physics](https://publishingsupport.iopscience.iop.org/journals/new-journal-of-physics/about-new-journal-physics/) | Whole-physics journal published by IOP Publishing and the German Physical Society | Significant results with interest extending beyond a very narrow niche | Fully open access |
| [SciPost Physics Core](https://scipost.org/SciPostPhysCore/about) | Community-led research across physics | Significant field research under a public editorial model | Diamond open access |
| [The European Physical Journal Plus](https://link.springer.com/journal/13360/aims-and-scope) | Fundamental, applied and interdisciplinary physics | Theoretical, computational, experimental and cross-field studies | Hybrid |
| [Annals of Physics](https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/annals-of-physics) | Basic theoretical physics across subject boundaries | Mature, often long-form theoretical articles | Hybrid |

“Broad” is not synonymous with indiscriminate. Each title retains a stated threshold, article culture and readership. Physical Review Research and New Journal of Physics are fully open-access broad venues under society-linked publishing structures. SciPost Physics Core uses a nonprofit community model. EPJ Plus spans a wide range of physics. Annals of Physics is broad within **theoretical** physics rather than across all experimental and applied physics.

## 10. Review journals and review-led titles

Review literature is a separate part of the journal system. Reviews are cited frequently because they organize a field, explain terminology, compare approaches and provide an entry point to the primary literature. Their citation patterns help explain why review journals often have high impact factors.

| Venue | Editorial identity in June 2026 | Relationship to original research |
|---|---|---|
| [Reviews of Modern Physics](https://journals.aps.org/rmp/authors) | Authoritative critical reviews for a broad physics readership; most articles are invited or originate in a formal proposal | Not a routine destination for a standard original research article |
| [Physics Reports](https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/physics-reports) | Extensive specialist reviews, with enough introduction for non-specialists; regular and Rapid Review formats | Review-only identity; articles are invitation-based, with proposals considered by the editorial office |
| [Reports on Progress in Physics](https://publishingsupport.iopscience.iop.org/journals/reports-on-progress-in-physics/about-reports-progress-physics/) | Highly selective journal publishing authoritative invited reviews and, under its expanded current scope, ground-breaking original research | Unlike a purely review journal, it now includes Letters and Research Papers as well as Review Articles, Reports on Progress and Key Issues Reviews |

A literature survey summarizes publications. An authoritative review does more: it defines the field, evaluates evidence, reconciles terminology, distinguishes consensus from dispute, compares methods and identifies unresolved questions. Review articles, reports on rapidly developing fields and perspective-like key-issues reviews consequently have different intellectual functions.

The 2026 scope of **Reports on Progress in Physics** is an important example of why journal identity is time-dependent. Its historic reputation is strongly associated with reviews, but its current article portfolio also includes highly selective original Letters and Research Papers. The journal title alone no longer describes every article type it publishes.

## 11. Access models and the HEP exception

Access model describes who can read the version of record and how publication is financed. It does not measure scientific quality.

| Model | Reader access | Author-side structure | Examples in this landscape |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Subscription** | Access normally supplied by a reader or library subscription | Often no mandatory OA fee, although other charges can exist | Traditional route in many hybrid journals |
| **Hybrid** | Subscription by default; individual articles may be made open | Optional gold OA commonly carries an APC or is covered by an agreement | Nature Physics, Physical Review A/D, Annals of Physics, EPJ Plus |
| **Gold open access** | Version of record is open to all readers | APC, waiver, institutional agreement or external sponsor commonly funds publication | Physical Review X, PRX Quantum, New Journal of Physics |
| **Diamond or sponsored OA** | Version of record is open | No author-facing APC | SciPost journals; several SCOAP³-supported HEP journals |
| **Green open access** | An allowed manuscript version is available in a repository | Governed by the publisher's self-archiving policy | Commonly associated with arXiv or institutional repositories |

APC size and editorial quality are independent variables. Strong journals exist under subscription, hybrid, gold and diamond arrangements. Similarly, absence of an APC can result from subscription funding, society support, institutional support or a disciplinary consortium rather than from lower editorial standards.

### SCOAP³

[SCOAP³](https://scoap3.org/faqs/) is a consortium-based open-access system for high-energy physics. It centrally supports full-journal open access for titles including **JHEP**, **European Physical Journal C**, **Physics Letters B** and **Nuclear Physics B**, and supports eligible HEP articles in participating hybrid journals. [APS describes the SCOAP³ arrangement](https://journals.aps.org/authors/scoap3) for eligible content in Physical Review journals.

This model reflects the distinctive HEP publication culture: wide use of arXiv, international laboratory and library cooperation, and a literature concentrated in a recognizable journal set. SCOAP³ coverage is policy- and article-dependent, so its presence is an access fact rather than a journal-quality label.

### Preprints and journal versions

An arXiv preprint provides rapid public dissemination and a dated scholarly record. It is not identical to journal peer-review certification. The accepted manuscript and the publisher's version of record are later stages with different editorial and legal status. Physics communities often read all three, particularly in HEP, quantum information and mathematical physics.

## 12. Why rankings and quartiles vary

Journal rankings are produced by defined databases and formulas rather than discovered as natural properties of journals. Variation arises from several sources.

### Different databases

Web of Science, Scopus and Google Scholar cover different sets of documents and citations. SCImago uses Scopus-derived data but applies its own SJR method. A journal's position can therefore change when the underlying universe of journals changes.

### Different categories

A journal may appear in more than one subject category. EPJ Plus, a broad physics journal, is compared with a different peer set from a specialist mathematical-physics or HEP title. The same journal can occupy different quartiles in different categories during the same year.

### Different time windows and formulas

Journal Impact Factor uses a short citation window. CiteScore uses a longer window and a different document treatment. SJR weights citations by the prestige network of citing journals. SNIP attempts field normalization. Journal h-index is cumulative and strongly affected by age and publication volume. None of these measures the same construct.

### Different citation cultures

Fast-moving experimental or technology fields can accumulate citations quickly. Mathematical physics and some areas of formal theory may develop influence over longer periods. Large collaborations, review articles, data releases and community software also create citation patterns unlike those of single-author theorem papers.

### Journal size, age and article mix

Older, larger journals usually have higher cumulative h-indices. Review-rich journals often have high short-window citation averages. A journal publishing many articles may have broad citation reach but a different average from a small selective journal. A small number of exceptionally cited papers can pull an average upward while most papers remain near or below the median.

### Annual movement

Quartiles are relative bins. A journal can move even when its own citation performance changes little because other journals enter the category, leave it or change rank. Database policy and category reassignment can also produce movement unrelated to a sudden change in editorial quality.

For these reasons, a complete quartile statement includes the **database, metric, data year and category**. Current JCR coverage belongs to the [Web of Science Master Journal List](https://mjl.clarivate.com/home) and Journal Citation Reports; current Scopus coverage and CiteScore context belong to [Scopus Sources](https://www.scopus.com/sources). A number copied without this context is not a reproducible bibliometric claim.

[DORA](https://sfdora.org/read/) further distinguishes journal-level citation behaviour from the quality of an individual article or researcher. Journal averages describe a publication venue; they do not predict the correctness, originality or future influence of every paper inside it.

## 13. The landscape at a glance

| Scientific area | Broad or exceptionally selective edge | Core or specialist journals | Distinctive article culture |
|---|---|---|---|
| Physics-wide quantum advance | Nature Physics, Physical Review X, Physical Review Letters | Broad venues vary with the dominant subfield | Concise conceptual message or complete major advance with cross-field significance |
| Quantum information and QIST | PRX Quantum | Quantum Science and Technology, npj Quantum Information, Physical Review A, Quantum | Operational results, algorithms, theorems, benchmarks, devices and quantum protocols |
| Quantum foundations | Broad journals only for exceptional cross-field consequences | Physical Review A, Foundations of Physics, Quantum Studies: Mathematics and Foundations | Formal, operational and conceptual analysis |
| Mathematical physics | Communications in Mathematical Physics, Annales Henri Poincaré | Journal of Mathematical Physics, Journal of Physics A, Letters in Mathematical Physics | Theorems, proofs, mathematical structures and physically motivated formal methods |
| Deep theoretical physics | Broad selective venues at landmark threshold | Annals of Physics, Journal of Physics A, Nuclear Physics B | Long derivations, first-principles development and complete theoretical constructions |
| HEP and QFT | Physical Review Letters for concise exceptional results | JHEP, Physical Review D, European Physical Journal C, Nuclear Physics B, IJMPA | Formal theory, phenomenology, calculations and specialist field certification |
| Gravitation and cosmology | Broad journals for physics-wide consequences | Physical Review D, JCAP, Classical and Quantum Gravity, General Relativity and Gravitation, EPJ C | Mathematical, theoretical, numerical and observationally connected gravity research |
| Broad physics | Physical Review X at exceptional threshold | Physical Review Research, New Journal of Physics, SciPost Physics Core, EPJ Plus | Cross-field or broadly scoped complete research |
| Reviews | Reviews of Modern Physics and other highly selective review routes | Physics Reports; review formats in Reports on Progress in Physics | Critical synthesis, field organization and long-term reference value |

The map is multidimensional. Breadth, selectivity, rigor, specialist centrality, article length, access and citation position describe different aspects of the publication system. A sophisticated understanding preserves those differences instead of collapsing them into a single ranking.
