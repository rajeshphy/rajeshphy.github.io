A scientific event is temporary research or teaching infrastructure. It brings a defined group of people, scientific questions, facilities and funds together for a stated purpose. The academic programme, institutional authority, participant system, financial record and post-event output are therefore parts of one activity—not separate ceremonial and administrative layers.

This chapter is an organizer-facing manual. [Module 8]({{ '/theoretical-physics-conferences-schools-programmes/' | relative_url }}) maps meetings from a participant's perspective, [Module 9]({{ '/theoretical-physics-collaboration-research-network/' | relative_url }}) explains how meetings support collaboration, and [Module 10]({{ '/funding-theoretical-physics-indian-colleges/' | relative_url }}) maps the wider funding system. The present module describes how an Indian college can turn an event idea into an approved, scientifically coherent, accessible and auditable institutional activity.

<div class="note-box" markdown="1">
**Seminar, workshop, school, symposium and conference are not interchangeable labels.** The format describes what participants will do. A sequence of invited talks does not become a workshop merely because it is advertised under that name, and participation by one overseas speaker does not by itself make an event an international conference.
</div>

The reference date is **30 June 2026**. Funder rules, application windows, admissible rates, visa processes and institutional delegations of financial power can change. The current call, portal, sanction letter and host-institution rules always prevail over this general manual.

## 1. Choosing the correct event format

Scientific-event names are not standardized perfectly across agencies, but the following operational distinctions are useful.

| Format | Primary purpose | Normal academic structure | Typical scale or duration | Appropriate output |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Research seminar | Communicate one piece of current work | One talk, questions and technical discussion | 60–120 minutes | Discussion record, references and possible follow-up contact |
| Colloquium | Explain a field or major result to a broad physics audience | Accessible lecture with a longer conceptual introduction | 60–90 minutes | Wider departmental awareness |
| Seminar series | Maintain regular contact with current research | Recurring talks under a stable theme or calendar | Weekly, fortnightly or monthly | Public calendar, talk archive and durable network |
| Symposium or discussion meeting | Compare several views on one sharply defined subject | A small number of invited contributions and substantial discussion | Half day to two days | Synthesis, open-problem list or research agenda |
| Lecture workshop | Strengthen understanding of a defined topic | Extended lectures, questions and sometimes tutorials | Commonly two or three days | Learning material and participant feedback |
| Focused workshop | Work actively on methods or problems | Tutorials, calculations, coding, problem groups, posters and collaboration time | Two to five days | Notes, notebooks, tested calculations or working groups |
| School or refresher course | Teach a coherent body of material in sequence | Prerequisites, linked lectures, tutorials, exercises and assessment or feedback | Several days to two weeks or longer | Demonstrable learning and reusable course material |
| Conference | Present and discuss recent research across a field or subfield | Plenary, invited and contributed talks, posters, and parallel sessions only if justified | Two to five days is common | Research exchange; an abstract book or proceedings only when separately arranged |
| Long research programme | Create a temporary research community | A light formal schedule with seminars, visitors and protected collaboration time | Several weeks or months | New calculations, collaborations, preprints or proposals |
| Public lecture or outreach event | Explain science beyond the specialist community | Non-technical presentation and moderated public questions | One session | Public understanding rather than specialist training |

The [ICTP event-format descriptions](https://www.ictp.it/home/propose-ictp-event) distinguish pedagogical schools, specialized advanced schools or workshops, conferences on recent results and extended collaborative workshops. The [ICTS programme guidelines](https://www.icts.res.in/organize-program/program_guidelines) similarly emphasize scientific interaction and pedagogy rather than an ordinary talk-heavy conference. These are useful design models even when an event is not proposed to ICTP or ICTS.

The audience determines the format. Undergraduate students may need a colloquium or syllabus-connected lecture workshop; MSc students and beginning PhD scholars may need a school with prerequisites and exercises; active specialists may need a small workshop with unscheduled research time. A programme advertised simultaneously for school students, undergraduates, doctoral researchers and international specialists usually has no clearly defined intellectual level.

## 2. A staged event system for a remote department

An institution does not need to begin with a large conference. A credible event system can grow in stages.

| Stage | Minimum viable activity | Administrative load | Scientific value created |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Monthly online or hybrid seminar | Official page, speaker consent, moderator, recording choice and attendance record | Regular exposure to active researchers |
| 2 | Half-day regional discussion meeting | Institutional approval, small programme, nearby speakers and local participants | Connects departments within practical travel distance |
| 3 | Two-day focused lecture workshop | Committees, itemized budget, participant call, hospitality and final report | Establishes a visible thematic node and an event record |
| 4 | Multi-institutional school or funded symposium | External proposal, selection system, finance file, travel, accommodation and formal closure | Builds advanced training and national participation |
| 5 | Recurring school, network or long programme | Stable partnerships, demonstrated past closure and multi-year academic continuity | Converts isolated events into a research community |

For a remote venue, travel time from the nearest railhead or airport, power reliability, bandwidth, accommodation safety and local transport are part of scientific design. A programme that begins before participants can realistically arrive, or loses remote speakers whenever the connection fails, is not operationally complete. Useful low-resource provisions include locally cached slides, a second mobile-network connection, UPS or generator support, a tested conference speakerphone, an offline copy of notebooks and a nearby university or institute as a scientific partner.

## 3. Institutional ownership and three separate approvals

Organizing a scientific event involves at least three distinct decisions:

1. **Academic approval** accepts the theme, purpose, audience, speakers and proposed programme.
2. **Administrative and financial approval** authorizes the institution to host the event, constitute committees, use facilities, collect any approved fee, receive funds and incur expenditure under specified rules.
3. **External funding sanction** approves only the agency's contribution and conditions. It does not replace the host institution's approval.

An event should therefore have a file or controlled digital record before public announcement. Depending on the institution, it may contain:

- the concept note and departmental recommendation;
- approval of the Principal, Director, Registrar or other competent authority;
- an office order constituting the committees and naming the convener;
- approved dates, venue, mode and capacity;
- expenditure sanction and the institutional finance or purchase route;
- the authorized institutional bank account, ledger or cost centre;
- permission to collect and account for a registration fee, if any;
- venue, IT, guest-house, transport and security permissions;
- on-duty or leave arrangements for employees and students; and
- funder sanction, partner letters and later amendments.

No speaker, hotel or vendor should receive a financial promise before the person with the required institutional authority has approved it. A scientific committee can recommend expenditure; it normally cannot override procurement, taxation, audit or delegation-of-power rules.

### The internal contact sequence

In a college without a conference office, the following sequence makes responsibility visible:

| Question | First institutional contact | Decision or information required |
|---|---|---|
| Is the scientific idea suitable? | Head of Department and proposed scientific co-organizer | Theme, audience, format, dates and academic ownership |
| May the college host and represent the event? | Principal, Director, Registrar or other competent authority | Administrative approval and authorized signatory |
| Is the institution eligible for the named scheme? | Research cell, IQAC or designated grants office, with the scheme guideline | Recognition, affiliation, accreditation, appointment and document tests |
| How will money be received and recorded? | Accounts or finance officer | Institutional bank details, ledger/cost centre, interest and audit route |
| Who may approve a purchase, hotel or vehicle? | Finance and purchase/stores section | Delegated power, quotations, order, tax and payment procedure |
| Can the proposed rooms and services be supplied? | Estate, IT, guest house, transport, security and medical units as applicable | Written availability, costs, access and emergency arrangements |
| Who handles access and conduct issues? | Disability/access officer, Internal Committee and competent administrative authority | Named event contacts, referral route, confidentiality limits and emergency escalation |
| Is a funder rule unclear? | Programme contact shown on the current official scheme page | A short written question quoting the exact clause and event facts |

External speakers and partners can be approached for scientific interest while approval is developing, but the message should say that dates and financial support remain subject to institutional and funding sanction. Only the authorized officer commits the institution.

## 4. Organization structure and responsibility

There is no single committee structure mandated for every Indian college. The event's scale, the host's statutes and the funder's rules control. The following functional structure is sufficient for most academic events:

```text
Head of Institution / competent authority
                    │
       Convener or scientific director
          ┌─────────┼─────────┐
          │         │         │
 Scientific     Local      Finance and
 programme    organizing    accounts
 committee     committee       lead
          │         │         │
  theme, speakers,  registration, venue, travel,
  abstracts, chairs hospitality, IT, safety,
  and programme     communication and records
```

| Function | Main responsibility | Records controlled |
|---|---|---|
| Head of Institution or competent authority | Gives institutional authority, accepts grants or agreements and designates financial signatories | Approval, endorsement, sanction acceptance and institutional certification |
| Convener or event director | Integrates the scientific, administrative and reporting work; is the principal contact | Concept note, master action register, correspondence and completion report |
| Scientific Organizing Committee (SOC) | Defines scope and prerequisites; selects speakers; reviews applications or abstracts; appoints chairs | Scientific programme, review criteria, decisions, conflicts and minutes |
| Local Organizing Committee (LOC) | Runs the venue, registration, accommodation, transport, catering, signage, IT, volunteers and help desk | Supplier and participant operations, room plans and run sheet |
| Finance and procurement lead | Checks admissibility, sanctions, purchasing, payments, ledger and closure | Budget, quotations, orders, invoices, claims, payment evidence, SoE and UC |
| Secretariat and communications lead | Maintains the official email, website, versioned programme and notices | Applicant register, mailing record, invitation and decision letters |
| Speaker and travel liaison | Confirms titles, biographies, travel plans, support terms and documents | Speaker status sheet, itinerary, reimbursement checklist and consent |
| Registration and selection team | Receives applications, assigns IDs and implements recorded decisions | Applications, abstracts, waitlist, acceptance and attendance records |
| Venue, AV and hybrid-production lead | Tests projection, blackboards, microphones, internet, platform access and backups | Technical checklist, rehearsal record and failure plan |
| Accessibility, safety and conduct contacts | Receive access requests; communicate safety and conduct routes; coordinate with authorized institutional bodies | Restricted access-needs record, incident route and emergency contacts |
| Session chair | Introduces speakers, keeps time, moderates questions and enforces the code | Chair sheet and session notes |
| Rapporteur or archive lead | Records scientific content, feedback and authorized materials | Session summaries, report, archive permissions and follow-up actions |
| Student volunteers | Perform narrowly assigned desk, escort, timekeeping or technical tasks under supervision | Duty roster and shift handover |

In a seminar, four or five people can combine several functions. In a larger conference, the SOC and LOC should be distinct enough that scientific selection is not displaced by hospitality work. The finance officer should be an institutional function, not a ceremonial “treasurer” collecting money into a personal account.

An office order should state names, roles, authority, reporting relationship, event dates and the life of the committee. It should also identify who may approve speakers, participants, travel support and purchases. Long lists of patrons, honorary advisers and chief guests do not substitute for these responsibilities. Some schemes prohibit such decorative lists: the Science Academies' Lecture Workshop manual, for example, specifies Convener and Coordinator roles and asks organizers to avoid patrons and management names in its publicity.

## 5. Whom to conduct the event with

The partner should supply a real scientific or administrative function. A logo without an agreed role is not a collaboration.

| Need | Appropriate partner | Possible documented role |
|---|---|---|
| Subject depth and active speakers | Nearby state or central university, IISER, IIT, NIT, national laboratory or research institute | Co-organizer, SOC member, resource person, tutorial leader or follow-up mentor |
| Academy-supported college teaching event | A Fellow of IASc, INSA or NASI with relevant expertise | Convener of a Science Academies' Lecture Workshop or Course Director, subject to its rules |
| DAE-aligned nuclear, particle, plasma, accelerator, quantum-sensor or related topic | Relevant DAE unit or BRNS community | Scientific partner or resource person; a BRNS event proposal must satisfy BRNS subject rules |
| Mathematical physics | Mathematicians and mathematical physicists with genuine subject fit | Programme design, review and a possible NBHM-aligned event |
| Astronomy, cosmology or gravitation | IUCAA faculty, Associates or relevant Resource Centre, where programme terms fit | Recurring local activity, resource persons or a continuing student link |
| Advanced thematic programme | ICTS scientific community or other programme centre | A reviewed programme proposal hosted under the centre's own arrangements |
| Low-resource international teaching connection | ICTP Physics Without Frontiers, under its current call | Volunteer-scientist school, course, roadshow, seminar series or faculty support |
| Formal doctoral participation | Affiliating university or recognized research centre | Approval, scholar participation, supervisor network and follow-up activity |

Speaker selection should begin with required topics, not famous names. Each speaker or instructor should have a defined function: foundation lecture, research lecture, derivation tutorial, computational laboratory, problem session, student-feedback session or research discussion. Current publication and group pages show subject fit; teaching clarity and willingness to engage show format fit. A balanced programme normally includes more than one institution and career stage and does not sell scientific slots to sponsors.

For the Science Academies route, the [subject-wise list of INSA Fellows](https://www.insaindia.res.in/pdf/Subjectwise_INSA_Fellow.pdf), the other Academies' directories and the [Joint Science Education Panel](https://webjapps.ias.ac.in/SEP/PanelMembers.jsp) can help identify a scientifically relevant person. An initial approach can attach a one-page concept note containing the local academic need, intended participants, narrow topic, tentative dates, host facilities and requested role. The named programme's formal rules still determine who may act as Convener or Course Director.

## 6. The one-page scientific concept note

The concept note is the common source for institutional approval, partner discussions and the later funding proposal. A compact template is:

```text
Working title and event format:
Host department and institution:
Narrow scientific theme:

Scientific context:
What body of theory, current problem or educational gap does the event address?

Purpose:
What will be different after the event—knowledge, methods, calculations,
research connections, teaching capacity or a defined collaborative output?

Audience and prerequisites:
Career stage, expected prior courses or methods, geographic reach and capacity.

Academic structure:
Lectures, tutorials, exercises, posters, discussion or collaboration blocks.

Proposed scientific team:
Convener, co-organizers, possible speakers/resource persons and their roles.
Mark each person as proposed, approached, interested or confirmed.

Dates, mode and venue:
Include the realistic arrival/departure window and hybrid requirements.

Expected outputs and follow-up:
Notes, notebooks, reading group, seminar series, open-problem record or collaboration.

Preliminary budget and sources:
Host contribution, requested agency support, other requested support and fee, if any.

Institutional requirements:
Approvals, account/cost centre, facilities, procurement and external clearances.
```

The title should identify a coherent subject—such as *Open Quantum Systems: Methods and Applications* or *Geometric Methods in Quantum Theory*—rather than combine every branch of modern physics. The programme should then make the title true.

## 7. Planning timeline

The following schedule is an operating model, not a substitute for a funder's deadline.

| Time before event | Academic work | Administrative and operational work |
|---|---|---|
| 12–9 months | Need assessment, format, theme, possible partners and anchor speakers | Obtain preliminary institutional approval; examine venue, accommodation and likely funding routes |
| 9–6 months | Form SOC; draft learning or research outcomes and tentative sessions | Form LOC and finance team; build the source-wise budget; submit schemes with long lead times |
| 6–4 months | Record speaker interest; prepare public scope, prerequisites and review rubric | Finalize website, official email, brochure, forms, risk plan and sponsor requests |
| 4–2 months | Review applicants and abstracts; create waitlist; confirm programme | Book authorized services; plan travel, rooms, access arrangements and international clearances |
| 8–4 weeks | Issue final titles and chair assignments; circulate preparatory material | Send joining instructions; collect itineraries and permitted financial documents; test hybrid system |
| Final 2 weeks | Freeze programme except necessary corrections; brief chairs and rapporteurs | Reconcile lists; create run sheet, desk packs, signage, emergency sheet and offline backups |
| Event days | Protect scientific time, discussion and participant access | Record attendance, consent, claims, invoices, schedule changes and incidents |
| 1–2 weeks after | Compile feedback, scientific report, materials and follow-up actions | Settle claims and vendors; reconcile every budget head and archive the controlled records |
| Funder deadline | Report scientific outputs | Submit SoE, UC, audit documents and unspent-balance action; obtain formal closure |

ANRF Seminar/Symposia currently requires an application 60–180 days before the start date; the Science Academies say that Lecture Workshop and Refresher Course proposals should preferably arrive at least four months ahead; NBHM specifies longer advance periods for national and international conferences; and BRNS indicates a multi-month appraisal process. These are different clocks. Publicity should not start on the assumption that a pending grant will be approved.

## 8. Designing the academic programme

The opening questions are: What must participants know before arrival? What should they be able to explain or do at the end? Which session produces that change?

### A research seminar

A useful 90-minute structure is 5 minutes for the introduction, 50–60 minutes for the talk, 20–30 minutes for questions and a short closing notice about references or follow-up. The chair protects the technical discussion from a prolonged ceremonial introduction.

### A two-day theoretical-physics workshop

One possible structure is:

| Day | Morning | Afternoon |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Common notation and foundations; one extended research lecture | Guided derivation, computational notebook or problem groups; poster discussion |
| 2 | Two connected advanced lectures | Open-problem groups; group reports; references, follow-up seminar dates and closing scientific synthesis |

### A school or refresher course

Lectures should form a sequence rather than a collection. Each unit states prerequisites, notation, core results, exercises and reading. Tutorials, blackboard calculations, problem solving or computing are part of the timetable. A feedback or diagnostic exercise can measure learning without turning the event into an examination unless formal assessment was announced.

### A conference

Plenary talks provide common reference points; contributed talks and posters bring new work into the programme. Parallel sessions are justified only when attendance and topic breadth make them necessary. Breaks, poster periods and unscheduled discussion are scientific programme elements, not empty spaces to eliminate. A “talk marathon” leaves little opportunity for comprehension or collaboration.

## 9. Speaker invitations and status control

The internal speaker sheet should mark each person as **proposed**, **approached**, **interested**, **formally invited**, **accepted** or **withdrawn**. Only accepted speakers should appear as confirmed. A reusable invitation contains:

```text
Subject: Invitation to contribute to [exact event title], [dates]

Institution and organizers:
Scientific purpose and intended audience:
Requested contribution: [lecture/tutorial/problem session/panel]
Proposed topic and duration:
Physical, online or hybrid format:

Financial terms:
Travel class or ceiling, lodging, local hospitality, honorarium if admissible,
reimbursement method, required evidence, and whether support is sanctioned
or still subject to approval.

Publication and media terms:
Abstract, slides or notes requested; separate choices for photography,
streaming, recording and later public release.

Please confirm by:
Please supply by:
Official contact and institutional webpage:
```

“Invited to speak” and “fully funded” are different statuses. The invitation must not promise airfare, hotel, honorarium or reimbursement outside the approved budget. Reimbursement instructions should precede ticket purchase and specify permitted class, route, booking evidence, tickets, boarding documents, local-transport evidence, bank form and submission deadline under the applicable rules.

## 10. Call for participation, abstracts and selection

The public event page or call should state:

- exact scope and topics;
- intended audience and prerequisites;
- dates, venue, mode and capacity;
- invited lecture, contributed talk, poster and attendance-only categories;
- application and abstract fields;
- selection and conflict-of-interest principles;
- what financial support is available, to whom and under what ceiling;
- fee, payment route, cancellation and refund policy, if any;
- accommodation and local-travel arrangements;
- accessibility, dietary and conduct information;
- deadlines and decision dates;
- photography, streaming and recording choices;
- data-use and retention information; and
- an official institutional email and authoritative webpage.

Registration, abstract acceptance, oral presentation, accommodation and travel support are separate decisions. A clear decision letter lists each status separately.

### A documented review rubric

| Criterion | Question recorded by reviewer |
|---|---|
| Scope | Does the application or abstract fit the announced scientific theme? |
| Preparation | Does the applicant have the stated prerequisites for this format? |
| Scientific content | Is the question, method and claimed result intelligible and credible at abstract level? |
| Contribution fit | Is a talk, poster, working group or attendance place appropriate? |
| Likely benefit | Is the event likely to supply knowledge or access not readily available to the applicant? |
| Representation | Does the final cohort serve the stated institutional, regional, career-stage and inclusion aims? |
| Feasibility | Can the event accommodate the requested technical, travel or access support? |

Reviewers should disclose conflicts and recuse. Selection minutes record the rubric, reviewers, recusals, capacity, decision and waitlist. Scientific selection should not be sold through a sponsorship package. A fee may support genuine event costs; it should not buy an invited or contributed presentation.

For a larger meeting, [Indico's official organizer documentation](https://learn.getindico.io/) explains programme tracks, calls for abstracts, reviewer roles, registration, timetables, privacy, surveys and documents. Indico is open-source software; an institution still needs an authorized hosted instance or another controlled system. A small event can use an institutional form and access-controlled register, provided the same records and decisions are maintained.

## 11. Data, communication and records

One event webpage and one institutional email should be authoritative. Messaging groups are useful for short operational notices but are not a replacement for official decisions, current programmes or financial instructions.

The application system should collect only information necessary at that stage. Passport data, bank details, identity or category documents and access requirements should be visible only to authorized personnel and should not be placed in a general volunteer spreadsheet. Every application receives a unique ID and acknowledgment. The event declares how long records will be retained and which public items—such as name, affiliation, title or abstract—will appear online.

Programme versions should display a revision date. A late change is recorded once and propagated to the webpage, chair sheet, room display and participant message from the same source. Funder and partner logos are used only after authorization. “Supported by,” “sponsored by,” “in collaboration with” and “hosted by” describe different relationships and should be used accurately.

## 12. Budget architecture

A budget contains both **uses** and **sources**. Estimating only expenditure conceals the funding gap.

| Cost head | Calculation basis | Funder requested | Host contribution | Other confirmed/requested source |
|---|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Invited-speaker travel | Eligible people × realistic approved fare |  |  |  |
| Participant travel support | Supported participants × ceiling |  |  |  |
| Accommodation | Room nights × approved rate |  |  |  |
| Working meals and refreshments | Eligible persons × sessions/days × rate |  |  |  |
| Local transport | Authorized trips or contracted vehicle basis |  |  |  |
| Venue and AV | Only if not supplied in kind by host |  |  |  |
| Hybrid platform or production | Licensed service or approved technical cost |  |  |  |
| Printing or event material | Quantity × unit cost; only if admissible |  |  |  |
| Accessibility arrangements | Captioning, interpretation or other approved provision |  |  |  |
| Honoraria | Named eligible roles × scheme rate, only when allowed |  |  |  |
| Contingency | Scheme-defined basis, not an unrecorded reserve |  |  |  |
| **Total uses** |  |  |  |  |

The source table also lists institutional in-kind facilities, registration income and sponsorship. Every external source should be disclosed to each funder. The same invoice or journey cannot be claimed twice. A shortfall scenario states which costs will be reduced if a partial award is made; it must not quietly transfer an unannounced burden to participants.

For theoretical physics, scientific access normally has priority over decorations, gifts, elaborate inaugurations and publicity objects. Capital equipment, furniture, alcohol, sightseeing, mementos, international airfare, honorarium, venue hire or publication costs may be inadmissible under a particular scheme even if useful elsewhere. Only the current guideline and sanction letter determine admissibility.

## 13. Financial control and audit trail

Event money should move through the authorized institutional account and books, never a convener's personal bank account, personal UPI ID or informal cash pool. The finance record normally includes:

- institutional approval and external sanction;
- approved head-wise budget and every approved revision;
- grant receipt, registration-fee and sponsorship registers;
- a separate ledger or cost centre;
- quotations, comparative statement, purchase approval and work or supply order where required;
- invoice, receipt, proof of delivery and proof of payment;
- travel approval, ticket, journey evidence and signed claim;
- advances and their adjustment;
- attendance or service evidence supporting the expense;
- applicable GST, TDS and other institutional tax treatment;
- head-wise reconciliation, bank reconciliation and interest record;
- Statement of Expenditure, Utilization Certificate and audit certification; and
- return or adjustment of unspent funds and formal closure.

The sanctioning agency's conditions and the institution's finance, procurement and delegation rules must be read together. Government institutions should consult the current [Department of Expenditure manuals and General Financial Rules](https://doe.gov.in/manuals) as incorporated into their own rules; a general web article cannot decide local quotation thresholds or signing powers.

### 14.1 ANRF Seminar/Symposia: application anatomy

The live ANRF page currently describes partial support for national or international scientific events held in India. The convener is an Indian citizen residing in India and holding a regular position at an eligible recognized institution. The application is submitted through the portal 60–180 days before an event of two to five consecutive days.

The proposal contains the event identity, objectives, keywords, venue, dates, estimated expenditure and institutional bank information. Current mandatory material includes the convener certificate, Head of Institution endorsement, announcement brochure, session-wise technical programme with chairs or co-chairs, and keynote-speaker information. ANRF's official files include:

- [technical proposal and budget template](https://www.anrfonline.in/ANRF/AbstractFilePath?FileName=OTD_SSY.pdf&FileType=E&PathKey=DOCUMENT_TEMPLATE);
- [convener certificate](https://www.anrfonline.in/ANRF/AbstractFilePath?FileName=CFI_SSY.pdf&FileType=E&PathKey=DOCUMENT_TEMPLATE);
- [Head of Institution endorsement](https://anrfonline.in/ANRF/AbstractFilePath?FileName=END_SSY.pdf&FileType=E&PathKey=DOCUMENT_TEMPLATE);
- [post-event completion report](https://anrfonline.in/ANRF/AbstractFilePath?FileName=SSY_PCR.pdf&FileType=E&PathKey=DOCUMENT_TEMPLATE); and
- [Statement of Expenditure and Utilization Certificate](https://anrfonline.in/ANRF/AbstractFilePath?FileName=UCR_SSY.pdf&FileType=E&PathKey=DOCUMENT_TEMPLATE).

The displayed support heads include domestic travel for Indian scientists, pre-conference printing and contingency or incidental working expenses under the scheme. It does not support international-participant airfare. The award is released to the Head of Institution or other authorized organizational head, not to the convener personally. Private colleges have additional institutional documents on recognition, governing-body authority, prior accounts and specified registrations or undertakings. ANRF's logo should not be used before approval. The current page and portal must be rechecked because the displayed eligible event window rolls with time.

### 14.2 The Science Academies' route for a college

The Joint Science Education Panel's Lecture Workshop is unusually well matched to a college outside a major centre. Its official manual states that proposals from less-endowed institutions may receive preference without compromising merit and quality, and it seeks geographical diversity. If the usual number of Academy Fellows is unavailable in a remote area, the Coordinator or Convener may seek special permission by supplying the backgrounds of proposed non-Fellow resource persons.

The standard structure is specific:

- a Fellow of one of the three Academies acts as **Convener**;
- a faculty member at the host institution acts as **Coordinator**;
- the event lasts two or three days and primarily serves local UG/PG students and teachers;
- the host contacts nearby colleges and supplies the hall, audiovisual facilities, reliable power and service space;
- the academic plan uses a small number of resource persons for extended lectures and interaction; and
- the proposal is preferably submitted at least four months in advance to the Joint Science Education Panel.

The current manual contains the actual budget heads, rates, instalment method and after-event account format. Rates should be read from the live manual rather than copied into a future plan. It also contains non-obvious programme rules: the activity is called a Science Academies' Lecture Workshop rather than a national conference; publicity avoids lists of patrons and management; and formal participant certificates are ordinarily replaced by an attendance letter if needed. The report, expenditure statement, supporting vouchers, programme, participant list and authorized photographs are due under the manual's post-event process.

The longer Refresher Course route is intended chiefly for teacher development. Its [detailed guide](https://webjapps.ias.ac.in/SEP/pdf_files/RCG.pdf) supplies proposal fields, course structure, resource-person expectations, participant selection and sample budget formats. A theoretical course should contain tutorials, questions and problem-solving rather than only lectures.

### 14.3 BRNS and NBHM

BRNS accepts symposium, conference, seminar and workshop proposals in areas relevant to its DAE mandate. The [BRNS activity process](https://brns.res.in/activities.php) and [downloads page](https://brns.res.in/download_forms.php) describe registration, online submission, required documents and closure forms; the [official symposium user manual](https://brns.res.in/public_files/201611101248510.USER_Manual_Symp_101116.pdf) gives screen-level instructions. An online application and any required signed institutional copy form one process, not alternatives. A retained portal page does not remove the need to check current subject, document and dispatch instructions.

NBHM supports national or international conferences, workshops and seminars that genuinely belong to mathematics. Its [conference-support page](https://nbhm.dae.gov.in/conferences.html) publishes the current lead times, ceilings, organizer credentials, form and closure conditions. It also states that the same event should not be submitted to both BRNS and NBHM. Mathematical notation alone does not make a general physics meeting an NBHM meeting.

### 14.4 Hosted-programme routes are not ordinary grants

An approved ICTS proposal becomes an ICTS programme under the centre's reviewed arrangements; it is not simply a grant transferred to a college for an independent conference. ICTS asks for a timely scientific theme, clear objectives, participant and speaker plans, expected interest, interaction, diversity and justified duration. Faculty at educational or research institutions can propose a programme, normally with co-organizers.

ICTP similarly reviews scientific-calendar and outside-activity proposals under its own programme conditions. Physics Without Frontiers is particularly relevant to departments with little local research access, but it works through calls and a jointly designed capacity-building activity rather than unconditional event finance. These routes can create deeper scientific contact than a one-time sponsorship, but their institutional object must be described correctly.

## 15. Venue, travel and remote-location logistics

Participant instructions should include:

- the full postal address and map coordinates;
- routes from the nearest rail, bus and air points, with realistic travel times;
- safe arrival windows and the last practical public-transport connection;
- authorized pickup points and a named contact;
- accommodation address, check-in rules, room sharing and distance from venue;
- venue accessibility, toilets, quiet or prayer space and dietary arrangements;
- weather, clothing and local health information where material; and
- reimbursement rules and documents sent before the journey.

The venue check covers capacity, sight lines, equation-readable projection, blackboard or writing tablet, microphones, power backup, ventilation, fire exits, drinking water, first aid, nearest medical facility and safe transport after evening sessions. Food allergens should be labelled. An access-needs question should be asked during registration, not at the door.

A simple risk register records the hazard, likelihood, impact, owner, prevention, response and escalation contact. Relevant hazards include speaker withdrawal, transport disruption, unsafe crowding, power or network failure, medical emergency, extreme weather, food or water problem, data breach, harassment report, vendor failure and event cancellation.

## 16. Hybrid and online operation

A hybrid session needs three roles: the scientific chair, the platform moderator and the technical producer. One person cannot reliably present, admit attendees, monitor chat, operate slides, troubleshoot audio and moderate scientific questions at the same time.

Minimum controls include:

- a rehearsal with each remote speaker;
- wired internet where possible and a second-network backup;
- separate room and remote microphones so both audiences can hear questions;
- direct screen sharing rather than a distant camera view of equations;
- captions where feasible and advance access to readable material;
- an access-controlled link and a defined late-admission rule;
- one moderated question queue across both audiences;
- offline slides, speaker phone number and a failure decision tree; and
- a clear notice about chat, screenshots, photography and recording.

Recording requires explicit permission. The form should distinguish live streaming, recording the talk, recording questions, publishing slides and permanent public release. A speaker can consent to one and decline another. Participants must have a usable no-photo or no-record route.

## 17. Conduct, inclusion, accessibility and safety

A written code of conduct should cover lecture rooms, online platforms, accommodation, transport, meals and social activity. It defines prohibited harassment, discrimination, intimidation and disruption; names at least two approachable contacts; explains confidentiality limits and possible immediate measures; and connects the event to the institution's competent authority, Internal Committee and emergency process. Event volunteers should not improvise an investigation beyond their authority.

Session chairs set the discussion standard: concise questions, no hostile monologues, no discriminatory remarks, respect for unpublished material and time for participants who are less likely to interrupt. The [IOP Inclusive Conference Guide](https://www.iop.org/sites/default/files/2026-02/Inclusive_Conference_Guide.pdf) supplies a practical checklist covering advance access questions, accessible venues and documents, breaks, dietary labelling, quiet or prayer space, caring needs, scheduling and balanced participation. The [IOP Conference and Event Guide](https://www.iop.org/sites/default/files/2026-01/conference-event-guide-2025.pdf) covers planning, risk, budgets, venue, registration and evaluation. [APS session-chair guidance](https://www.aps.org/about/governance/policies-procedures/session-chair-guidelines) provides a useful model for introductions, timekeeping, question moderation and conduct.

Where minors attend, safeguarding, supervision and consent requirements need a separate institutional plan. Accessibility and safety costs should be budgeted rather than treated as unexpected favors.

## 18. Day-of-event operating manual

The master run sheet has one row for every operational change:

| Time | Activity | Responsible person | Location or link | Equipment/document | Dependency | Fallback |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 08:00 | Desk and AV open | Registration and technical leads | Foyer / Room 1 | Lists, badges, QR/manual attendance, projector, microphones | Power and room access | Printed list, UPS and spare laptop |
| 09:00 | Opening scientific orientation | Convener | Room 1 | Programme and conduct slide | Key organizers present | Co-convener delivers briefing |
| 09:15 | Session 1 | Chair and AV lead | Room 1 / online link | Chair sheet, slides, timer | Speaker and connection | Offline slides or substitute discussion |
| 11:00 | Break and claim desk | Hospitality and finance staff | Foyer | Dietary labels and claim checklist | Vendor arrival | Sealed backup refreshments |
| 17:00 | Daily close | Convener, LOC and finance lead | Office | Issue log, attendance and expenditure log | Team leads | Short online reconciliation |

The desk pack contains the current programme, accepted-participant list, emergency contacts, access arrangements known to the desk, room map, transport sheet and escalation chain. Sensitive bank, passport or conduct information is kept elsewhere with authorized staff.

The session-chair sheet states the speaker's correct name and affiliation, allotted talk and question times, warning cards or signals, pronunciation, recording status, conduct reminder, AV contact and fallback plan. The rapporteur records scientific points and follow-up actions, not a transcript of private discussion.

At the end of each day, the Convener, LOC, technical and finance leads reconcile programme changes, missing participants, next-day travel, access issues, collected claims, new invoices and unresolved risks.

## 19. Certificates, materials, recording and proceedings

Different event objects must not be confused:

| Object | What it certifies or contains |
|---|---|
| Programme | Planned schedule; not proof that every session occurred |
| Attendance record | Verified presence under the stated event rule |
| Abstract book | Accepted summaries; not peer-reviewed full papers unless explicitly subjected to such a process |
| Lecture material | Slides, notes, exercises or code shared with author permission |
| Recording | Audiovisual record under explicit speaker and participant consent |
| Proceedings | Separate scholarly publication with editors, full-paper criteria, review and publisher agreement |
| Journal special issue | Journal-controlled submission and peer-review process; conference acceptance does not guarantee publication |

Certificate wording should distinguish attendance, paper or poster presentation, invited-resource-person service and organizing service. Verification comes from the attendance and session records, and corrections are entered in a certificate register. Certificates should not be issued before the activity or merely in exchange for a fee. Scheme-specific rules prevail: the Science Academies' current Lecture Workshop manual ordinarily permits an attendance letter rather than a formal participant certificate.

Recording, slides and photographs remain subject to permission. An abstract or attendance certificate is not a journal publication. Proceedings should not be advertised with an ISBN, DOI, journal title, indexing claim or “Scopus” label until a legitimate publisher has formally agreed to the exact volume and its editorial process. The [IOP proceedings author guidance](https://publishingsupport.iopscience.iop.org/author-guidelines-for-conference-proceedings/) illustrates the additional author and publication layer. For many focused theory workshops, edited lecture notes, verified notebooks and an open-problem report are more useful than rushed proceedings.

## 20. International speakers and events in India

Foreign participation creates a distinct visa and clearance workstream. Before final travel assurances are issued, the host's international office or competent authority should examine the current conference-visa, political-clearance, security-clearance and restricted/protected-area rules.

The [Ministry of Home Affairs Conference Visa FAQ](https://conference.mha.gov.in/events/FAQ.aspx) explains when prior reference to MEA and MHA is required, while the [MHA International Events portal](https://conference.mha.gov.in/) and its [organizer user manual](https://conference.mha.gov.in/events/User_Guide.pdf) describe the event-clearance system. The [MEA political-clearance portal](https://conference.epolclearance.gov.in/) handles the political-clearance process described by MHA. Requirements depend on the organizer, participant nationality, event subject and location; the live portal and relevant Ministry instructions are authoritative because a static manual may lag a portal change.

An invitation letter should state only confirmed facts and support. Foreign guests may need adequate lead time for a Conference Visa and, in specified cases, prior governmental authorization. Calling a meeting “international” also creates expectations about participant composition, review, communication and administration; it should not be used as a promotional adjective for a local event with a single online guest.

## 21. Post-event report and financial closure

Closure is part of the event, not optional work after it. The file normally contains:

1. final programme, including actual changes;
2. participant, speaker and contribution lists;
3. attendance and certificate or attendance-letter register;
4. concise account of lectures, tutorials, posters, problems and discussion;
5. feedback summary and response to significant issues;
6. consent-cleared photographs or materials;
7. outputs and follow-up actions with responsible persons and dates;
8. head-wise expenditure, source-wise income and reconciliation;
9. original or certified invoices, claims and payment records as required;
10. SoE, UC, audit or institutional certificates in the prescribed form;
11. interest and unspent-balance calculation and return or adjustment; and
12. funder acknowledgment and formal closure correspondence.

The scientific report can use this structure:

```text
Event identity, dates, venue and approved purpose
Organizers, partners and support sources
Target audience, applications, selection and actual attendance
Final academic programme and principal scientific content
Tutorials, posters, computations, discussions and participant outputs
Access, conduct or operational issues and how they were handled
Feedback evidence and learning/research outcomes
Public materials and permissions
Follow-up actions, owners and dates
Financial summary and list of submitted closure documents
```

ANRF publishes its event completion-report and UC/SoE templates on the [Seminar/Symposia page](https://www.anrfonline.in/ANRF/seminar_symposia). BRNS supplies a symposium closure form through its [downloads page](https://brns.res.in/download_forms.php). NBHM describes its conference closure record on the [conference-support page](https://nbhm.dae.gov.in/conferences.html). The sanction letter's deadline controls.

## 22. Measuring scientific success

Attendance, inauguration photographs, dignitary names and certificate counts are administrative or publicity facts. Scientific success is better described by:

- whether the intended audience and prerequisite level were reached;
- the completeness of the lecture, tutorial or problem sequence;
- quality of questions, posters and contributed work;
- solved exercises, tested code, notes or references produced;
- later correspondence between participants and speakers;
- new visits, reading groups, seminar invitations or joint calculations;
- continued participation by nearby institutions;
- repeat activity under a stable scientific theme; and
- accurate, timely financial and documentary closure.

A small event can therefore be more successful than a large conference. Scale is not a substitute for a coherent intellectual outcome.

## 23. Common failure modes and what they reveal

| Failure | Underlying control that was missing |
|---|---|
| Event announced before approval or funding | Approval gate and status-controlled publicity |
| “International conference” with no real international programme | Accurate event classification |
| Theme covers all of physics | Audience and outcome definition |
| Famous but unconfirmed speaker list | Speaker status register and consent |
| Long inauguration and continuous talks | Academic programme ownership by the SOC |
| Participant later discovers that travel is unsupported | Separate, explicit support decision |
| Fees or grant received in a personal account | Institutional account and finance authority |
| Sponsor receives a talk slot | Separation of scientific selection from sponsorship |
| No record of why abstracts were accepted | Rubric, conflict declaration and minutes |
| Projector, internet or power failure stops the event | Rehearsal, offline copy and backup plan |
| Photographs or recordings made without permission | Granular media consent |
| Certificate or abstract presented as a publication | Clear distinction among event records |
| Accounts remain open after the event | Named closure owner and deadline |

The most important practical distinction is between an **organizer manual**, a **funding guideline**, a **programme proposal** and a **sanction letter**. Reading one cannot replace the others.
