Research funding is an institutional system for paying the admissible costs of a defined programme of work. A professor may be the scientific applicant and Principal Investigator, but the grant is normally sanctioned to, received by and accounted for through the college or university. It is not personal income and it is not a discretionary bank balance belonging to the investigator.

Theoretical physics has legitimate research costs even when it does not require a large experimental laboratory. These may include research personnel, workstations, storage, high-performance or cloud computing, specialist software, books, data access, collaboration visits, scientific meetings, visitors and publication costs where the rules permit them.

<div class="note-box" markdown="1">
**A remote location is a real operating condition, but usually not an automatic grant category.** “Remote,” “rural,” “interior,” “affiliated college,” “state institution,” “Focus District” and “emerging academic research institution” have different meanings. A scheme gives preference or relaxation only when its current call defines the relevant category.
</div>

This chapter maps the funding system rather than promising that a named call is open. Its reference date is **30 June 2026**. Scheme pages, old application forms and completed-call portals often remain online after a call has closed. The current official call, guidelines, frequently asked questions and application portal together determine the operative rules.

## 1. Five different objects called “research funding”

Several unlike arrangements are often discussed as though they were one kind of grant.

| Funding object | Normal applicant | Legal or administrative recipient | What it supports |
|---|---|---|---|
| Investigator-led project | Eligible faculty member as PI, sometimes with Co-PIs | Implementing institution | A bounded scientific problem, personnel and approved research costs |
| Personal fellowship or associateship | Individual researcher | Individual or host, according to the award | Salary, stipend, visits or protected research time |
| Institutional-capacity grant | College, university, department or consortium | Institution | Shared equipment, computing, library, networking, renovation or research systems |
| Facility or collaborative-access award | Researcher with an external centre or partner | Host, home institution or both | Facility time, travel, local support or a jointly administered project |
| Travel, school or event grant | Participant, convener or institution | Individual or institution, according to the scheme | A particular journey, presentation, workshop, school or conference |

A travel award cannot be converted into a general project grant. An institutional facility is not owned privately by the faculty member who prepared the proposal. An invitation from a collaborator is not a funding sanction. A fellowship that pays for a stay at a host institution does not necessarily transfer a research budget to the home college.

## 2. The actors and their responsibilities

| Actor | Meaning in a funded project |
|---|---|
| Funder | Publishes the call, evaluates proposals, issues the sanction and releases funds |
| Principal Investigator (PI) | Holds primary scientific responsibility under the award |
| Co-Principal Investigator (Co-PI) | Has a formally approved scientific and administrative role; this is not the same as co-authorship |
| Implementing institution | Accepts the grant and its legal, financial, employment and asset obligations |
| Host institution | Supplies facilities, mentorship or a place of work; it may or may not receive part of the grant |
| Head or authorized signatory | Certifies eligibility and accepts institutional obligations |
| Finance office | Maintains ledgers, payments, financial statements, utilization records and audit documents |
| Project staff | Hold temporary appointments under the sanctioned project and institutional rules |
| Scientific collaborator | Contributes expertise without automatically becoming a Co-PI or controlling funds |
| Vendor or service provider | Supplies equipment, software, cloud computing or another approved service |

The sanction order is the controlling document after an award. The proposal explains the intended science, but the sanction order fixes the approved duration, heads of expenditure, conditions, reports and amount. Material changes in the PI, institution, project site, equipment, scope or budget may require written approval.

## 3. Direct ANRF routes for a college professor

The [Anusandhan National Research Foundation portal](https://www.anrfonline.in/ANRF/HomePage) separates current ANRF programmes from **erstwhile SERB schemes**. Direct routes relevant to theoretical physics include ARG-MATRICS, the broader Advanced Research Grant, the Prime Minister's Early Career Research Grant, the Inclusivity Research Grant and, for a highly established senior researcher, the J. C. Bose Grant.

### 3.1 ARG-MATRICS: the clearest low-capital theory route

[ARG-MATRICS](https://www.anrfonline.in/ANRF/arg4_anrf?HomePage=New) is part of the Advanced Research Grant programme and explicitly covers Mathematical Sciences, Theoretical Sciences and Quantitative Social Sciences. The 2026 framework provides **₹5 lakh per year plus overheads for up to five years**, with at most **₹5 lakh in total for international travel**.

The permitted purposes are unusually well matched to theoretical work:

- books and monographs;
- a workstation, laptop or other minor equipment;
- research software;
- travel and registration for invited talks or paper presentations;
- collaborative visits in India or abroad; and
- travel and logistics for inviting students and young researchers for research activity.

The general ARG eligibility rules apply: a PhD and a regular sanctioned or qualifying tenure-track position at a recognized Indian academic or research institution, with the required service remaining. Annual-renewal faculty, guest or ad hoc faculty, consultants, project fellows and postdoctoral researchers do not satisfy the programme's definition of a regular position. MATRICS has no Co-PI, allows one proposal per call and uses a short one-to-two-page research proposal together with publication records and institutional endorsement.

Its small fixed budget does not make it a minor scientific category. It is a deliberately low-capital grant for active theoretical work. It does not normally provide the personnel and larger computational resources available under a full project grant.

### 3.2 Advanced Research Grant (ARG)

The [Advanced Research Grant 2026 call framework](https://anrf.gov.in/assets/pdf/ARG_2026_CFP_V6_NEW.pdf) supports three classes of work: Fundamental Frontier Research, Use-Inspired Basic Research, and Applied or Translational Research. Pure theoretical physics can therefore be submitted as **fundamental frontier research**; an artificial product or immediate commercial claim is not required. The proposal must nevertheless identify a non-incremental question, rigorous method, credible outputs and longer-term scientific significance.

ARG is a call-based investigator-led programme for an individual or small team. Important structural features in the 2026 framework include:

- a maximum duration of five years, with support beyond the first three years released in performance-dependent phases;
- a maximum ceiling of ₹5 crore, while the actual budget must be realistic and justified;
- up to five Co-PIs;
- funded participation of at most three institutions;
- personnel, consumables, travel, fieldwork, contingency, software, cloud computing, publication and facility-access costs under recurring heads;
- equipment, purchased software and long-term cloud or remote-facility access under non-recurring heads; and
- a compact pre-proposal, followed by an invited full proposal and expert review, with a presentation where required.

Projects of ₹1 crore or more receive deeper scrutiny and require Co-PI participation. The framework prefers a justified PI or Co-PI role from an **emerging academic research institution** in larger projects. Its definition includes a state university or a research-active public or private academic institution below the specified NIRF overall threshold. This creates a formal multi-institution route for less-endowed institutions, but it is not a reserved grant for every affiliated or rural college.

For a theory project, the distinction from MATRICS is mainly scale and structure. ARG can support project personnel, larger computation, paid facility access and multiple funded institutions. A Co-PI must have a real technical role, accountability and a justified budget; adding a prominent name without a defined contribution conflicts with the programme framework.

### 3.3 Prime Minister's Early Career Research Grant

The [Prime Minister's Early Career Research Grant](https://www.anrfonline.in/ANRF/ecrg_anrf?HomePage=New) is a one-time, call-based grant for researchers beginning work at a new institution. Its standing conditions include:

- a PhD and a regular academic or research position at a recognized Indian institution;
- application normally within two years of joining;
- an upper age of 42, with the stated three-year relaxations;
- no Co-PI, except under the separate PIO/OCI condition;
- exclusion after receipt of specified earlier SERB or ANRF project grants; and
- support up to **₹60 lakh plus overheads for three years**.

The current programme page contains a one-time joining-date relaxation for the referenced call: joining on or after **1 February 2023** replaces the normal “within two years” condition for that call. Such a relaxation must not be assumed in a later round. The grant can support equipment, personnel, consumables, travel and contingency. International project travel and approved open-access publication expenses are permitted within the applicable heads and limits.

### 3.4 Inclusivity Research Grant

The [Inclusivity Research Grant](https://www.anrfonline.in/ANRF/inrg_anrf?HomePage=New) is an annual call-based route for an Indian researcher belonging to the Scheduled Caste or Scheduled Tribe category who holds a PhD and a regular position in an eligible academic or research institution. At least four years of service must remain, and no Co-PI is allowed. The programme provides up to **₹60 lakh plus overheads for three years** and supports personnel, equipment, recurring research costs, project travel and approved dissemination costs.

IRG is category-specific, not a general remote-college scheme. An eligible SC/ST investigator may also apply to other ANRF programmes when their separate eligibility rules are met and the proposals are genuinely distinct.

### 3.5 J. C. Bose Grant for an established senior researcher

The [ANRF J. C. Bose Grant](https://anrfonline.in/ANRF/jcbose_anrf?HomePage=New) is a highly selective, nomination-based route for an active senior scientist or engineer aged 45–65 with a sustained record of outstanding research. The Head of Institution or the President of a National Science Academy submits the nomination; it is not an ordinary self-submitted open project proposal.

The current structure provides **₹25 lakh per year for five years**, with equipment limited to 20 per cent of the total grant, plus ₹1 lakh per year as institutional overhead. The nominee must hold a regular position at a recognized Indian institution and meet the residual-service conditions, subject to the published host arrangement after superannuation. There is no Co-PI. The 2026 nomination window closed on 10 February 2026, so the programme is relevant to long-term awareness rather than a June 2026 submission.

### 3.6 Compact comparison of the direct ANRF routes

| Situation defined by the scheme | Main route | Scale | Availability model |
|---|---|---|---|
| Active researcher in theoretical or mathematical sciences with low-capital needs | ARG-MATRICS | ₹5 lakh/year plus overheads, up to 5 years | Call-based |
| Larger fundamental, use-inspired or translational project, possibly multi-institutional | ARG | Realistic budget up to ₹5 crore, up to 5 years | Call-based |
| Newly established early-career researcher meeting joining, age and prior-grant rules | PM ECRG | Up to ₹60 lakh plus overheads, 3 years | Call-based; the portal announces each window |
| Eligible SC/ST investigator with required regular service | IRG | Up to ₹60 lakh plus overheads, 3 years | Normally one call per year |
| Highly established senior researcher nominated by an authorized institution or Academy head | J. C. Bose Grant | ₹25 lakh/year for 5 years plus fixed overhead | Annual nomination call; 2026 call closed |

“Programme exists” and “application window is open” are separate facts. The dashboard and current call PDF supply the second fact.

## 4. Subject-specific direct grants

### 4.1 BRNS for work aligned with DAE programmes

The [Board of Research in Nuclear Sciences](https://brns.res.in/interest.php), Department of Atomic Energy, supports work connected to DAE programmes. Its areas include high-energy and nuclear physics, particle physics, condensed matter, fusion and plasma, accelerators, lasers, gravitational waves, astronomy, quantum sensors, quantum computing and related basic science. Subject fit is decisive; a general quantum-theory proposal does not become BRNS-relevant merely by using the word “nuclear.”

For a **Relevant Research Project**, the external PI develops the project with a **Principal Collaborator from a DAE unit**. The DAE collaborator is mandatory for ordinary project proposals. The official BRNS information page states that applications in this route are accepted throughout the year, through the BRNS portal, and that regular scientists or engineers in universities and higher-education or research institutions are eligible. The [BRNS process page](https://brns.res.in/activities.php) describes final online submission followed by two signed hard copies and supporting documents sent by Speed Post. It gives a typical research-project decision period of six to eight months and permits only one project submission at a time.

The **Young Scientist Research Project** is an exception to the mandatory DAE-collaborator rule. The published framework specifies an age ceiling of 35, a maximum of 20 projects per year and a maximum budget of ₹35 lakh for new or emerging subjects, preferably related to DAE programmes.

BRNS also publishes call-specific **Coordinated Research Projects**. A CRP joins non-DAE PIs to DAE Principal Collaborators around predefined topics and deliverables. It is not a permanently open topic-free grant: the title, participating expertise, deadline and DAE partner come from the particular call.

### 4.2 NBHM for genuinely mathematical physics

The [National Board for Higher Mathematics Research Project Grant](https://nbhm.dae.gov.in/research.html) is relevant when the central research question belongs genuinely to mathematics or mathematical physics: operator and spectral theory, rigorous quantum theory, geometry, mathematical aspects of field theory, integrable systems, probability, PDEs or mathematical quantum information are possible examples. The use of mathematical notation in a physics proposal is not by itself enough.

NBHM accepts fresh proposals in two processing cycles, with published deadlines of **30 June and 31 December**. An eligible faculty member at an Indian institution must have tenure covering the project, which may run for at most three years. The completed institutional form is submitted by email under the process on the official page.

The [NBHM mandate effective from July 2025](https://nbhm.dae.gov.in/DOCUMENTS/RP_NBHM_Mandate_2025.pdf) is important because older descriptions are no longer accurate. It discontinued JRF, SRF and RA support under these project grants. Its normal published limits include ₹25,000 in total for books or library material, a computer up to ₹1.5 lakh in total unless a larger cost is specially justified, domestic travel up to ₹60,000 per year, contingency up to ₹15,000 per year and recommended overhead up to 10 per cent. Consequently, NBHM can support a focused mathematical research programme but is not the manpower route described in older webpages or past sanctions.

### 4.3 CSIR extramural research: visible scheme, no general rolling intake

The [CSIR-HRDG extramural research framework](https://www.csirhrdg.res.in/Home/Index/1/Default/3283/63) includes regular faculty in universities, postgraduate institutions and recognized laboratories; a college normally needs a postgraduate course in the proposed area. However, the CSIR portal has retained scheme information while fresh general submissions have been suspended or restricted to specifically announced calls. It must therefore be classified as **monitor for a fresh call**, not as a continuously open grant.

This illustrates a recurring status trap: an agency can continue administering sanctioned projects, publishing guidelines and displaying a proposal portal without accepting new proposals in that category.

## 5. UGC faculty grants: listed programmes with call-dependent intake

The current [UGC Faculty Corner](https://www.ugc.gov.in/Home/faculty_Corner) lists three research-support programmes. The public programme pages do not by themselves establish a live 2026 application window; the application portal and a fresh UGC notification must also be present.

| UGC programme | Published target group | Stated support | Important eligibility structure |
|---|---|---|---|
| [Dr D. S. Kothari Research Grant for Newly Recruited Faculty](https://www.ugc.gov.in/ResearchAwards/Fac_ra1) | Newly appointed permanent Assistant Professor | ₹10 lakh for 2 years | PhD, at least five papers under the published guideline and application within two years of joining; eligible-institution rules include defined college classes |
| [Research Grant for In-Service Faculty](https://www.ugc.gov.in/ResearchAwards/Fac_ra2) | Established regular faculty | ₹10 lakh for 2 years | Age up to 50, at least 10 years of service remaining and the substantial prior supervision/project record stated in the guidelines |
| [Fellowship for Superannuated Faculty](https://www.ugc.gov.in/ResearchAwards/Fac_ra3) | Eligible Associate Professors or Professors near or after retirement | ₹50,000/month plus ₹50,000/year contingency, for the stated tenure | Age, supervision, prior-project and institution conditions apply |

The detailed guidelines for the [newly recruited faculty grant](https://frg.ugc.ac.in/download/Guidelines-Newly%20rectt%20faculty.pdf) and [in-service faculty grant](https://frg.ugc.ac.in/download/Guidelines-In%20service%20faculty.pdf) identify eligible classes that include specified constituent or affiliated colleges under section 2(f) with valid NAAC accreditation. They also impose restrictions on expenditure: the ₹10 lakh faculty grants do not fund a project fellow or project assistant, international travel or furniture under the published framework.

The in-service grant is not an entry-level substitute for an ANRF project. Its published conditions require prior supervision of five full-time PhDs and completion of at least two sponsored projects. The Kothari grant is the career-stage route, but its institutional and candidate wording must be checked against the exact new call and the college's listing in the portal.

Old UGC pages for **Minor Research Projects for College Teachers**, **Major Research Projects**, XII Plan travel grants or older Basic Scientific Research start-up grants are archival evidence, not proof of an open scheme. Current UGC Faculty Corner notices have priority over search-engine results and copied circulars.

## 6. Grants that the college or university must submit

### 6.1 DST-FIST Level A for postgraduate colleges

The [DST Fund for Improvement of S&T Infrastructure](https://dst.gov.in/node/492) is an institutional-capacity programme. For an eligible college, the proposal is from the **college as a whole** for its postgraduate science and technology departments; DST explicitly excludes an individual R&D proposal under FIST.

The current Level A framework covers active government, government-aided and private PG colleges and provides up to **₹1.50 crore for five years**. The published eligibility framework requires the relevant PG and research programme to have existed for at least five years. Eligible support includes:

- renovation of existing research space, but not a new building;
- essential equipment and modernization of facilities;
- networking and computational facilities;
- software and databases;
- scientific and technical books, but not journals; and
- maintenance or refurbishment of facilities.

This makes FIST relevant to a theory group seeking shared workstations, a computational laboratory, licensed software, networking, storage and books. The Principal, science departments, accounts office and institutional committees must assemble and endorse the college-wide package. The resulting assets are shared institutional facilities rather than the property of one PI.

### 6.2 PM-USHA and geography-sensitive college strengthening

[PM-USHA](https://pmusha.education.gov.in/) is routed through State or Union Territory higher-education systems, not through an individual-faculty application to the Ministry of Education. Its [published guidelines](https://www.education.gov.in/sites/upload_files/mhrd/files/upload_document/pm-usha_guidelines.pdf) include **Grants to Strengthen Colleges**, with a stated unit cost of ₹5 crore, and give attention in state shortlisting to Focus Districts and underserved, remote, rural, difficult-geography, low-GER and other defined areas.

The operational chain is therefore different from a PI grant:

```text
departmental research and capacity need
        ↓
college Principal / IQAC / research committee
        ↓
institutional development or project plan
        ↓
State Higher Education Council / PM-USHA nodal system
        ↓
State or UT consolidated proposal
        ↓
national appraisal and approval process
```

A professor can formulate the scientific component—computing, library, training, networking, research space and measurable departmental outcomes—but cannot submit it as a private proposal directly to the central PM-USHA portal. The published scheme cycle ran through 31 March 2026, while implementation and approved State projects may continue. Any new selection therefore depends on a current Ministry and State window; the national guideline is not a rolling faculty call.

### 6.3 State Science and Technology Councils

The [DST State Science and Technology Programme](https://dst.gov.in/state-science-technology-programme) works through State and Union Territory councils and departments, but it does not create one uniform nationwide PI scheme. Each State defines its own live calls, eligible institutions, subjects, budget and approval route. A professor at a college can apply only where the current State call expressly permits that applicant and institution class.

Many State calls emphasize location-specific technology, environment, agriculture, health, resources or societal problems. A purely formal theory proposal has no automatic fit; a genuine use-inspired mathematical or computational problem may fit when the call says so. The official State council notice, rather than a list of councils or an older sanctioned-project document, establishes availability.

### 6.4 ANRF PAIR

[PAIR](https://www.anrfonline.in/ANRF/PAIR) is a hub-and-spoke institutional programme intended to strengthen research capacity. In its published first phase, only specified classes of universities and institutions participate, and the eligible hub submits the network proposal. A standalone affiliated college professor cannot submit an individual PAIR application. Where a parent or state university is an eligible spoke, college participation depends on the university's formal network structure and the exact phase rules.

### 6.5 DST-PURSE: a university route, not a college route

[DST-PURSE](https://dst.gov.in/promotion-university-research-and-scientific-excellence-purse) supports university research capacity and expressly states that colleges are not eligible applicants. It also rejects individual R&D proposals. An affiliated-college researcher may collaborate with a supported parent university or seek access under its institutional policy, but cannot represent PURSE as a direct college-professor scheme.

The exclusion is useful knowledge: not every prominent higher-education funding programme is a plausible destination for a college application.

## 7. Access and mobility can substitute for missing local infrastructure

Some of the highest-value routes for a remote college do not transfer a large project budget to the college. They provide repeated access to a research centre, a collaborator, a facility or a concentrated programme.

### IUCAA Associateship

The [IUCAA Associateship Programme](https://www.iucaa.in/en/16-english?start=88) is designed for a faculty member at an Indian university or the postgraduate department of a college working in astronomy and astrophysics or related fields. The three-year association supports short or longer visits to IUCAA, with travel and local living support under programme rules. Applications are normally invited annually around April, but the dates belong to each round. Relevant areas can include cosmology, gravitation, gravitational waves, high-energy astrophysics, computation and quantum metrology or sensing where they connect to IUCAA activity.

An associateship is not an unrestricted cash grant. Its value is protected access to experts, computing, library resources, seminars and repeated research time. IUCAA material also describes support for some workshops or conferences organized by Associates at their home institutions, creating a possible route for bringing a scientific activity to a geographically isolated department.

### Science Academies Summer Research Fellowship and FAST-SF

The annual [Science Academies Summer Research Fellowship Programme](https://webjapps.ias.ac.in/fellowship2026/) includes college and university teachers for a roughly two-month project with an Academy-associated guide, with travel and a living fellowship under the call. It is host-supported research time, not a project grant to the college.

The 2026 [Focus Area Science Technology Summer Fellowship](https://webjapps.ias.ac.in/fastsf2026/index.html) was explicitly geography-sensitive. Its named locations included the northeastern States, Andaman and Nicobar Islands, Bihar, Chhattisgarh, Himachal Pradesh, Jharkhand, Lakshadweep, Madhya Pradesh, Jammu and Kashmir, Ladakh and Uttarakhand. That list belongs to the 2026 call and can change. It is one of the few mechanisms where place of institution is a defined selection condition rather than an informal description.

### UGC-DAE Consortium for Scientific Research

The [UGC-DAE CSR Collaborative Research Scheme](https://www.csr.res.in/CRS_project/) supports eligible permanent researchers from defined universities, affiliated colleges and recognized institutions whose proposals require Consortium or DAE experimental facilities and beamlines. Researchers receive travel and local support for facility use, while longer CRS project calls appear from time to time. This is relevant to theory–experiment, modelling–data or computation–facility projects, but it is not a general cash grant for a purely analytic calculation unrelated to those facilities.

### ICTS programme proposals

The [ICTS programme-proposal system](https://www.icts.res.in/organize-program/program_guidelines) allows faculty organizers to propose concentrated programmes in physical, mathematical and computational sciences. An approved programme is hosted and administered at ICTS with programme support; it is not funding for a conference at the home college. It can nevertheless create a working group around a subject and connect a remote institution's faculty and scholars to an international research programme.

### ICTP Associates and sabbatical routes

The [ICTP Associates Programme](https://www.ictp.it/home/associates-programme) and call-based sabbatical routes support eligible researchers from developing countries through repeated or extended visits to ICTP. These are international host-supported mobility schemes rather than Indian extramural grants. Their career-stage, country, age, visit and support rules differ by call, so a visible programme page without an announced call is not an active application window.

## 8. Travel and scientific-event support

Small, purpose-specific awards can connect a college group to the wider field even when a full project is unavailable.

| Route | Funding object | Central limitation |
|---|---|---|
| [ANRF International Travel Support](https://www.anrfonline.in/ANRF/its) | Economy travel and specified associated costs for an eligible accepted international contribution | Not an internship, personal research stay or general collaboration grant |
| [ANRF Seminar/Symposia](https://www.anrfonline.in/ANRF/seminar_symposia) | Partial institutional support for an eligible scientific event in India | Grant goes through the Head of Institution and does not fully finance the event |
| [BRNS symposia and conferences](https://brns.res.in/activities.php) | Event support in areas relevant to BRNS | Subject, institutional and application conditions apply |
| [NBHM conference support](https://nbhm.dae.gov.in/conferences.html) | National or international mathematical meeting | The event must genuinely fall within NBHM's mathematical remit |
| [NBHM travel grant](https://nbhm.dae.gov.in/travel.html) | Travel for an eligible mathematician | Advance timing, frequency and eligible-cost limits apply |
| [CSIR travel support](https://www.csirhrdg.res.in/Home/Index/1/InPage/52/14) | Partial international travel support under current terms | It is partial and distinct from the suspended general EMR intake |

ANRF International Travel Support is displayed as a continuously open route. The application normally enters the portal **60–90 days before** the event and requires an accepted original contribution or eligible invited role, together with institutional endorsement. It covers the specified economy airfare, airport tax and visa cost; under the current rules, an eligible researcher below 35 may also receive registration support up to the stated limit. It does not pay for an internship or an extended private research visit.

ANRF Seminar/Symposia is also displayed as continuously open. A regular faculty convener applies through the recognized institution **60–180 days before** an eligible two-to-five-day event. Support is partial and is released to the Head of Institution for specified domestic-participant travel, pre-conference printing and incidental costs. An event budget must therefore distinguish this award from registration income, institutional contribution and support requested from other agencies.

The [international-opportunities module]({{ '/india-international-theoretical-physics-opportunities/' | relative_url }}) describes mobility applications, while the [conference module]({{ '/theoretical-physics-conferences-schools-programmes/' | relative_url }}) distinguishes an abstract acceptance, invitation, registration waiver, accommodation offer and reimbursement. [Module 11]({{ '/organizing-theoretical-physics-events-indian-colleges/' | relative_url }}) is the organizer's institutional manual for choosing the format, constituting committees, applying for support, conducting the event and closing its scientific and financial records.

This table is not a guarantee of eligibility. It identifies the first scheme family whose legal design matches the stated situation.

| Team project requiring staff, computation or funded institutional partners | ARG | Scientific category, team roles, service and host administration |

## 10. Institutional readiness is part of eligibility

A scientifically eligible PI and a scientifically sound proposal can still fail administrative screening if the implementing institution cannot accept and account for the award. The exact checklist varies, but a college may be asked to establish some or all of the following:

| Institutional record or capacity | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| Recognition, regulatory approval, affiliation, 2(f)/12(B) or accreditation record | Different schemes define eligible institutions using different legal categories |
| Appointment order and service certificate | Establishes regular status, joining date and service remaining |
| Authorized Head-of-Institution endorsement | Commits the college to administer funds and provide facilities |
| Portal institution profile and nodal officer | Connects the PI's submission to the legal institution |
| Institutional bank and payment-system details, including PFMS where required | Allows lawful release and tracking of grant-in-aid |
| Finance officer and separate project ledger | Separates project expenditure from personal and general college accounts |
| Procurement, recruitment and travel rules | Determines how approved activities are actually purchased or appointed |
| Audit, utilization-certificate and statement-of-expenditure capacity | Supports later instalments and final settlement |
| Stock and asset register | Records workstations, servers, books and other project property |
| Space, electricity, connectivity and security commitment | Demonstrates that the approved work can operate at the college |
| Leave, on-duty and visit policy | Makes collaboration periods and conference travel administratively possible |
| University arrangement for PhD registration | Separates the project's research staff from the legal power to award a doctorate |

UGC 2(f), 12(B), NAAC accreditation and a postgraduate programme are important in some schemes but are not universal prerequisites. Conversely, a general statement that a college is government recognized does not replace the exact institutional test in a call.

For a college with little grant history, administrative readiness can be built before the application window: the identity and recognition files, authorized signatory, finance contact, grant ledger method, purchase process, institutional bank documentation and research-space certificate can already be known. This does not influence scientific selection, but it prevents the institution from discovering after selection that it cannot accept the award.

## 11. The grant lifecycle

```text
official call and scheme classification
        ↓
PI eligibility + institution eligibility + subject fit
        ↓
portal registration and institutional authorization
        ↓
scientific proposal, partner documents and budget
        ↓
Head-of-Institution endorsement and final submission
        ↓
administrative screening
        ↓
peer review, committee review or presentation
        ↓
recommendation and budget examination
        ↓
formal sanction and institutional acceptance
        ↓
release of an instalment
        ↓
recruitment, procurement, travel and scientific work
        ↓
progress report + financial statements + audit records
        ↓
later instalment, approved amendment or extension
        ↓
final scientific report, accounts, assets and closure
```

Each arrow is a change of legal or administrative status:

- **registered** does not mean eligible;
- **submitted** does not mean institutionally endorsed;
- **eligible** does not mean scientifically selected;
- **recommended** does not mean sanctioned;
- **sanctioned** does not mean that the first instalment has reached the institution;
- **funds received** does not make every proposed purchase admissible;
- **money spent** does not mean that the expenditure has been audited and accepted; and
- **project duration ended** does not mean that the grant has been financially closed.

## 12. The anatomy of a theoretical-physics proposal

The proposal is an argument that the question is important, non-trivial and answerable by the named investigator and institution. Its normal technical components are:

1. **Problem and context:** the precise unresolved question and the state of the literature.
2. **Research gap:** what is missing from known calculations, models, mathematical results or computational evidence.
3. **Objectives:** a small set of testable or assessable scientific goals rather than broad subjects.
4. **Method:** analytic derivation, approximation scheme, numerical method, symbolic computation, theorem, simulation, data comparison or a defensible combination.
5. **Preliminary basis:** earlier work, pilot calculation, relevant publications or demonstrated technical competence.
6. **Work packages and milestones:** what is expected at each stage and how later work depends on earlier results.
7. **Outputs and outcomes:** theories, validated calculations, bounds, algorithms, code, datasets, preprints, articles, trained researchers, seminars or reusable notes, as appropriate.
8. **Risk and alternatives:** what may fail mathematically or computationally and what remains scientifically meaningful.
9. **Collaborator roles:** the exact expertise, task, facility and responsibility of each formal partner.
10. **Institutional feasibility:** local facilities, external access commitments and the administrative capacity to execute the award.
11. **Budget justification:** a one-to-one connection between each cost and a work package.

Theory should not be disguised as experiment to make the budget appear larger. Nor should “no laboratory required” be written as “no resources required.” A credible theory budget follows from the method: personnel for sustained calculations, a workstation or remote compute for numerical work, software for a defined task, books for a defined body of mathematics, and travel or visitors for a defined collaboration.

ARG's Fundamental Frontier Research category is especially important in this respect. Fundamental outputs can be new theories, resolved knowledge gaps, validated hypotheses, rigorous datasets or other advances in understanding; a prototype is not the only recognized form of impact.

## 13. Budget architecture for theoretical research

| Budget head | Legitimate theoretical-physics use | Administrative issue |
|---|---|---|
| Research personnel | JRF, SRF, project associate or research assistant where the scheme permits | Temporary appointment, sanctioned emoluments and recruitment procedure |
| Equipment | Workstation, compute server, storage, networking or power protection | Capital asset, specifications, procurement, tagging and institutional ownership |
| Computing service | HPC allocation, cloud credits, remote facility access | May be recurring service or non-recurring access according to scheme definitions |
| Software | Symbolic, numerical, optimization or specialist data software | Subscription versus perpetual purchase, licence users and renewal period |
| Books and information | Monographs, technical books, approved databases | Some schemes exclude journals or cap library expenditure |
| Travel | Approved collaboration, project meeting, conference or facility visit | Domestic/international distinction, advance sanction, fare and TA/DA rules |
| Visitors and focused activity | Young-researcher visit, working meeting or workshop when explicitly allowed | Cannot be inferred from a generic contingency head |
| Publication and dissemination | Open-access fee, repository or project report where allowed | APC eligibility is scheme-specific and does not validate the journal |
| Contingency | Defined incidental research expenditure | Not a personal discretionary fund |
| Institutional overhead | Contribution to institutional administration and infrastructure | Normally controlled by the institution under the funder's rules |

Several accounting distinctions recur:

- **recurring** expenditure is consumed or paid as an ongoing service;
- **non-recurring** expenditure normally creates an asset or long-term right;
- **reappropriation** moves funds between permitted heads only with the required authority;
- **carry-forward** moves an unspent balance into a later financial period when allowed;
- a **no-cost extension** changes time, not the sanctioned total, and is not automatic;
- taxes and foreign-exchange changes do not automatically enlarge the grant; and
- an approved total budget does not override a cap on an individual head.

Quotations collected for budgeting are not necessarily the final procurement process. After sanction, the college may have to follow its purchase rules, applicable government financial rules, GeM, quotation or tender procedures, conflict-of-interest controls, acceptance testing and asset registration.

## 14. Working from a college without strong local infrastructure

Teaching-focused and geographically isolated colleges may have a heavy timetable, a small or absent research office, slow procurement, few specialist vendors, unstable power or connectivity, limited journal access, no local doctoral registration and few resident researchers in the same specialty. These are infrastructure and administrative conditions, not evidence of weaker scientific ability. A grant proposal represents them by separating what exists locally, what the institution formally commits to provide, and what will be obtained from a documented external partner.

Lack of a large local centre can be represented accurately rather than hidden. Different documents establish different levels of commitment:

| Document or relationship | What it proves |
|---|---|
| Informal scientific contact | Interest, but no enforceable access or funded role |
| Collaborator letter | Intended contribution under the terms stated in the letter |
| Facility-access letter | Availability of a named resource, possibly subject to scheduling or charges |
| Host letter | Willingness to receive the researcher under stated conditions |
| Co-PI endorsement and budget | Formal funded responsibility under a scheme that permits it |
| Memorandum of Understanding | Institutional relationship within its defined scope; not automatically a project sanction |
| Consortium agreement | Roles, funds, outputs, assets, data and governance for a multi-institution programme |

Practical infrastructure models for theoretical physics include:

- a local workstation combined with national HPC or paid cloud access;
- documented visits to a collaborator for intensive calculations;
- a distributed Git and LaTeX workspace with a shared bibliography and decision record;
- an external library or associateship arrangement;
- a formal Co-PI node receiving its own justified share of a multi-institution budget;
- an annual local workshop connected to an active collaboration; and
- external co-supervision under the affiliating university's regulations.

The [collaboration and network module]({{ '/theoretical-physics-collaboration-research-network/' | relative_url }}) describes the technical infrastructure for distributed work. Funding does not replace that infrastructure; it pays specified costs inside a relationship whose roles must already be intelligible.

## 15. Funding and PhD supervision in an affiliated college

A funded project, a project appointment and PhD registration are three separate legal objects.

- A scheme may allow a college professor to be PI even when the college cannot itself award a PhD.
- A project fellow can be appointed only if the grant contains an approved personnel post and the institution completes the required recruitment.
- Appointment to a project does not automatically admit the appointee to a doctoral programme.
- PhD registration, supervisor recognition, coursework, research advisory committees and thesis submission follow the affiliating or degree-awarding university's regulations.
- An external collaborator or project Co-PI becomes a co-supervisor only through the relevant university process.
- Salary from a project does not create automatic authorship; authorship follows actual scholarly contribution and the ethics described in the [supervision module]({{ '/phd-supervision-publication-system/' | relative_url }}).

The funded project may nevertheless create the research environment in which supervision becomes possible: a research question, computing, visits, seminars, project staff and a record of outputs. In a remote college, these elements are often more valuable than equipment purchased without an active scientific network.

## 16. Recruitment, accounts and closure

Project personnel are normally temporary employees of the host institution for the project period, not permanent employees of the funder or college. The sanction and institutional rules govern advertisement, selection committee, qualifications, emoluments, HRA, leave, travel, resignation and end-of-project consequences.

The administrative record normally contains:

- sanction and release orders;
- institutional acceptance;
- a head-wise project ledger;
- appointment and selection records;
- purchase approvals, bids or quotations, invoices and payment evidence;
- travel sanction, tickets, boarding documents and settlement;
- stock and asset entries;
- annual scientific progress reports;
- statements of expenditure and utilization certificates;
- audit records;
- records of bank interest and unspent balance where applicable;
- final scientific report and publications; and
- asset, refund and project-completion documents.

An audit asks whether the expenditure was authorized, documented, correctly classified and made during the permitted period. It does not decide whether a theorem was profound. Scientific review and financial audit are separate forms of accountability.

## 17. Schemes that are often misread

| Name found online | Correct 2026 interpretation |
|---|---|
| SERB-SURE | Historically designed for state/private universities and colleges, but displayed under **erstwhile SERB schemes**; not an open route unless ANRF publishes a fresh call or successor |
| TARE | Former mentor-and-host route for state/private university and college faculty; retained for old awards, not a current open call |
| Old MATRICS | Obsolete ₹2 lakh/year terms; current ARG-MATRICS has a different framework and amount |
| Core Research Grant or SUPRA | Erstwhile SERB programmes; current ARG is the broad active investigator-led programme, without assuming every old scheme has a one-to-one successor |
| Old UGC Minor/Major Research Project | XII Plan or archival material, not evidence of current intake |
| CSIR EMR general portal | Existing administrative framework with no current rolling fresh submission; wait for an official fresh call |
| SERB-POWER page | It describes a Level II route for women at state universities, colleges and private institutions, but its retained page is not evidence of a current ANRF call; verify a fresh official announcement before treating it as available |
| PURSE | University-only institutional scheme; an affiliated college professor cannot apply directly |
| PAIR | Institutional hub-and-spoke programme, not a personal college-faculty grant |
| UGC-DAE CRS | Facility-linked collaboration, not a generic pure-theory cash grant |
| National Quantum Mission thematic call | Call-defined consortium or deliverable route; not automatically a standing individual theory grant |

An archived page may still contain useful historical eligibility and financial terms, but those terms should not be copied into a new application plan.

## 20. The central picture

For theoretical physics in an Indian college, the funding system has no single “remote-college research grant.” It has several intersecting routes:

- **ARG-MATRICS** is the most directly designed current route for a low-capital theoretical programme;
- **ARG**, **PM ECRG** and **IRG** cover larger, career-stage or category-specific investigator-led work;
- **BRNS** serves theory aligned with DAE programmes, while **NBHM** serves genuinely mathematical physics;
- **UGC faculty programmes** remain relevant when a fresh call and the exact college eligibility are confirmed;
- **DST-FIST** and **PM-USHA** build college capacity through institutional rather than personal applications;
- **IUCAA, the Science Academies, UGC-DAE CSR, ICTS and ICTP** provide research access and mobility that can compensate for weak local infrastructure; and
- travel and event programmes connect the college to the scientific community without becoming general project grants.

The decisive facts are the scientific question, regular appointment, legal status of the institution, live-call status, remaining service, subject fit, formal partner roles and the college's capacity to receive, spend, document and close public funds. Geographic isolation affects the design of the research environment; it does not reduce the scientific standard expected by peer review.
