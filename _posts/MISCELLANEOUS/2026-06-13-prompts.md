## Daily Theoretical Physics Paper Search

Use this prompt in Scholar Labs or Google Scholar-style AI search to find papers aligned with the Theoretical Physics digest.

```text
Find recent arXiv papers in theoretical and mathematical physics that are useful for a postgraduate physics teacher working on analytic quantum mechanics.
```

```text
Prioritize papers on supersymmetric quantum mechanics, SUSY-generated potentials, superpotentials, factorization methods, shape invariance, Darboux and Crum transformations, intertwining operators, isospectral Hamiltonians, rational extensions, exceptional orthogonal polynomials, PT-symmetric quantum mechanics, non-Hermitian or pseudo-Hermitian Hamiltonians, quasi-Hermiticity, metric operators, similarity transformations, exactly solvable or quasi-exactly solvable Schrodinger systems, spectral theory, and explicit pen-and-paper calculations.
```

```text
Prefer papers that contain explicit equations, potentials, spectra, wave functions, algebraic constructions, solvable examples, or methods that can be turned into lecture notes, postgraduate projects, or self-contained derivations.
```

```text
Deprioritize or exclude experimental papers, detector or instrumentation papers, observational astronomy, data-analysis-only work, numerical-only simulations, machine-learning-only papers, and broad phenomenology without reusable analytic calculations.
```
```text
For each useful paper, explain why it is relevant, what calculation or method can be extracted, what background concepts are needed, and whether it is suitable for lecture notes, a student project, or research reading.
```

## Compact Keyword Version

```text
"SUSY QM" "PT symmetry" "Darboux transformation" "shape invariance" "similarity transformation" "exactly solvable potentials" "non-Hermitian quantum mechanics" "pseudo-Hermiticity" "superpotential"
```

## Stricter Version

```text
Find recent arXiv papers that give explicit analytic or algebraic calculations in SUSY quantum mechanics, PT-symmetric or non-Hermitian quantum mechanics, Darboux transformations, shape-invariant potentials, similarity transformations, pseudo-Hermitian Hamiltonians, and exactly solvable Schrodinger systems. Only include papers with clear pen-and-paper content: formulas, spectra, wave functions, potential construction, factorization, intertwining relations, or solvable examples. Exclude experimental, numerical-only, machine-learning-only, detector, observational, and purely data-driven papers.
```

## Output Format To Ask For

```text
Return a short ranked list. For each paper include:
1. Title and link
2. Why it matches SUSY/PT/analytic quantum mechanics
3. One calculation or technique worth studying
4. Required background vocabulary
5. Suitability: lecture note, postgraduate project, or research reading
```
