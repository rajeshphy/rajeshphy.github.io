## Daily Theoretical Physics Paper Search

Scholar Labs is designed to answer a detailed scholarly research question rather than follow instructions about formatting, ranking, classification, or reviewing “this paper.”

Use this question for discovering relevant literature:

```text
How are supersymmetric quantum mechanics, Darboux transformations, shape invariance, PT symmetry, pseudo-Hermiticity, and similarity transformations being used in recent research to construct analytically solvable quantum Hamiltonians and derive their potentials, spectra, or wave functions?
```

For a narrower search, use:

```text
What recent research uses supersymmetric quantum mechanics, Darboux transformations, or PT-symmetric and pseudo-Hermitian methods to obtain explicit analytic results for one-dimensional Schrodinger Hamiltonians?
```

## Paper-Specific Follow-Up

Replace `[PAPER TITLE]` with the complete title. Do not write “this paper.”

```text
How does the paper “[PAPER TITLE]” use supersymmetry, factorization, Darboux transformations, PT symmetry, pseudo-Hermiticity, or another analytic method to derive its main quantum-mechanical result?
```

To locate the most useful calculation, ask:

```text
What explicit derivation in “[PAPER TITLE]” best demonstrates its main analytic method, and which mathematical concepts are needed to understand that derivation?
```

## Prompt Types for Optimal Use of Scholar Labs

Scholar Labs works best when the prompt is written as a research question containing a clear topic, relationship, method, system, or comparison.

### 1. Method and Application

```text
How are Darboux transformations used to construct isospectral or rationally extended quantum potentials?
```

### 2. Comparison of Approaches

```text
How do pseudo-Hermiticity and PT symmetry differ in establishing real spectra for non-Hermitian Hamiltonians?
```

### 3. Conditions for a Result

```text
Under what conditions does a non-Hermitian Hamiltonian admit a positive-definite metric operator and a real energy spectrum?
```

### 4. Development Across the Literature

```text
How has shape invariance been extended beyond conventional supersymmetric quantum mechanics in recent research?
```

### 5. Connection Between Concepts

```text
What is the relationship between Darboux transformations, supersymmetric factorization, and exceptional orthogonal polynomials?
```

### 6. System-Specific Question

```text
Which analytic methods have been used to solve PT-symmetric one-dimensional Schrodinger potentials?
```

### 7. Teaching-Oriented Research Question

```text
Which exactly solvable quantum-mechanical models best illustrate supersymmetric factorization, partner potentials, and shape invariance?
```

### 8. Research-Gap Question

```text
What unresolved problems remain in constructing physically meaningful metric operators for non-Hermitian quantum systems?
```

## Prompt Pattern

A useful Scholar Labs question usually follows this structure:

```text
How does [method or concept] affect, explain, or produce [specific result] in [defined physical system or research area]?
```

Example:

```text
How does supersymmetric factorization produce exactly solvable partner Hamiltonians in one-dimensional quantum mechanics?
```

Avoid prompts that mainly ask Scholar Labs to:

- return a fixed number of papers;
- rank results;
- obey a rigid output format;
- classify papers as lecture notes or projects;
- analyse an unnamed “this paper”;
- perform many separate tasks in one request.

## Google Scholar Keyword Search

Use ordinary Google Scholar when exact keyword matching is more useful than a synthesized research answer:

```text
"supersymmetric quantum mechanics" OR "PT-symmetric quantum mechanics" OR "Darboux transformation" OR "shape invariant potentials" OR "pseudo-Hermitian Hamiltonian" OR "exactly solvable potentials"
```
