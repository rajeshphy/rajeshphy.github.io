These topics are suitable for M.Sc. Physics students and can be completed using Python, Jupyter Notebook, or Google Colab.

| Topic | Difficulty | Main Requirement |
|---|---|---|
| Falling body with air resistance | Very Easy | Basic mechanics |
| Damped pendulum | Easy | Oscillations and ODEs |
| Projectile motion with drag | Easy | Mechanics and vectors |
| Radioactive decay simulation | Easy | Exponential decay |
| Logistic map and chaos | Easy | Basic Python plotting |
| Heat conduction in a rod | Moderate | Finite difference method |
| Random walk and diffusion | Moderate | Probability |
| Schrödinger equation in 1D | Moderate | Quantum mechanics |
| Ising model | Moderate | Statistical mechanics |

---

## 1. Falling Body with Air Resistance

**Title:** Numerical Study of Falling Bodies with Air Resistance

**Work:**

- compare motion with and without air resistance;
- calculate terminal velocity;
- vary mass, area, and drag coefficient.

**Difficulty:** Very Easy  
**GitHub:** [Computational Physics Examples](https://github.com/mihaicodes/computational-physics)

---

## 2. Damped Pendulum

**Title:** Computational Analysis of Simple and Damped Pendulum Motion

**Work:**

- compare small-angle and nonlinear motion;
- introduce damping;
- plot displacement, velocity, and phase space.

**Difficulty:** Easy  
**GitHub:** [Computational Physics Notebooks](https://github.com/abelsr/Computational-Physics)

---

## 3. Projectile Motion with Drag

**Title:** Numerical Study of Projectile Motion with Air Resistance

**Work:**

- compare trajectories with and without drag;
- calculate range and maximum height;
- determine the optimum launch angle.

**Difficulty:** Easy  
**GitHub:** [Computational Physics Examples](https://github.com/mihaicodes/computational-physics)

---

## 4. Radioactive Decay

**Title:** Numerical and Monte Carlo Study of Radioactive Decay

**Work:**

- simulate exponential decay;
- study half-life;
- compare analytical and random-decay models.

**Difficulty:** Easy  
**GitHub:** [Computational Physics](https://github.com/CompPhysics/ComputationalPhysics)

---

## 5. Logistic Map and Chaos

**Title:** Bifurcation and Chaos in the Logistic Map

**Work:**

- generate a bifurcation diagram;
- study period doubling;
- examine sensitivity to initial conditions.

**Difficulty:** Easy  
**GitHub:** [Undergraduate Computational Physics](https://github.com/haulek/CompPhysU)

---

## 6. Heat Conduction in a Rod

**Title:** Numerical Solution of the One-Dimensional Heat Equation

**Work:**

- study temperature variation with time;
- compare different boundary conditions;
- compare materials such as copper and aluminium.

**Difficulty:** Moderate  
**GitHub:** [Computational Methods in Physics](https://github.com/monitsharma/Computational-Methods-in-Physics)

---

## 7. Random Walk and Diffusion

**Title:** Monte Carlo Simulation of Random Walk and Diffusion

**Work:**

- simulate one- and two-dimensional random walks;
- calculate mean-square displacement;
- compare simulation with diffusion theory.

**Difficulty:** Moderate  
**GitHub:** [Undergraduate Computational Physics](https://github.com/haulek/CompPhysU)

---

## 8. One-Dimensional Schrödinger Equation

**Title:** Numerical Study of Bound States in One-Dimensional Potentials

**Work:**

- calculate energy eigenvalues;
- plot wavefunctions;
- compare square-well and harmonic-oscillator potentials.

**Difficulty:** Moderate  
**GitHub:** [Computational Physics Notebooks](https://github.com/abelsr/Computational-Physics)

---

## 9. Two-Dimensional Ising Model

**Title:** Monte Carlo Simulation of the Two-Dimensional Ising Model

**Work:**

- calculate magnetisation and energy;
- study temperature dependence;
- estimate the critical temperature.

**Difficulty:** Moderate  
**GitHub:** [Computational Methods in Physics](https://github.com/monitsharma/Computational-Methods-in-Physics)
