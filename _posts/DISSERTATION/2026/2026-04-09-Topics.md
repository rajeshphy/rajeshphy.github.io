## Three-Body and N-Body Motion

![N-Body gravitational simulation](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/abelsr/Computational-Physics/main/Classical%20Mechanics/N-Body.gif)

**Title:** Numerical Simulation of Gravitational N-Body Motion

**Work:**

- simulate interacting bodies under gravity;
- study trajectories for different initial conditions;
- examine stability and energy conservation.

**Difficulty:** Moderate

## Planetary Motion and Mercury’s Orbit

![Mercury orbit simulation](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/abelsr/Computational-Physics/main/Classical%20Mechanics/Central%20Potentials/mercury.gif)

**Title:** Computational Study of Planetary Motion in a Central Force Field

**Work:**

- simulate planetary orbits;
- vary orbital velocity and initial position;
- study bound, circular, and elliptical trajectories.

**Difficulty:** Easy–Moderate  
**Notebook:** [Kepler Orbit](https://github.com/abelsr/Computational-Physics/blob/main/Classical%20Mechanics/Central%20Potentials/kepler.ipynb)

## Orbital Precession

![Orbital precession simulation](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/abelsr/Computational-Physics/main/Classical%20Mechanics/Central%20Potentials/precesion.gif)

**Title:** Numerical Study of Orbital Precession in Central Potentials

**Work:**

- reproduce a closed Kepler orbit;
- introduce a small correction to the potential;
- measure the resulting orbital precession.

**Difficulty:** Moderate  
**Notebook:** [Central-Potential Simulation](https://github.com/abelsr/Computational-Physics/blob/main/Classical%20Mechanics/Central%20Potentials/kepler.ipynb)

## Spring Pendulum

![Spring pendulum simulation](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/abelsr/Computational-Physics/main/Classical%20Mechanics/Pendulums/springed_pendulum.gif)

**Title:** Numerical Study of Coupled Oscillations in a Spring Pendulum

**Work:**

- simulate radial and angular oscillations;
- vary spring constant, mass, and initial displacement;
- study energy transfer between the two modes.

**Difficulty:** Moderate  
**Notebook:** [Spring Pendulum](https://github.com/abelsr/Computational-Physics/blob/main/Classical%20Mechanics/Pendulums/springed_pendulum.ipynb)
