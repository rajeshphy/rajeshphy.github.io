 <h2 class="act-section-heading"><span>21</span>Exit Strategy</h2>
<p class="act-essence-box investment-intro">Exit strategy decides when money leaves an investment. Selling should happen because a rule is triggered, not because emotion is loud. This chapter covers sell reasons, goal completion, allocation drift, tax-aware withdrawal, and emotional selling.</p>
<h3 class="investment-section-heading">When to sell</h3>
<p class="investment-section-story"><strong>Example:</strong> selling because a TV headline is frightening is different from selling because the goal date has arrived or the thesis has broken. A sell decision needs a rule before emotion enters.</p>
<p>Selling is justified when the goal arrives, allocation drifts, product quality changes, or original thesis fails. Selling because of noise is not strategy.</p>
<section class="investment-box investment-box-law">
  <h4>Predefine sell conditions</h4>
<ul>
  <li>Define sell rules before investing.</li>
  <li>Distinguish price fall from thesis failure.</li>
  <li>Avoid selling only due to discomfort.</li>
</ul>
</section>
<h3 class="investment-section-heading">Goal completion</h3>
<p class="investment-section-story"><strong>Example:</strong> when a house down payment goal reaches the required amount, shifting from equity to safer instruments protects the achievement. The purpose of investing is to fund life, not to maximize app numbers forever.</p>
<p>When the goal is near or complete, the portfolio should move from growth to certainty. The purpose of investing was to fund the goal, not to maximize excitement.</p>
<p><strong>Protect completed goals.</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li>Shift required money to safe liquid assets before payment date.</li>
  <li>Stop taking equity risk with money already needed.</li>
  <li>Keep surplus invested according to its own goal.</li>
</ul>
<h3 class="investment-section-heading">Asset allocation drift</h3>
<p class="investment-section-story"><strong>Example:</strong> after a strong equity rally, a portfolio meant to be balanced may become equity-heavy. Selling a portion is not pessimism; it is returning to the agreed risk budget.</p>
<section class="investment-box investment-box-definition">
<p>Drift happens when market movement changes the portfolio mix away from its target allocation.</p>
</section>
<p>A rising equity market can quietly make the portfolio riskier. Ignoring drift lets markets choose your risk level.</p>
<p><strong>Correct material drift.</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li>Compare actual allocation with target allocation.</li>
  <li>Rebalance when drift crosses your threshold.</li>
  <li>Use drift control to prevent overexposure.</li>
</ul>
<h3 class="investment-section-heading">Tax-aware withdrawal</h3>
<p class="investment-section-story"><strong>Example:</strong> redeeming across funds, dates, and holding periods can change tax outcome. Planning withdrawals before March or before a large expense can preserve more after-tax wealth.</p>
<section class="investment-box investment-box-definition">
<p>Tax-aware withdrawal plans redemption to reduce avoidable Indian tax while meeting cash needs.</p>
</section>
<p>It considers holding period, gain size, surcharge, cess, and available exemptions. Tax efficiency is useful, but liquidity and goal safety come first.</p>
<p><strong>Tax is secondary to goal safety.</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li>Check tax impact before selling.</li>
  <li>Redeem in planned stages when possible.</li>
  <li>Do not delay a necessary goal payment only for tax optimization.</li>
</ul>
<h3 class="investment-section-heading">Avoiding emotional selling</h3>
<p class="investment-section-story"><strong>Example:</strong> investors often sell after bad news and buy back after recovery, paying twice for emotion. A written exit rule protects the plan from the mood of the week.</p>
<section class="investment-box investment-box-definition">
<p>Emotional selling responds to fear, regret, or headlines instead of the investment objective.</p>
</section>
<p>A written plan lowers the chance that temporary emotion becomes permanent damage.</p>
<p><strong>Pause before non-urgent exits.</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li>Wait before making non-urgent sell decisions.</li>
  <li>Compare action with written policy.</li>
  <li>Discuss major exits with a calm, competent person if needed.</li>
</ul>
