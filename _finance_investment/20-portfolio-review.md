 <h2 class="act-section-heading"><span>20</span>Portfolio Review</h2>
<p class="act-essence-box investment-intro">Portfolio review is maintenance. It checks whether the portfolio still matches goals, risk, taxes, and life situation. This chapter covers annual review, rebalancing, SIP increases, bad-product removal, and goal progress.</p>
<h3 class="investment-section-heading">Annual review</h3>
<p class="investment-section-story"><strong>Example:</strong> one fixed review month lets an Indian household update income, goals, insurance, tax position, and portfolio values together. Review is maintenance, not a search for excitement.</p>
<section class="investment-box investment-box-definition">
<p>Annual review is a scheduled check of allocation, performance, cost, tax position, and goal progress.</p>
</section>
<p>Too frequent review creates noise; no review creates drift.</p>
<p><strong>Review on schedule.</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li>Review once a year or after major life events.</li>
  <li>Check allocation, performance, cost, and goal progress.</li>
  <li>Avoid changing products without evidence.</li>
</ul>
<h3 class="investment-section-heading">Rebalancing</h3>
<p class="investment-section-story"><strong>Example:</strong> if a 60:40 equity-debt portfolio becomes 75:25 after a rally, the investor now owns more risk than planned. Rebalancing restores the original risk level.</p>
<section class="investment-box investment-box-definition">
<p>Rebalancing restores target allocation after market movement.</p>
</section>
<section class="investment-box investment-box-theory">
  <h4>Risk reset</h4>
<p>Rebalancing forces partial selling of what rose and buying of what lagged. It is a risk-control rule, not a return-maximizing promise.</p>
</section>
<section class="investment-box investment-box-law">
  <h4>Use drift threshold</h4>
<ul>
  <li>Rebalance by calendar or drift threshold.</li>
  <li>Use new investments first to reduce tax and cost.</li>
  <li>Do not rebalance tiny deviations.</li>
</ul>
</section>
<h3 class="investment-section-heading">Increasing SIP</h3>
<p class="investment-section-story"><strong>Example:</strong> annual salary increments can raise SIPs without reducing current lifestyle. Step-up investing captures income growth before expenses absorb it.</p>
<p>Increasing SIP aligns investing with rising income and inflation. A flat SIP may become insufficient for growing goals.</p>
<p><strong>Step up with income.</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li>Increase SIP annually or after salary hikes.</li>
  <li>Link increases to goal gaps.</li>
  <li>Automate step-up where available.</li>
</ul>
<h3 class="investment-section-heading">Removing bad products</h3>
<p class="investment-section-story"><strong>Example:</strong> old insurance-investment policies, expensive regular plans, or overlapping funds may remain in a portfolio through inertia. Removing them releases capital and reduces confusion.</p>
<section class="investment-box investment-box-definition">
<p>A bad product has poor fit, high cost, unclear risk, or persistent failure against suitable alternatives.</p>
</section>
<p>Removing bad products simplifies the system. Exit decisions should be based on role and evidence, not irritation.</p>
<p><strong>Exit with reason.</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li>Identify why the product is bad before selling.</li>
  <li>Consider tax, exit load, and replacement.</li>
  <li>Do not keep a product because of sunk cost.</li>
</ul>
<h3 class="investment-section-heading">Checking goal progress</h3>
<p class="investment-section-story"><strong>Example:</strong> a child education goal should be checked against current corpus, monthly contribution, remaining years, and revised cost. Progress is a measured gap, not a feeling of comfort.</p>
<section class="investment-box investment-box-definition">
<p>Goal progress compares current corpus with required corpus at the goal date.</p>
</section>
<p>Without this measurement, investing becomes motion without navigation.</p>
<p><strong>Measure path, not mood.</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li>Update goal cost for inflation.</li>
  <li>Compare actual corpus with required path.</li>
  <li>Increase investment or reduce risk according to remaining time.</li>
</ul>
