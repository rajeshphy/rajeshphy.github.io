 <h2 class="act-section-heading"><span>12</span>Types of Mutual Funds</h2>
<p class="act-essence-box investment-intro">Mutual funds differ by what they own and what risk they carry. This chapter moves from broad categories such as equity, debt, hybrid, liquid, and index funds to tax-linked and market-cap-specific funds.</p>
<h3 class="investment-section-heading">Equity fund</h3>
<p class="investment-section-story"><strong>Example:</strong> an equity fund may hold listed Indian companies across sectors and market caps. It suits long horizons because business growth is uncertain year to year but can compound over time.</p>
<section class="investment-box investment-box-definition">
<p>An Indian equity fund invests mainly in shares of Indian companies.</p>
</section>
<p>It seeks growth through business ownership. It can be volatile, so it suits long-term goals rather than near cash needs.</p>
<p><strong>Long horizon only.</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li>Use equity funds for horizons generally above five to seven years.</li>
  <li>Diversify by market cap and strategy.</li>
  <li>Expect temporary losses.</li>
</ul>
<h3 class="investment-section-heading">Debt fund</h3>
<p class="investment-section-story"><strong>Example:</strong> a debt fund may hold treasury bills, government securities, PSU bonds, or corporate paper. It should be chosen by duration and credit quality, not by yesterday's return.</p>
<section class="investment-box investment-box-definition">
<p>An Indian debt fund invests in bonds, money-market instruments, or other lending instruments.</p>
</section>
<p>It seeks income and stability, but risk depends on credit quality, duration, liquidity, and fund mandate.</p>
<p><strong>Read credit and duration.</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li>Match debt fund duration to goal horizon.</li>
  <li>Prefer high-quality debt for safety buckets.</li>
  <li>Read portfolio quality before investing.</li>
</ul>
<h3 class="investment-section-heading">Hybrid fund</h3>
<p class="investment-section-story"><strong>Example:</strong> a hybrid fund combines equity and debt so one scheme carries both growth and stability components. It can help investors who need discipline but still must understand the underlying mix.</p>
<section class="investment-box investment-box-definition">
<p>An Indian hybrid fund combines equity and debt in one scheme.</p>
</section>
<p>It can simplify behavior but may not match every goal exactly.</p>
<p><strong>Check actual mix.</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li>Check actual equity-debt range.</li>
  <li>Use only if its mandate fits your goal.</li>
  <li>Do not assume all hybrid funds are conservative.</li>
</ul>
<h3 class="investment-section-heading">Liquid fund</h3>
<p class="investment-section-story"><strong>Example:</strong> liquid funds invest in very short maturity instruments and are often used for temporary parking. They are not a substitute for insured bank money, but they can serve planned short cash needs.</p>
<section class="investment-box investment-box-definition">
<p>Indian liquid funds invest in very short-term debt and money-market instruments.</p>
</section>
<p>They aim for liquidity and low volatility. They suit temporary parking, not high return.</p>
<p><strong>Liquidity product, not growth product.</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li>Use for emergency or short-term parking after understanding risks.</li>
  <li>Check portfolio quality.</li>
  <li>Keep instant-access bank balance too.</li>
</ul>
<h3 class="investment-section-heading">Index fund</h3>
<p class="investment-section-story"><strong>Example:</strong> a Nifty 50 or Nifty 500 index fund tries to own the index at low cost. The investor accepts market return instead of trying to select the winning manager.</p>
<section class="investment-box investment-box-definition">
<p>An index fund replicates a market index such as Nifty 50, Sensex, Nifty Next 50, or Nifty 500.</p>
</section>
<p>It accepts market return minus cost and tracking difference. It removes the need to identify winning fund managers.</p>
<section class="investment-box investment-box-law">
  <h4>Core simplicity</h4>
<ul>
  <li>Prefer low expense and low tracking error.</li>
  <li>Use broad indices for core equity allocation.</li>
  <li>Stay invested through full market cycles.</li>
</ul>
</section>
<h3 class="investment-section-heading">ELSS</h3>
<p class="investment-section-story"><strong>Example:</strong> ELSS funds provide tax benefit under the applicable Indian tax rules and carry a three-year lock-in. The tax feature should not hide the fact that it remains an equity fund.</p>
<section class="investment-box investment-box-definition">
<p>Equity Linked Savings Scheme is an Indian equity fund with tax-saving eligibility and statutory lock-in under prevailing tax rules.</p>
</section>
<p>The lock-in reduces early exit but does not reduce equity risk. It should be chosen only when tax planning and long-term equity allocation both fit.</p>
<p><strong>Tax benefit is not risk reduction.</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li>Treat ELSS as equity.</li>
  <li>Invest only if tax benefit and lock-in suit you.</li>
  <li>Avoid choosing ELSS only from last year's return.</li>
</ul>
<h3 class="investment-section-heading">Flexi-cap fund</h3>
<p class="investment-section-story"><strong>Example:</strong> a flexi-cap fund can move across large, mid, and small companies. The flexibility is useful only if the process is disciplined and the investor accepts equity volatility.</p>
<section class="investment-box investment-box-definition">
<p>Flexi-cap funds can invest across large, mid, and small companies.</p>
</section>
<p>The manager has freedom to shift market-cap exposure. This flexibility is useful only if the process is disciplined.</p>
<p><strong>Monitor style.</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li>Check portfolio distribution and style.</li>
  <li>Use as a diversified active equity option.</li>
  <li>Monitor consistency, not short-term rank.</li>
</ul>
<h3 class="investment-section-heading">Large-cap fund</h3>
<p class="investment-section-story"><strong>Example:</strong> large-cap funds hold established companies with higher liquidity and analyst coverage. They can still fall sharply, but business and trading depth are usually stronger than in smaller names.</p>
<section class="investment-box investment-box-definition">
<p>Large-cap funds invest mainly in large established companies.</p>
</section>
<p>They may be relatively stable within equity but still volatile. Because large-cap markets are well researched, active outperformance can be difficult.</p>
<p><strong>Compare with index.</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li>Compare active large-cap funds with index alternatives.</li>
  <li>Use for core equity exposure if cost and performance justify it.</li>
  <li>Do not expect debt-like stability.</li>
</ul>
<h3 class="investment-section-heading">Mid-cap fund</h3>
<p class="investment-section-story"><strong>Example:</strong> mid-cap companies may have more growth runway than large caps but weaker resilience in stress. Mid-cap funds need longer holding periods and smaller allocation sizes for many investors.</p>
<section class="investment-box investment-box-definition">
<p>Mid-cap funds invest in medium-sized companies.</p>
</section>
<p>They can grow faster but fall harder. Liquidity and business maturity risks are higher than in large caps.</p>
<p><strong>Satellite allocation.</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li>Use only for long horizons and limited allocation.</li>
  <li>Expect deeper drawdowns.</li>
  <li>Avoid buying after a strong run without allocation logic.</li>
</ul>
<h3 class="investment-section-heading">Small-cap fund</h3>
<p class="investment-section-story"><strong>Example:</strong> small-cap funds can deliver strong phases and brutal drawdowns because liquidity and business quality vary widely. They are satellite holdings, not emergency or near-goal money.</p>
<section class="investment-box investment-box-definition">
<p>Small-cap funds invest in smaller companies.</p>
</section>
<p>Upside can be high, but failure, liquidity, and valuation risks are high. They are satellites, not the foundation of a beginner portfolio.</p>
<p><strong>Small allocation for small caps.</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li>Keep allocation modest.</li>
  <li>Stay prepared for long underperformance.</li>
  <li>Use only after core portfolio is stable.</li>
</ul>
