 <h2 class="act-section-heading"><span>16</span>Reading a Mutual Fund Factsheet</h2>
<p class="act-essence-box investment-intro">A factsheet is the instrument panel of a mutual fund. The reading order matters: objective first, then portfolio, sector allocation, benchmark, rolling return, riskometer, expense ratio, and fund objective.</p>
<figure class="investment-diagram-figure">
  <img src="{{ '/assets/finance/investment-16-reading-a-mutual-fund-factsheet.png' | relative_url }}" alt="Factsheet reading path">
  <figcaption>Factsheet reading path</figcaption>
</figure>
<h3 class="investment-section-heading">Portfolio</h3>
<p class="investment-section-story"><strong>Example:</strong> a factsheet showing top holdings tells whether a fund owns banks, IT, energy, pharma, or small companies. The portfolio reveals the actual exposure behind the fund name.</p>
<section class="investment-box investment-box-definition">
<p>The portfolio shows what the Indian mutual fund owns.</p>
</section>
<p>It is the real substance behind the scheme name. Holdings reveal concentration, style, quality, and hidden risk.</p>
<p><strong>Read holdings.</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li>Check top holdings and their weights.</li>
  <li>Look for repeated exposure across your funds.</li>
  <li>Ensure holdings match the fund mandate.</li>
</ul>
<h3 class="investment-section-heading">Sector allocation</h3>
<p class="investment-section-story"><strong>Example:</strong> two flexi-cap funds can have very different sector weights, one heavy in financials and another in manufacturing. Sector allocation explains why their returns diverge in different market phases.</p>
<section class="investment-box investment-box-definition">
<p>Sector allocation shows how fund assets are spread across industries.</p>
</section>
<p>A sector tilt can help or hurt depending on cycle, valuation, and earnings.</p>
<p><strong>Check economic exposure.</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li>Compare sector weights with benchmark.</li>
  <li>Avoid accidental overexposure through multiple funds.</li>
  <li>Understand large sector deviations.</li>
</ul>
<h3 class="investment-section-heading">Benchmark return</h3>
<p class="investment-section-story"><strong>Example:</strong> if a fund returns 14 per cent while its benchmark returns 16 per cent over the same period, the investor paid active cost for underperformance. Benchmark return gives the comparison baseline.</p>
<section class="investment-box investment-box-definition">
<p>Benchmark return is the outcome delivered by the fund's reference index.</p>
</section>
<p>It indicates what a passive alternative delivered. Active performance should be judged against this, after cost and over suitable periods.</p>
<p><strong>Compare against the right index.</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li>Compare fund return with benchmark over 3, 5, and 7 years where available.</li>
  <li>Check consistency, not one lucky year.</li>
  <li>Consider passive funds if active value is absent.</li>
</ul>
<h3 class="investment-section-heading">Rolling return</h3>
<p class="investment-section-story"><strong>Example:</strong> a five-year point-to-point return can look excellent if the start date was a crash bottom. Rolling returns test many start dates and give a cleaner view of consistency.</p>
<section class="investment-box investment-box-definition">
<p>Rolling return measures return across many overlapping periods.</p>
</section>
<section class="investment-box investment-box-theory">
  <h4>Repeated sampling</h4>
<p>Rolling return reduces dependence on one start and end date. It is like sampling the system repeatedly instead of trusting one measurement.</p>
</section>
<p><strong>Prefer consistency.</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li>Prefer rolling return for judging consistency.</li>
  <li>Compare with category and benchmark.</li>
  <li>Use longer rolling periods for equity funds.</li>
</ul>
<h3 class="investment-section-heading">Riskometer</h3>
<p class="investment-section-story"><strong>Example:</strong> SEBI riskometers label scheme risk from low to very high, but the label is a starting signal, not full analysis. It tells the investor to inspect asset type, duration, credit, and volatility.</p>
<section class="investment-box investment-box-definition">
<p>Riskometer indicates the risk level assigned to a fund.</p>
</section>
<p>It is a quick warning label, not a complete diagnosis. It should be cross-checked with portfolio, duration, credit quality, and asset class.</p>
<p><strong>Do not ignore the label.</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li>Read riskometer before investing.</li>
  <li>Do not ignore high or very high risk labels.</li>
  <li>Use it as a starting filter.</li>
</ul>
<h3 class="investment-section-heading">Expense ratio</h3>
<p class="investment-section-story"><strong>Example:</strong> a factsheet expense ratio of 1.5 per cent means that cost is deducted from the scheme return. In similar funds, lower recurring cost leaves more compounding for the investor.</p>
<section class="investment-box investment-box-definition">
<p>Factsheets report the cost charged by the fund.</p>
</section>
<p>Small annual cost differences become large over long horizons because investors receive return after expenses.</p>
<p><strong>Watch cost over time.</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li>Compare direct plan expense ratios.</li>
  <li>Watch expense changes over time.</li>
  <li>Demand clear value for higher cost.</li>
</ul>
<h3 class="investment-section-heading">Fund objective</h3>
<p class="investment-section-story"><strong>Example:</strong> a fund objective may say large-cap, value, short-duration debt, or aggressive hybrid. Reading it prevents buying a product whose mandate does not match the goal.</p>
<section class="investment-box investment-box-definition">
<p>The fund objective defines what the scheme is trying to do.</p>
</section>
<p>If the objective does not fit your goal, good performance does not make it suitable.</p>
<section class="investment-box investment-box-law">
  <h4>Suitability before return</h4>
<ul>
  <li>Read the objective before checking returns.</li>
  <li>Match objective with horizon and risk need.</li>
  <li>Exit if the fund persistently behaves outside its mandate.</li>
</ul>
</section>
