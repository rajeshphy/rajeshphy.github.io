 <h2 class="act-section-heading"><span>17</span>Taxation Basics</h2>
<p class="act-essence-box investment-intro">Tax changes the return that actually compounds. This chapter introduces capital-gain language for Indian resident individuals: STCG, LTCG, taxation of equity and debt funds, ELSS lock-in, and capital gains records.</p>
<h3 class="investment-section-heading">STCG</h3>
<p class="investment-section-story"><strong>Example:</strong> selling an equity mutual fund within the short-term tax period can create short-term capital gains tax. The tax cost reduces the benefit of frequent switching.</p>
<section class="investment-box investment-box-definition">
<p>Short-Term Capital Gain is profit from selling an Indian capital asset before the Income-tax Act's long-term holding period.</p>
</section>
<p>The rate depends on asset type and current law. Tax changes net return and should be included in exit decisions.</p>
<p><strong>Avoid unnecessary short-term exits.</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li>Know the holding period for each asset.</li>
  <li>Avoid unnecessary selling that creates short-term tax.</li>
  <li>Check current rules before redemption.</li>
</ul>
<h3 class="investment-section-heading">LTCG</h3>
<p class="investment-section-story"><strong>Example:</strong> long-term capital gains rules reward holding periods differently across asset types. A resident investor should know the tax clock before redeeming, not after the sale.</p>
<section class="investment-box investment-box-definition">
<p>Long-Term Capital Gain is profit after meeting the required Indian holding period.</p>
</section>
<p>Long-term taxation may differ from STCG. Tax benefit can reward patience, but tax benefit alone should not decide investment quality.</p>
<p><strong>Plan, do not freeze.</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li>Track purchase dates and cost.</li>
  <li>Plan exits with tax impact in mind.</li>
  <li>Do not hold a bad product only to avoid tax.</li>
</ul>
<h3 class="investment-section-heading">Tax on equity funds</h3>
<p class="investment-section-story"><strong>Example:</strong> equity-oriented funds have specific Indian capital gains treatment based on holding period and current law. The post-tax return, not headline CAGR, is what the investor keeps.</p>
<p>Indian equity fund taxation depends on holding period and current law. Dividends, capital gains, surcharge, and cess may apply differently.</p>
<p><strong>Think post-tax.</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li>Check current Indian equity fund tax rules before selling.</li>
  <li>Prefer fewer unnecessary switches.</li>
  <li>Keep transaction records.</li>
</ul>
<h3 class="investment-section-heading">Tax on debt funds</h3>
<p class="investment-section-story"><strong>Example:</strong> debt fund taxation changed in recent years, making product selection and holding period more important. A debt fund must be compared with FDs and other options after tax.</p>
<p>Indian debt fund taxation depends on classification, holding period, and law applicable at investment and sale. Rules have changed over time.</p>
<section class="investment-box investment-box-law">
  <h4>Verify current rules</h4>
<ul>
  <li>Verify current debt fund taxation before investing.</li>
  <li>Compare post-tax return with FD and other options.</li>
  <li>Avoid assuming old indexation rules still apply.</li>
</ul>
</section>
<section class="investment-box investment-box-event">
  <h4>2023 - Debt fund taxation change</h4>
<p>India changed tax treatment for certain debt-oriented mutual funds bought after the specified date. The lesson is that product selection must include current law, not memory of old tax rules.</p>
</section>
<h3 class="investment-section-heading">ELSS lock-in</h3>
<p class="investment-section-story"><strong>Example:</strong> ELSS units cannot be redeemed for three years from each investment date, including each SIP instalment. The lock-in supports discipline but reduces flexibility.</p>
<section class="investment-box investment-box-definition">
<p>ELSS has a statutory lock-in period in India. Each SIP installment has its own lock-in clock.</p>
</section>
<p>The lock-in can support discipline but reduces liquidity.</p>
<p><strong>Lock-in is not safety.</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li>Use ELSS only for long-term equity allocation and tax planning.</li>
  <li>Remember every SIP installment unlocks separately.</li>
  <li>Do not put emergency money into ELSS.</li>
</ul>
<h3 class="investment-section-heading">Capital gains</h3>
<p class="investment-section-story"><strong>Example:</strong> gains arise only when an asset is sold or otherwise taxed under applicable rules, not merely because the app shows a higher value. Tax planning begins with knowing when gains are realized.</p>
<section class="investment-box investment-box-definition">
<p>Capital gain is sale value minus cost, adjusted according to Indian tax rules where applicable.</p>
</section>
<p>It is taxed when realized by sale or redemption. Unrealized gain is not spendable and usually not taxable until sale.</p>
<p><strong>Keep records.</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li>Maintain records of purchase price, date, and units.</li>
  <li>Plan redemptions across financial years if useful.</li>
  <li>Use tax awareness, not tax fear.</li>
</ul>
