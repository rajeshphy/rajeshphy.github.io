 <h2 class="act-section-heading"><span>13</span>SIP Discipline</h2>
<p class="act-essence-box investment-intro">SIP discipline converts income rhythm into investment rhythm. This chapter explains monthly investing, rupee-cost averaging, fixed-date automation, the problem of market timing, and why SIPs should usually continue during market falls.</p>
<figure class="investment-diagram-figure">
  <img src="{{ '/assets/finance/investment-13-sip-discipline.png' | relative_url }}" alt="SIP converts volatility into unit accumulation">
  <figcaption>SIP converts volatility into unit accumulation</figcaption>
</figure>
<h3 class="investment-section-heading">Monthly investing</h3>
<p class="investment-section-story"><strong>Example:</strong> a monthly SIP aligned with salary day converts investing into a recurring bill owed to the future self. The habit matters because it removes the need to decide afresh every month.</p>
<section class="investment-box investment-box-definition">
<p>Monthly SIP investing means investing a fixed or planned amount at a regular monthly frequency.</p>
</section>
<p>It converts Indian income flow into asset accumulation. The main benefit is behavior: money is invested before it is casually spent.</p>
<p><strong>Invest after income arrives.</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li>Invest soon after income arrives.</li>
  <li>Increase SIP when income rises.</li>
  <li>Keep SIP aligned with goal amounts.</li>
</ul>
<h3 class="investment-section-heading">Rupee-cost averaging</h3>
<p class="investment-section-story"><strong>Example:</strong> when an index fund NAV falls, the same SIP amount buys more units; when NAV rises, it buys fewer. The process averages entry price without requiring market prediction.</p>
<section class="investment-box investment-box-definition">
<p>Rupee-cost averaging buys more units when NAV is low and fewer units when NAV is high for the same investment amount.</p>
</section>
<p>It does not guarantee profit, but it reduces dependence on one purchase date.</p>
<p><strong>Use averaging for volatile assets.</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li>Use averaging for volatile long-term assets.</li>
  <li>Do not stop SIP during temporary falls.</li>
  <li>Remember that asset selection still matters.</li>
</ul>
<h3 class="investment-section-heading">Fixed-date investment</h3>
<p class="investment-section-story"><strong>Example:</strong> choosing the fifth of every month after salary credit prevents waiting for the perfect market level. Fixed date investing turns discipline into a calendar rule.</p>
<section class="investment-box investment-box-definition">
<p>Fixed-date investment means the SIP is scheduled on a chosen date each month.</p>
</section>
<p>Automation protects the plan from mood and market headlines.</p>
<p><strong>Automate decision timing.</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li>Choose a date just after salary credit.</li>
  <li>Maintain enough bank balance before SIP date.</li>
  <li>Review date only if cash-flow timing changes.</li>
</ul>
<h3 class="investment-section-heading">Avoiding market timing</h3>
<p class="investment-section-story"><strong>Example:</strong> many investors stopped SIPs during sharp falls and restarted only after recovery, buying fewer cheap units. Avoiding timing means the plan survives uncomfortable headlines.</p>
<p>Market timing requires predicting both exit and re-entry. Getting one right is not enough.</p>
<section class="investment-box investment-box-theory">
  <h4>Two correct predictions</h4>
<p>For most investors, time in the market beats repeated attempts to outguess the market because each timing decision creates two errors to avoid.</p>
</section>
<p><strong>Use allocation, not prediction.</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li>Use allocation, not prediction, to manage risk.</li>
  <li>Invest according to goal schedule.</li>
  <li>Avoid holding long-term cash because of fear.</li>
</ul>
<h3 class="investment-section-heading">Continuing SIP during market fall</h3>
<p class="investment-section-story"><strong>Example:</strong> during the March 2020 fall, SIP investors who continued accumulated units at lower NAVs and participated in the recovery. The value of SIP discipline appears most clearly in bad months.</p>
<p>During a fall, SIP buys more units for the same money. This is useful only if the asset remains suitable and the investor continues.</p>
<section class="investment-box investment-box-law">
  <h4>Continue if goal and product remain valid</h4>
<ul>
  <li>Continue SIP if goal horizon and fund quality remain valid.</li>
  <li>Do not redeem long-term money due to temporary loss.</li>
  <li>Use emergency fund so SIP is not interrupted by shocks.</li>
</ul>
</section>
<section class="investment-box investment-box-event">
  <h4>2020 - COVID-19 market crash</h4>
<p>Indian and global equity markets fell sharply in early 2020 and then recovered strongly over time. Investors who stopped disciplined investing during the panic often converted volatility into missed participation.</p>
</section>
