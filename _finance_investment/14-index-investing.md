 <h2 class="act-section-heading"><span>14</span>Index Investing</h2>
<p class="act-essence-box investment-intro">Index investing accepts broad market exposure instead of trying to select winners. This chapter defines the key Indian indices and vehicles, then explains passive investing, tracking error, and low-cost investing.</p>
<h3 class="investment-section-heading">Nifty 50</h3>
<p class="investment-section-story"><strong>Example:</strong> Nifty 50 represents fifty large Indian companies across important sectors. It is not the whole economy, but it gives a transparent reference for large-cap equity behavior.</p>
<section class="investment-box investment-box-definition">
<p>Nifty 50 represents fifty large Indian companies across major sectors on NSE.</p>
</section>
<p>It is a broad large-cap reference for the Indian equity market. Buying a Nifty 50 index fund means accepting this basket rather than choosing individual companies.</p>
<p><strong>Use as large-cap core.</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li>Use Nifty 50 as a simple large-cap core option.</li>
  <li>Understand that it is not the whole economy.</li>
  <li>Compare index funds by cost and tracking.</li>
</ul>
<h3 class="investment-section-heading">Sensex</h3>
<p class="investment-section-story"><strong>Example:</strong> the Sensex tracks thirty large companies on BSE and is often used in Indian headlines. It is a thermometer of market mood, not a complete portfolio by itself.</p>
<section class="investment-box investment-box-definition">
<p>Sensex tracks thirty large companies on BSE.</p>
</section>
<p>It is an older, widely followed Indian market barometer. Like any index, it is a rule-based sample, not a guarantee of return.</p>
<p><strong>Reference, not advice.</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li>Use Sensex for broad market reference.</li>
  <li>Do not treat index level as investment advice.</li>
  <li>Invest through suitable funds or ETFs if the index fits the plan.</li>
</ul>
<h3 class="investment-section-heading">Index fund</h3>
<p class="investment-section-story"><strong>Example:</strong> an investor buying a Nifty 50 index fund owns the index through a mutual fund structure. The decision is to accept market return at low cost instead of searching for a superior stock picker.</p>
<section class="investment-box investment-box-definition">
<p>An index fund holds securities to mimic an index.</p>
</section>
<p>Its success is measured by low cost, low tracking difference, and faithful replication. <strong>Prefer simple replication.</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li>Prefer simple broad-market index funds for core investing.</li>
  <li>Check expense ratio and tracking error.</li>
  <li>Hold for long horizons.</li>
</ul>
<section class="investment-box investment-box-event">
  <h4>1976 - First retail index mutual fund</h4>
<p>Vanguard launched the first retail index mutual fund in the United States. The event matters because it turned a simple idea into an investable product: accept market return at low cost.</p>
</section>
<h3 class="investment-section-heading">ETF</h3>
<p class="investment-section-story"><strong>Example:</strong> an ETF trades on the exchange like a share, so the investor needs a demat and trading account. Its price can differ slightly from NAV, especially when liquidity is weak.</p>
<section class="investment-box investment-box-definition">
<p>An Exchange Traded Fund is traded on the stock exchange like a share while tracking an index or asset basket.</p>
</section>
<p>ETF return depends not only on NAV movement but also on market liquidity and bid-ask spread.</p>
<p><strong>Trade only if you understand execution.</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li>Use ETFs only if you have a demat and understand trading.</li>
  <li>Check liquidity and tracking.</li>
  <li>Avoid buying at a large premium to NAV.</li>
</ul>
<h3 class="investment-section-heading">Passive investing</h3>
<p class="investment-section-story"><strong>Example:</strong> a passive investor admits that consistently beating the market after costs is difficult. The discipline shifts from prediction to allocation, cost control, and staying invested.</p>
<section class="investment-box investment-box-definition">
<p>Passive investing accepts market return instead of trying to beat it through selection or timing.</p>
</section>
<section class="investment-box investment-box-theory">
  <h4>Low error, low cost</h4>
<p>Passive investing is based on the difficulty of consistently selecting outperformers after cost. It is a disciplined refusal to overfit noise.</p>
</section>
<p><strong>Stay boring.</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li>Use passive funds when simplicity and cost control matter.</li>
  <li>Stay diversified and patient.</li>
  <li>Do not switch passive funds based on short-term index moves.</li>
</ul>
<h3 class="investment-section-heading">Tracking error</h3>
<p class="investment-section-story"><strong>Example:</strong> if an index rises 12 per cent and the fund delivers 11.6 per cent before investor timing effects, the difference matters. Tracking error measures how tightly the fund follows its stated index.</p>
<section class="investment-box investment-box-definition">
<p>Tracking error measures how much a fund's return deviates from its index.</p>
</section>
<p>It arises from cost, cash holding, replication limits, liquidity, and execution. Lower tracking error means closer index behavior.</p>
<p><strong>Check tracking quality.</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li>Compare tracking error among similar index funds.</li>
  <li>Prefer consistent tracking over small marketing claims.</li>
  <li>Watch tracking difference over longer periods too.</li>
</ul>
<h3 class="investment-section-heading">Low-cost investing</h3>
<p class="investment-section-story"><strong>Example:</strong> a small annual cost difference can compound into a large gap over twenty years. Low cost is powerful because it is known in advance, unlike outperformance.</p>
<p>Low cost matters because every fee is a negative return with certainty.</p>
<section class="investment-box investment-box-theory">
  <h4>Cost compounds backward</h4>
<p>Over decades, cost savings compound. When two products give similar exposure, the lower-cost one starts with a mathematical advantage.</p>
</section>
<section class="investment-box investment-box-law">
  <h4>Control friction</h4>
<ul>
  <li>Control expense ratio, transaction cost, and advisory cost.</li>
  <li>Do not pay high cost for unclear value.</li>
  <li>Let low cost support long-term discipline.</li>
</ul>
</section>
