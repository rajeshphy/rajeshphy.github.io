  <h2 class="act-section-heading"><span>53</span>Milestones: Beginner to Challenge Level</h2>
  <p class="act-essence-box">Progress should be visible. Move from following scores to explaining context, then to forming grounded opinions.</p>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Why This Chapter Matters</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>Progress should be visible. Move from following scores to explaining context, then to forming grounded opinions.</p>
    <p>This chapter gives you the background that childhood sports followers usually collect slowly through repeated matches, arguments, highlights, and newspaper reports. Read it as a foundation, then attach the ideas to real sports news using the source links below.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Core Material</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>Beginner level: you can identify sport, format, teams, score, and winner. Early intermediate: you can explain table consequence, player role, and one turning point.</p>
    <p>Intermediate level: you can read a scorecard, follow live scores, understand rankings, and compare two players by role. Challenge level: you can make a reasoned opinion and revise it after evidence.</p>
    <p>Do not compare yourself with someone who watched since childhood. Compare today&#x27;s clarity with last month&#x27;s confusion. That is real progress.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Worked Example</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>Suppose a headline uses this topic but gives very little background. Do not begin by arguing. First identify the event and competition. Second identify the role of the main player, team, number, or condition. Third explain the consequence in one calm sentence. In this chapter, the first words to watch are beginner, intermediate, challenge level.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Common Mistakes</h3>
  <section class="investment-box investment-box-event">
    <ul>
      <li>Reading only the headline and missing the match context.</li>
      <li>Using a term in conversation before connecting it to a real score, table, player, or event.</li>
      <li>Treating one clip, one statistic, or one fan opinion as the full story.</li>
    </ul>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Vocabulary Bank</h3>
  <section class="investment-box investment-box-definition">
    <ul>
      <li><strong>beginner</strong>: can follow result and basic terms</li>
      <li><strong>intermediate</strong>: can explain context and roles</li>
      <li><strong>challenge level</strong>: can debate with evidence</li>
      <li><strong>grounded opinion</strong>: opinion based on reasons</li>
      <li><strong>self-check</strong>: method to measure progress</li>
      <li><strong>revision</strong>: changing view after new evidence</li>
    </ul>
  </section>
