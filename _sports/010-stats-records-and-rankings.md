  <h2 class="act-section-heading"><span>10</span>Stats, Records, and Rankings</h2>
  <p class="act-essence-box">Statistics are useful only with context. A number without role, format, venue, opponent, and sample size can mislead.</p>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Why This Chapter Matters</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>Statistics are useful only with context. A number without role, format, venue, opponent, and sample size can mislead.</p>
    <p>This chapter gives you the background that childhood sports followers usually collect slowly through repeated matches, arguments, highlights, and newspaper reports. Read it as a foundation, then attach the ideas to real sports news using the source links below.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Core Material</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>Separate career stats from current stats. Career average tells long-term level; recent form tells current readiness. In cricket, average and strike rate must be read with format. In football, goals must be read with position, minutes, and team style.</p>
    <p>Records compare history; rankings compare current position. A record can be permanent for years, while rankings move regularly. Do not treat a ranking as destiny.</p>
    <p>A careful sentence sounds like this: &#x27;The number is strong, but we should check the opposition, venue, format, and sample size.&#x27; That one sentence protects you from bluffing.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Worked Example</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>Suppose a headline uses this topic but gives very little background. Do not begin by arguing. First identify the event and competition. Second identify the role of the main player, team, number, or condition. Third explain the consequence in one calm sentence. In this chapter, the first words to watch are average, strike rate, sample size.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Common Mistakes</h3>
  <section class="investment-box investment-box-event">
    <ul>
      <li>Reading only the headline and missing the match context.</li>
      <li>Using a term in conversation before connecting it to a real score, table, player, or event.</li>
      <li>Treating one clip, one statistic, or one fan opinion as the full story.</li>
    </ul>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Vocabulary Bank</h3>
  <section class="investment-box investment-box-definition">
    <ul>
      <li><strong>average</strong>: mean performance over matches or attempts</li>
      <li><strong>strike rate</strong>: scoring speed in cricket</li>
      <li><strong>sample size</strong>: amount of data behind a claim</li>
      <li><strong>record</strong>: historical best or notable achievement</li>
      <li><strong>ranking</strong>: ordered current position</li>
      <li><strong>context</strong>: conditions that explain the number</li>
    </ul>
  </section>
