  <h2 class="act-section-heading"><span>14</span>World Tournament Calendar</h2>
  <p class="act-essence-box">Sports become clearer when you know which events are annual, which are seasonal, and which come in multi-year cycles.</p>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Why This Chapter Matters</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>Sports become clearer when you know which events are annual, which are seasonal, and which come in multi-year cycles.</p>
    <p>This chapter gives you the background that childhood sports followers usually collect slowly through repeated matches, arguments, highlights, and newspaper reports. Read it as a foundation, then attach the ideas to real sports news using the source links below.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Core Material</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>Cricket rotates through bilateral series, ICC tournaments, domestic leagues, and franchise tournaments. Football has domestic leagues, continental club tournaments, World Cup qualifiers, continental championships, and the FIFA World Cup cycle.</p>
    <p>Olympic sports often run through world championships, continental championships, ranking events, qualifying events, and the Olympic Games. Tennis has weekly tours plus four Grand Slams. F1 has a season of Grand Prix races.</p>
    <p>Keep a living calendar. It does not need every match. It needs the next major event, current ranking event, and the tournament that decides qualification.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Worked Example</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>Suppose a headline uses this topic but gives very little background. Do not begin by arguing. First identify the event and competition. Second identify the role of the main player, team, number, or condition. Third explain the consequence in one calm sentence. In this chapter, the first words to watch are season, tour, qualifier.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Common Mistakes</h3>
  <section class="investment-box investment-box-event">
    <ul>
      <li>Reading only the headline and missing the match context.</li>
      <li>Using a term in conversation before connecting it to a real score, table, player, or event.</li>
      <li>Treating one clip, one statistic, or one fan opinion as the full story.</li>
    </ul>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Vocabulary Bank</h3>
  <section class="investment-box investment-box-definition">
    <ul>
      <li><strong>season</strong>: regular competition period</li>
      <li><strong>tour</strong>: series of events across locations</li>
      <li><strong>qualifier</strong>: event that grants entry to a bigger event</li>
      <li><strong>world championship</strong>: global title event for a sport</li>
      <li><strong>continental event</strong>: event for a region such as Asia or Europe</li>
      <li><strong>cycle</strong>: multi-year rhythm leading to a major event</li>
    </ul>
  </section>
