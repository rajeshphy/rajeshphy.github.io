  <h2 class="act-section-heading"><span>45</span>How to Follow Live Scores</h2>
  <p class="act-essence-box">Live scores teach match movement. Learn what to watch by sport: score, time, wickets, rate, possession, cards, games, sets, and standings.</p>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Why This Chapter Matters</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>Live scores teach match movement. Learn what to watch by sport: score, time, wickets, rate, possession, cards, games, sets, and standings.</p>
    <p>This chapter gives you the background that childhood sports followers usually collect slowly through repeated matches, arguments, highlights, and newspaper reports. Read it as a foundation, then attach the ideas to real sports news using the source links below.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Core Material</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>In cricket, watch runs, wickets, overs, run rate, required rate, batter balls faced, bowler economy, and partnership. In football, watch minute, score, cards, substitutions, possession, shots, and table impact.</p>
    <p>In tennis and badminton, watch set score, game score, break points, match points, and momentum. In athletics, watch heat, lane, time, distance, and qualification status.</p>
    <p>Live text commentary is useful because it explains moments. Do not only stare at numbers. Read short comments around wickets, goals, injuries, and tactical changes.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Worked Example</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>Suppose a headline uses this topic but gives very little background. Do not begin by arguing. First identify the event and competition. Second identify the role of the main player, team, number, or condition. Third explain the consequence in one calm sentence. In this chapter, the first words to watch are live score, score ticker, possession.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Common Mistakes</h3>
  <section class="investment-box investment-box-event">
    <ul>
      <li>Reading only the headline and missing the match context.</li>
      <li>Using a term in conversation before connecting it to a real score, table, player, or event.</li>
      <li>Treating one clip, one statistic, or one fan opinion as the full story.</li>
    </ul>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Vocabulary Bank</h3>
  <section class="investment-box investment-box-definition">
    <ul>
      <li><strong>live score</strong>: real-time match score</li>
      <li><strong>score ticker</strong>: rapid score update line</li>
      <li><strong>possession</strong>: football measure of ball control time</li>
      <li><strong>required rate</strong>: cricket chase rate needed</li>
      <li><strong>match point</strong>: point that can end a match</li>
      <li><strong>qualification status</strong>: whether athlete or team advances</li>
    </ul>
  </section>
