  <h2 class="act-section-heading"><span>46</span>How to Read Statistics Without Bluffing</h2>
  <p class="act-essence-box">Good sports conversation uses statistics carefully. The safest habit is number plus context.</p>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Why This Chapter Matters</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>Good sports conversation uses statistics carefully. The safest habit is number plus context.</p>
    <p>This chapter gives you the background that childhood sports followers usually collect slowly through repeated matches, arguments, highlights, and newspaper reports. Read it as a foundation, then attach the ideas to real sports news using the source links below.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Core Material</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>Every statistic needs a frame: format, opponent, venue, sample size, role, and period. A T20 strike rate, Test average, football goal total, or tennis ranking means little without frame.</p>
    <p>Beware of cherry-picking. A fan may choose one stat that supports a player and ignore others. Ask what the stat excludes.</p>
    <p>Use cautious language. Say &#x27;This suggests...&#x27; or &#x27;In this format...&#x27; instead of pretending a number proves everything.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Worked Example</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>Suppose a headline uses this topic but gives very little background. Do not begin by arguing. First identify the event and competition. Second identify the role of the main player, team, number, or condition. Third explain the consequence in one calm sentence. In this chapter, the first words to watch are cherry-picking, sample, per match.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Common Mistakes</h3>
  <section class="investment-box investment-box-event">
    <ul>
      <li>Reading only the headline and missing the match context.</li>
      <li>Using a term in conversation before connecting it to a real score, table, player, or event.</li>
      <li>Treating one clip, one statistic, or one fan opinion as the full story.</li>
    </ul>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Vocabulary Bank</h3>
  <section class="investment-box investment-box-definition">
    <ul>
      <li><strong>cherry-picking</strong>: choosing only helpful data</li>
      <li><strong>sample</strong>: data being measured</li>
      <li><strong>per match</strong>: average by match</li>
      <li><strong>per minute</strong>: football or basketball rate adjusted by playing time</li>
      <li><strong>split</strong>: stat separated by condition</li>
      <li><strong>trend</strong>: direction over time</li>
    </ul>
  </section>
