  <h2 class="act-section-heading"><span>25</span>League Table and Points</h2>
  <p class="act-essence-box">Football league tables tell a season story: wins, draws, losses, goals, form, qualification, and relegation.</p>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Why This Chapter Matters</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>Football league tables tell a season story: wins, draws, losses, goals, form, qualification, and relegation.</p>
    <p>This chapter gives you the background that childhood sports followers usually collect slowly through repeated matches, arguments, highlights, and newspaper reports. Read it as a foundation, then attach the ideas to real sports news using the source links below.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Core Material</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>Most football leagues give three points for a win, one for a draw, and zero for a loss. Goal difference breaks ties when points are equal.</p>
    <p>Look at matches played. A team with games in hand may rise later. Look at goals for and goals against. A high-scoring team with weak defense tells a different story from a low-scoring team with many clean sheets.</p>
    <p>Table regions matter: title race, continental qualification, mid-table, and relegation battle. Every match means different things depending on region.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Vocabulary Bank</h3>
  <section class="investment-box investment-box-definition">
    <ul>
      <li><strong>points</strong>: league score earned from match results</li>
      <li><strong>draw</strong>: match ending level</li>
      <li><strong>relegation</strong>: drop to lower division</li>
      <li><strong>promotion</strong>: rise to higher division</li>
      <li><strong>clean sheet</strong>: no goals conceded</li>
      <li><strong>goal difference</strong>: goals scored minus goals conceded</li>
    </ul>
  </section>
