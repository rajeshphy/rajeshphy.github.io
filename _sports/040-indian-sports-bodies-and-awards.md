  <h2 class="act-section-heading"><span>40</span>Indian Sports Bodies and Awards</h2>
  <p class="act-essence-box">Indian sports news often mentions federations, trials, selection, camps, awards, and government bodies. These words explain the system around athletes.</p>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Why This Chapter Matters</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>Indian sports news often mentions federations, trials, selection, camps, awards, and government bodies. These words explain the system around athletes.</p>
    <p>This chapter gives you the background that childhood sports followers usually collect slowly through repeated matches, arguments, highlights, and newspaper reports. Read it as a foundation, then attach the ideas to real sports news using the source links below.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Core Material</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>A federation governs a sport. A selection trial tests athletes for teams. A national camp prepares selected players. A sports ministry or authority may fund schemes, awards, and training systems.</p>
    <p>Awards such as Arjuna Award, Major Dhyan Chand Khel Ratna, Dronacharya Award, and Padma awards appear in athlete biographies. They show recognition, not current form.</p>
    <p>Separate athlete performance from administrative news. A selection dispute, federation election, or funding problem is different from match performance.</p>
  </section>
