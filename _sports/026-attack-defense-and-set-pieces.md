  <h2 class="act-section-heading"><span>26</span>Attack, Defense, and Set Pieces</h2>
  <p class="act-essence-box">Football reports often describe how goals were created or prevented: attack pattern, defensive shape, transition, and set piece.</p>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Why This Chapter Matters</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>Football reports often describe how goals were created or prevented: attack pattern, defensive shape, transition, and set piece.</p>
    <p>This chapter gives you the background that childhood sports followers usually collect slowly through repeated matches, arguments, highlights, and newspaper reports. Read it as a foundation, then attach the ideas to real sports news using the source links below.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Core Material</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>Attack can come through possession, counter-attack, crosses, through balls, dribbling, or pressing mistakes. Defense can be high press, mid-block, low block, man-marking, or zonal marking.</p>
    <p>Set pieces are restarts: corners, free kicks, throw-ins, and penalties. Many matches are decided by set-piece quality rather than open play.</p>
    <p>A useful goal description has four parts: build-up, final pass, finish, and defensive error. This makes you sound analytical without pretending to know everything.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Worked Example</h3>

Second identify the role of the main player, team, number, or condition. Third explain the consequence in one calm sentence. In this chapter, the first words to watch are press, block, transition.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Vocabulary Bank</h3>
  <section class="investment-box investment-box-definition">
    <ul>
      <li><strong>press</strong>: team pressure on opponent with ball</li>
      <li><strong>block</strong>: defensive shape without pressing high</li>
      <li><strong>transition</strong>: change from defense to attack or reverse</li>
      <li><strong>corner</strong>: attacking restart from corner area</li>
      <li><strong>free kick</strong>: restart after foul</li>
      <li><strong>through ball</strong>: pass behind defense for attacker</li>
    </ul>
  </section>
