  <h2 class="act-section-heading"><span>19</span>Bowling Vocabulary</h2>
  <p class="act-essence-box">Bowling language explains how runs are controlled and wickets are created.</p>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Why This Chapter Matters</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>Bowling language explains how runs are controlled and wickets are created.</p>
    <p>This chapter gives you the background that childhood sports followers usually collect slowly through repeated matches, arguments, highlights, and newspaper reports. Read it as a foundation, then attach the ideas to real sports news using the source links below.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Core Material</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>Fast bowling uses pace, bounce, swing, seam, line, and length. Spin bowling uses turn, flight, drift, dip, and variation. A bowler can attack wickets or contain runs.</p>
    <p>An over is six legal balls. A spell is a sequence of overs by one bowler. Economy rate tells runs conceded per over. Wickets tell success, but economy and pressure often create wickets for someone else.</p>
    <p>Death bowling is a special skill in limited-overs cricket. Yorkers, slower balls, wide lines, and field placement become crucial when batters attack every ball.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Worked Example</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>Suppose a headline uses this topic but gives very little background. Do not begin by arguing. First identify the event and competition. Second identify the role of the main player, team, number, or condition. Third explain the consequence in one calm sentence. In this chapter, the first words to watch are line, length, swing.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Vocabulary Bank</h3>
  <section class="investment-box investment-box-definition">
    <ul>
      <li><strong>line</strong>: direction of the ball toward off, middle, or leg</li>
      <li><strong>length</strong>: where the ball pitches</li>
      <li><strong>swing</strong>: movement in the air</li>
      <li><strong>seam</strong>: movement after pitching</li>
      <li><strong>economy</strong>: runs conceded per over</li>
      <li><strong>yorker</strong>: ball aimed near the batter&#x27;s feet</li>
    </ul>
  </section>
