  <h2 class="act-section-heading"><span>22</span>Domestic Indian Cricket and Jharkhand</h2>
  <p class="act-essence-box">Domestic cricket is the pathway beneath international cricket. It connects state teams, local grounds, selectors, and national opportunity.</p>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Why This Chapter Matters</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>Domestic cricket is the pathway beneath international cricket. It connects state teams, local grounds, selectors, and national opportunity.</p>
    <p>This chapter gives you the background that childhood sports followers usually collect slowly through repeated matches, arguments, highlights, and newspaper reports. Read it as a foundation, then attach the ideas to real sports news using the source links below.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Core Material</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>India&#x27;s domestic structure includes red-ball cricket, one-day cricket, T20 cricket, age-group tournaments, and zonal or developmental cricket. Ranji Trophy is the major first-class red-ball tournament.</p>
    <p>Jharkhand cricket matters because state-level performance can lead to IPL attention, India A selection, and national discussion. MS Dhoni&#x27;s story is the best-known Ranchi-to-world cricket reference.</p>
    <p>When following domestic news, learn names of teams, tournament format, top run-scorers, wicket-takers, and whether a player has a role useful for higher levels.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Vocabulary Bank</h3>
  <section class="investment-box investment-box-definition">
    <ul>
      <li><strong>first-class cricket</strong>: multi-day red-ball domestic or international cricket</li>
      <li><strong>List A</strong>: domestic one-day cricket</li>
      <li><strong>state team</strong>: team representing a state association</li>
      <li><strong>selector</strong>: person involved in choosing squads</li>
      <li><strong>India A</strong>: developmental national side below senior India</li>
      <li><strong>age-group</strong>: junior categories such as under-19</li>
