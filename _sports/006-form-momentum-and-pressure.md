  <h2 class="act-section-heading"><span>6</span>Form, Momentum, and Pressure</h2>
  <p class="act-essence-box">These three words appear everywhere. Form is recent trend, momentum is current control, and pressure is the mental difficulty of performing when consequences are high.</p>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Why This Chapter Matters</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>These three words appear everywhere. Form is recent trend, momentum is current control, and pressure is the mental difficulty of performing when consequences are high.</p>
    <p>This chapter gives you the background that childhood sports followers usually collect slowly through repeated matches, arguments, highlights, and newspaper reports. Read it as a foundation, then attach the ideas to real sports news using the source links below.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Core Material</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>Form belongs to a period: last five matches, recent series, current season, or latest tournament. A player can have great career reputation but poor current form.</p>
    <p>Momentum belongs to the match. A team can start badly, then gain momentum through wickets, goals, rallies, or aggressive field position. Momentum can shift quickly.</p>
    <p>Pressure belongs to the situation. A World Cup knockout, a final over chase, a penalty shootout, a match point, or an Olympic final creates pressure because every action is magnified.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Worked Example</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>Suppose a headline uses this topic but gives very little background. Do not begin by arguing. First identify the event and competition. Second identify the role of the main player, team, number, or condition. Third explain the consequence in one calm sentence. In this chapter, the first words to watch are form, momentum, pressure.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Vocabulary Bank</h3>
  <section class="investment-box investment-box-definition">
    <ul>
      <li><strong>form</strong>: recent performance trend</li>
      <li><strong>momentum</strong>: current direction of control</li>
      <li><strong>pressure</strong>: difficulty created by high consequence</li>
      <li><strong>comeback</strong>: recovery after falling behind</li>
      <li><strong>choke</strong>: failure under pressure after being in control</li>
      <li><strong>clutch</strong>: good performance at a decisive moment</li>
    </ul>
  </section>
