  <h2 class="act-section-heading"><span>28</span>International Football Tournaments</h2>
  <p class="act-essence-box">International football is built around national teams, qualification paths, continental tournaments, and World Cup cycles.</p>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Why This Chapter Matters</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>International football is built around national teams, qualification paths, continental tournaments, and World Cup cycles.</p>
    <p>This chapter gives you the background that childhood sports followers usually collect slowly through repeated matches, arguments, highlights, and newspaper reports. Read it as a foundation, then attach the ideas to real sports news using the source links below.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Core Material</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>National teams do not train together like clubs. They meet during international windows, so chemistry and selection become important. Squad announcement is a major news event.</p>
    <p>Qualification matters. A country may play a long path before reaching the World Cup or continental championship. Group tables, goal difference, playoffs, and seeding are central.</p>
    <p>Penalty shootouts, knockout pressure, and national identity make international football emotionally intense even for neutral fans.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Vocabulary Bank</h3>
  <section class="investment-box investment-box-definition">
    <ul>
      <li><strong>international window</strong>: period reserved for national team matches</li>
      <li><strong>qualifier</strong>: match deciding tournament entry</li>
      <li><strong>playoff</strong>: extra qualification tie</li>
      <li><strong>friendly</strong>: non-competitive international match</li>
      <li><strong>caps</strong>: international appearances</li>
      <li><strong>shootout</strong>: penalty kicks used to decide a tied knockout match</li>
    </ul>
  </section>
