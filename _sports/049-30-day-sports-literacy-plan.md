  <h2 class="act-section-heading"><span>49</span>30-Day Sports Literacy Plan</h2>
  <p class="act-essence-box">A month is enough to become conversational if the plan is small, repeated, and structured.</p>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Why This Chapter Matters</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>A month is enough to become conversational if the plan is small, repeated, and structured.</p>
    <p>This chapter gives you the background that childhood sports followers usually collect slowly through repeated matches, arguments, highlights, and newspaper reports. Read it as a foundation, then attach the ideas to real sports news using the source links below.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Core Material</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>Days 1-7: universal vocabulary, one headline scan daily, and one scorecard or table. Days 8-16: cricket formats, batting, bowling, scorecards, and India fixtures. Days 17-22: football rules, tables, goals, and international tournaments.</p>
    <p>Days 23-27: Olympic sports, especially badminton, hockey, athletics, archery, and shooting. Days 28-30: legends, debate templates, and one spoken summary per day.</p>
    <p>Keep proof of learning. Your notebook should show 100 terms, 10 match summaries, 5 player profiles, and 3 opinions by the end.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Worked Example</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>Suppose a headline uses this topic but gives very little background. Do not begin by arguing. First identify the event and competition. Second identify the role of the main player, team, number, or condition. Third explain the consequence in one calm sentence. In this chapter, the first words to watch are daily block, review day, spoken summary.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Common Mistakes</h3>
  <section class="investment-box investment-box-event">
    <ul>
      <li>Reading only the headline and missing the match context.</li>
      <li>Using a term in conversation before connecting it to a real score, table, player, or event.</li>
      <li>Treating one clip, one statistic, or one fan opinion as the full story.</li>
    </ul>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Vocabulary Bank</h3>
  <section class="investment-box investment-box-definition">
    <ul>
      <li><strong>daily block</strong>: fixed small study period</li>
      <li><strong>review day</strong>: day used to revise previous notes</li>
      <li><strong>spoken summary</strong>: short explanation said aloud</li>
      <li><strong>match file</strong>: one-page summary of a match</li>
      <li><strong>player profile</strong>: role, sport, country, skill, and source</li>
      <li><strong>learning proof</strong>: visible output showing progress</li>
    </ul>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Where To Learn This</h3>
  <section class="investment-box investment-box-law">
    <ul>
      <li><a href="https://www.espncricinfo.com/">ESPNcricinfo</a>: daily cricket scorecards and reports for practice.</li>
      <li><a href="https://www.bbc.com/sport">BBC Sport</a>: short international reports written in accessible news language.</li>
      <li><a href="https://olympics.com/en/">Olympics</a>: multi-sport explainers and athlete background.</li>
    </ul>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Study Path</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>Read this chapter once for meaning, then open one source below and find a real example of the topic. Add three notes to your sports notebook: one vocabulary word, one sentence of context, and one question for later.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Practice</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>Start today with day 1: five headlines, five terms, and one paragraph on why a result mattered.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Chapter Takeaway</h3>
  <section class="investment-box investment-box-theory">
    <p>The chapter is complete when you can explain the topic in your own words and connect it to one real match, athlete, table, ranking, or news report.</p>
  </section>
