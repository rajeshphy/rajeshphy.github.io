  <h2 class="act-section-heading"><span>9</span>Tactics, Strategy, and Commentary</h2>
  <p class="act-essence-box">Tactics are match-level choices; strategy is the longer plan. Commentary names these choices in real time.</p>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Why This Chapter Matters</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>Tactics are match-level choices; strategy is the longer plan. Commentary names these choices in real time.</p>
    <p>This chapter gives you the background that childhood sports followers usually collect slowly through repeated matches, arguments, highlights, and newspaper reports. Read it as a foundation, then attach the ideas to real sports news using the source links below.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Core Material</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>A cricket captain may attack with slips, defend with boundary riders, use spin to slow scoring, or save a bowler for death overs. A football coach may press high, sit in a low block, counter-attack, or overload one wing.</p>
    <p>Strategy is wider. A team may build for a World Cup, rotate in a long season, invest in young players, or prepare for specific overseas conditions.</p>
    <p>Good commentary words are clues: tempo, shape, line, length, transition, overload, field, angle, match-up, and control. Pause after hearing one and ask what decision it describes.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Worked Example</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>Suppose a headline uses this topic but gives very little background. Do not begin by arguing. First identify the event and competition. Second identify the role of the main player, team, number, or condition. Third explain the consequence in one calm sentence. In this chapter, the first words to watch are tactics, strategy, tempo.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Common Mistakes</h3>
  <section class="investment-box investment-box-event">
    <ul>
      <li>Reading only the headline and missing the match context.</li>
      <li>Using a term in conversation before connecting it to a real score, table, player, or event.</li>
      <li>Treating one clip, one statistic, or one fan opinion as the full story.</li>
    </ul>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Vocabulary Bank</h3>
  <section class="investment-box investment-box-definition">
    <ul>
      <li><strong>tactics</strong>: choices used inside a match</li>
      <li><strong>strategy</strong>: larger plan across many matches</li>
      <li><strong>tempo</strong>: speed and rhythm of play</li>
      <li><strong>shape</strong>: team structure on the field</li>
      <li><strong>match-up</strong>: specific contest between player types</li>
      <li><strong>control</strong>: ability to decide the pattern of play</li>
    </ul>
  </section>
