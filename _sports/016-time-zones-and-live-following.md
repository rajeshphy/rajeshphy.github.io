  <h2 class="act-section-heading"><span>16</span>Time Zones and Live Following</h2>
  <p class="act-essence-box">International sports often happens outside Indian waking hours. A smart follow-up routine keeps you updated without watching everything live.</p>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Why This Chapter Matters</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>International sports often happens outside Indian waking hours. A smart follow-up routine keeps you updated without watching everything live.</p>
    <p>This chapter gives you the background that childhood sports followers usually collect slowly through repeated matches, arguments, highlights, and newspaper reports. Read it as a foundation, then attach the ideas to real sports news using the source links below.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Core Material</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>Use three modes: live, delayed, and summary. Watch live only for major matches. For ordinary events, read score first, then report, then highlights. For very small events, a table or official result may be enough.</p>
    <p>Avoid opening social media first after you miss a match. It gives emotion before structure. Open the scorecard or official result first.</p>
    <p>If a match is in Australia, Europe, or America, note Indian Standard Time before the event. This prevents missing finals, tosses, starts, or qualification rounds.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Worked Example</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>Suppose a headline uses this topic but gives very little background. Do not begin by arguing. First identify the event and competition. Second identify the role of the main player, team, number, or condition. Third explain the consequence in one calm sentence. In this chapter, the first words to watch are IST, delayed viewing, spoiler.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Common Mistakes</h3>
  <section class="investment-box investment-box-event">
    <ul>
      <li>Reading only the headline and missing the match context.</li>
      <li>Using a term in conversation before connecting it to a real score, table, player, or event.</li>
      <li>Treating one clip, one statistic, or one fan opinion as the full story.</li>
    </ul>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Vocabulary Bank</h3>
  <section class="investment-box investment-box-definition">
    <ul>
      <li><strong>IST</strong>: Indian Standard Time</li>
      <li><strong>delayed viewing</strong>: watching after the event has finished</li>
      <li><strong>spoiler</strong>: result revealed before you watch</li>
      <li><strong>start time</strong>: scheduled beginning of match or event</li>
      <li><strong>session</strong>: part of a long event</li>
      <li><strong>overnight result</strong>: result that happened while you slept</li>
    </ul>
  </section>
