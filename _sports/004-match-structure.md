  <h2 class="act-section-heading"><span>4</span>Match Structure</h2>
  <p class="act-essence-box">Every sport has a structure: time, scoring unit, stage, and method of deciding the winner. Learn this first and the report becomes easier.</p>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Why This Chapter Matters</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>Every sport has a structure: time, scoring unit, stage, and method of deciding the winner. Learn this first and the report becomes easier.</p>
    <p>This chapter gives you the background that childhood sports followers usually collect slowly through repeated matches, arguments, highlights, and newspaper reports. Read it as a foundation, then attach the ideas to real sports news using the source links below.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Core Material</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>Cricket is divided into innings and overs. Football is divided into halves, added time, and sometimes extra time or penalties. Tennis is divided into points, games, sets, and match. Badminton uses games to 21 points. Hockey has quarters. F1 has laps and race distance.</p>
    <p>Also learn competition structure. A league rewards consistency across many matches. A knockout rewards survival. A group stage combines both: teams collect points first, then qualify.</p>
    <p>When reading a report, identify the structure before the drama. A late goal in football, a wicket in the death overs, or a tie-break in tennis matters because of where it occurs inside the structure.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Worked Example</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>Suppose a headline uses this topic but gives very little background. Do not begin by arguing. First identify the event and competition. Second identify the role of the main player, team, number, or condition. Third explain the consequence in one calm sentence. In this chapter, the first words to watch are innings, half, set.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Common Mistakes</h3>
  <section class="investment-box investment-box-event">
    <ul>
      <li>Reading only the headline and missing the match context.</li>
      <li>Using a term in conversation before connecting it to a real score, table, player, or event.</li>
      <li>Treating one clip, one statistic, or one fan opinion as the full story.</li>
    </ul>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Vocabulary Bank</h3>
  <section class="investment-box investment-box-definition">
    <ul>
      <li><strong>innings</strong>: one team&#x27;s turn to bat in cricket</li>
      <li><strong>half</strong>: one of two main football periods</li>
      <li><strong>set</strong>: a scoring unit in tennis</li>
      <li><strong>quarter</strong>: one of four periods in hockey or basketball</li>
      <li><strong>knockout</strong>: elimination stage where the loser exits</li>
      <li><strong>group stage</strong>: round where teams collect points before qualification</li>
    </ul>
  </section>
