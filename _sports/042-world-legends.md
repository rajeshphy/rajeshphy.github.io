  <h2 class="act-section-heading"><span>42</span>World Legends</h2>
  <p class="act-essence-box">World legends help you understand international references in news, commentary, debates, and comparisons.</p>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Why This Chapter Matters</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>World legends help you understand international references in news, commentary, debates, and comparisons.</p>
    <p>This chapter gives you the background that childhood sports followers usually collect slowly through repeated matches, arguments, highlights, and newspaper reports. Read it as a foundation, then attach the ideas to real sports news using the source links below.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Core Material</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>Football news often references Pele, Lionel Messi, and Cristiano Ronaldo for greatness, scoring, legacy, and rivalry. Tennis news often references Serena Williams and Roger Federer for Grand Slams, dominance, style, and longevity.</p>
    <p>Athletics uses Usain Bolt as a shorthand for sprinting greatness. Basketball uses Michael Jordan for championship mentality and clutch performance. Boxing uses Muhammad Ali for heavyweight greatness, public personality, and athlete activism.</p>
    <p>Use legends to decode comparisons. When a commentator says a player has &#x27;Jordan-like mentality&#x27; or a &#x27;Federer-like touch&#x27;, they are borrowing a cultural reference.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Worked Example</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>Suppose a headline uses this topic but gives very little background. Do not begin by arguing. First identify the event and competition. Second identify the role of the main player, team, number, or condition. Third explain the consequence in one calm sentence. In this chapter, the first words to watch are GOAT debate, rivalry, dominance.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Common Mistakes</h3>
  <section class="investment-box investment-box-event">
    <ul>
      <li>Reading only the headline and missing the match context.</li>
      <li>Using a term in conversation before connecting it to a real score, table, player, or event.</li>
      <li>Treating one clip, one statistic, or one fan opinion as the full story.</li>
    </ul>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Vocabulary Bank</h3>
  <section class="investment-box investment-box-definition">
    <ul>
      <li><strong>GOAT debate</strong>: greatest-of-all-time argument</li>
      <li><strong>rivalry</strong>: long repeated contest between greats</li>
      <li><strong>dominance</strong>: sustained superiority</li>
      <li><strong>longevity</strong>: performing at high level for many years</li>
      <li><strong>clutch</strong>: decisive under pressure</li>
      <li><strong>cultural icon</strong>: athlete known beyond sport</li>
    </ul>
  </section>
