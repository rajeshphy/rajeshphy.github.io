  <h2 class="act-section-heading"><span>44</span>Match Preview, Report, and Recap</h2>
  <p class="act-essence-box">Preview, report, and recap are three different reading tasks. Preview prepares; report explains; recap compresses.</p>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Why This Chapter Matters</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>Preview, report, and recap are three different reading tasks. Preview prepares; report explains; recap compresses.</p>
    <p>This chapter gives you the background that childhood sports followers usually collect slowly through repeated matches, arguments, highlights, and newspaper reports. Read it as a foundation, then attach the ideas to real sports news using the source links below.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Core Material</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>A preview is written before the match. It discusses team news, conditions, stakes, key players, and likely tactics. It teaches what to watch.</p>
    <p>A report is written after the match. It gives result, turning points, standout performers, and consequence. A recap is shorter and useful when you need quick memory.</p>
    <p>Read preview before highlights if possible. Then compare it with the report. This trains prediction and correction.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Worked Example</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>Suppose a headline uses this topic but gives very little background. Do not begin by arguing. First identify the event and competition. Second identify the role of the main player, team, number, or condition. Third explain the consequence in one calm sentence. In this chapter, the first words to watch are preview, report, recap.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Common Mistakes</h3>
  <section class="investment-box investment-box-event">
    <ul>
      <li>Reading only the headline and missing the match context.</li>
      <li>Using a term in conversation before connecting it to a real score, table, player, or event.</li>
      <li>Treating one clip, one statistic, or one fan opinion as the full story.</li>
    </ul>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Vocabulary Bank</h3>
  <section class="investment-box investment-box-definition">
    <ul>
      <li><strong>preview</strong>: before-match article</li>
      <li><strong>report</strong>: after-match article</li>
      <li><strong>recap</strong>: short post-event summary</li>
      <li><strong>team news</strong>: selection and injury information</li>
      <li><strong>key battle</strong>: important player-vs-player or role contest</li>
      <li><strong>post-match quotes</strong>: comments after event</li>
    </ul>
  </section>
