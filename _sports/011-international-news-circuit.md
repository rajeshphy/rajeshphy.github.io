  <h2 class="act-section-heading"><span>11</span>International News Circuit</h2>
  <p class="act-essence-box">International sports news moves through a circuit: schedule, squad, live score, table, report, ranking, and reaction.</p>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Why This Chapter Matters</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>International sports news moves through a circuit: schedule, squad, live score, table, report, ranking, and reaction.</p>
    <p>This chapter gives you the background that childhood sports followers usually collect slowly through repeated matches, arguments, highlights, and newspaper reports. Read it as a foundation, then attach the ideas to real sports news using the source links below.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Core Material</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>Start with official schedule and squad. This tells you whether the event is a bilateral series, league fixture, qualifier, world championship, or multi-sport event.</p>
    <p>During the match, follow live score and table consequence. After the match, read one report for turning points and one analysis for what it means next.</p>
    <p>For India versus another country, build one file in your notebook: opponent strength, key Indian player, event format, previous meeting, and what result changes.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Worked Example</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>Suppose a headline uses this topic but gives very little background. Do not begin by arguing. First identify the event and competition. Second identify the role of the main player, team, number, or condition. Third explain the consequence in one calm sentence. In this chapter, the first words to watch are official release, squad announcement, live blog.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Common Mistakes</h3>
  <section class="investment-box investment-box-event">
    <ul>
      <li>Reading only the headline and missing the match context.</li>
      <li>Using a term in conversation before connecting it to a real score, table, player, or event.</li>
      <li>Treating one clip, one statistic, or one fan opinion as the full story.</li>
    </ul>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Vocabulary Bank</h3>
  <section class="investment-box investment-box-definition">
    <ul>
      <li><strong>official release</strong>: announcement from governing body or team</li>
      <li><strong>squad announcement</strong>: list of selected players</li>
      <li><strong>live blog</strong>: minute-by-minute or over-by-over updates</li>
      <li><strong>reaction</strong>: quotes and debate after the result</li>
      <li><strong>analysis</strong>: article explaining causes and consequences</li>
      <li><strong>calendar</strong>: schedule of upcoming events</li>
    </ul>
  </section>
