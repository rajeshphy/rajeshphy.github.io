  <h2 class="act-section-heading"><span>5</span>Scorelines, Results, and Tables</h2>
  <p class="act-essence-box">A score is not only a number. It tells margin, control, pressure, and consequence in the table or ranking.</p>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Why This Chapter Matters</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>A score is not only a number. It tells margin, control, pressure, and consequence in the table or ranking.</p>
    <p>This chapter gives you the background that childhood sports followers usually collect slowly through repeated matches, arguments, highlights, and newspaper reports. Read it as a foundation, then attach the ideas to real sports news using the source links below.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Core Material</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>Read scorelines by sport. In football, 1-0 often means a narrow win. In cricket, &#x27;won by 6 wickets&#x27; means the chasing team had six wickets left; &#x27;won by 40 runs&#x27; means the defending team stopped the chase short. In tennis, 6-4, 7-6 tells set control and tie-break pressure.</p>
    <p>Tables convert results into position. In football, wins and draws produce points, while goal difference separates teams. In cricket group tournaments, net run rate can decide qualification. In league sports, games played matters because two teams may not have played equal fixtures.</p>
    <p>Always ask: Did the result change qualification? Did it improve ranking? Did it damage confidence? Did it expose a weakness?</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Worked Example</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>Suppose a headline uses this topic but gives very little background. Do not begin by arguing. First identify the event and competition. Second identify the role of the main player, team, number, or condition. Third explain the consequence in one calm sentence. In this chapter, the first words to watch are margin, points table, goal difference.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Common Mistakes</h3>
  <section class="investment-box investment-box-event">
    <ul>
      <li>Reading only the headline and missing the match context.</li>
      <li>Using a term in conversation before connecting it to a real score, table, player, or event.</li>
      <li>Treating one clip, one statistic, or one fan opinion as the full story.</li>
    </ul>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Vocabulary Bank</h3>
  <section class="investment-box investment-box-definition">
    <ul>
      <li><strong>margin</strong>: size of win or loss</li>
      <li><strong>points table</strong>: competition ranking based on results</li>
      <li><strong>goal difference</strong>: goals scored minus goals conceded</li>
      <li><strong>net run rate</strong>: cricket rate metric used in limited-overs tables</li>
      <li><strong>games in hand</strong>: matches a team still has compared with rivals</li>
      <li><strong>qualification</strong>: earning a place in the next stage</li>
    </ul>
  </section>
