  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Why This Chapter Matters</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>Archery and shooting are precision sports. They reward repetition, calm, scoring consistency, and pressure control.</p>
    <p>This chapter gives you the background that childhood sports followers usually collect slowly through repeated matches, arguments, highlights, and newspaper reports. Read it as a foundation, then attach the ideas to real sports news using the source links below.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Core Material</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>Archery has ranking rounds, elimination matches, sets or cumulative scoring depending on format, and shoot-offs when tied. Recurve archery is the Olympic form; compound archery is important in world events but not the standard Olympic bow category.</p>
    <p>Shooting uses qualification and final rounds, with decimal scoring in several events. Small differences decide medals, so consistency matters.</p>
    <p>Deepika Kumari is a natural Jharkhand anchor for learning archery terms: recurve, end, target, ranking round, release, grouping, and shoot-off.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Vocabulary Bank</h3>
  <section class="investment-box investment-box-definition">
    <ul>
      <li><strong>recurve</strong>: Olympic bow style</li>
      <li><strong>compound</strong>: bow style with pulley system</li>
      <li><strong>end</strong>: set of arrows shot before scoring</li>
      <li><strong>shoot-off</strong>: tie-break arrow or shot</li>
      <li><strong>grouping</strong>: closeness of arrows or shots</li>
      <li><strong>qualification</strong>: round deciding seeding or entry</li>
    </ul>
  </section>
