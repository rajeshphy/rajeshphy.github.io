  <h2 class="act-section-heading"><span>21</span>IPL, Auction, and Franchise Language</h2>
  <p class="act-essence-box">Franchise cricket adds business vocabulary to cricket vocabulary: purse, retention, release, role balance, and auction strategy.</p>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Why This Chapter Matters</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>Franchise cricket adds business vocabulary to cricket vocabulary: purse, retention, release, role balance, and auction strategy.</p>
    <p>This chapter gives you the background that childhood sports followers usually collect slowly through repeated matches, arguments, highlights, and newspaper reports. Read it as a foundation, then attach the ideas to real sports news using the source links below.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Core Material</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>An IPL squad is not built by fame alone. Teams need openers, middle-order stability, finishers, wicket-keepers, domestic players, overseas players, pace, spin, and backups.</p>
    <p>Auction language includes base price, bid, purse, retention, release, overseas slot, uncapped player, and marquee player. A high price usually means role scarcity, not only popularity.</p>
    <p>During auction news, ask which problem the player solves. Does the team need death bowling, spin hitting, left-hand balance, captaincy, or bench depth?</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Vocabulary Bank</h3>
  <section class="investment-box investment-box-definition">
    <ul>
      <li><strong>purse</strong>: money available to buy players</li>
      <li><strong>base price</strong>: starting auction price</li>
      <li><strong>retention</strong>: player kept before auction</li>
      <li><strong>release</strong>: player let go by franchise</li>
      <li><strong>overseas slot</strong>: place for non-Indian player</li>
      <li><strong>impact player</strong>: substitution rule used in IPL</li>
    </ul>
  </section>
