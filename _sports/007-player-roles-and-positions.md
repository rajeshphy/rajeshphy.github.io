  <h2 class="act-section-heading"><span>7</span>Player Roles and Positions</h2>
  <p class="act-essence-box">A sports conversation becomes sharper when you describe the job, not only the name. Roles explain why a player matters.</p>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Why This Chapter Matters</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>A sports conversation becomes sharper when you describe the job, not only the name. Roles explain why a player matters.</p>
    <p>This chapter gives you the background that childhood sports followers usually collect slowly through repeated matches, arguments, highlights, and newspaper reports. Read it as a foundation, then attach the ideas to real sports news using the source links below.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Core Material</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>In cricket, an opener handles the new ball, an anchor stabilizes, a finisher closes a chase, a spinner controls pace and turn, and an all-rounder balances the team. In football, a striker scores, a winger stretches play, a midfielder connects phases, and a defender protects space.</p>
    <p>In Olympic sports, roles are sometimes event-based. A javelin thrower needs approach speed, release angle, and strength. An archer needs consistency, aim, and pressure control. A badminton player needs rally construction, court coverage, and net play.</p>
    <p>When a player is selected, ask what role the team needed. This is better than saying only &#x27;he is famous&#x27; or &#x27;she is in good form.&#x27;</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Worked Example</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>Suppose a headline uses this topic but gives very little background. Do not begin by arguing. First identify the event and competition. Second identify the role of the main player, team, number, or condition. Third explain the consequence in one calm sentence. In this chapter, the first words to watch are opener, finisher, playmaker.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Common Mistakes</h3>
  <section class="investment-box investment-box-event">
    <ul>
      <li>Reading only the headline and missing the match context.</li>
      <li>Using a term in conversation before connecting it to a real score, table, player, or event.</li>
      <li>Treating one clip, one statistic, or one fan opinion as the full story.</li>
    </ul>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Vocabulary Bank</h3>
  <section class="investment-box investment-box-definition">
    <ul>
      <li><strong>opener</strong>: cricket batter who starts the innings</li>
      <li><strong>finisher</strong>: player trusted to close a chase or match</li>
      <li><strong>playmaker</strong>: footballer who creates attacking moves</li>
      <li><strong>defender</strong>: player mainly responsible for stopping attacks</li>
      <li><strong>all-rounder</strong>: cricketer who contributes with bat and ball</li>
      <li><strong>specialist</strong>: player chosen for one primary skill</li>
    </ul>
  </section>
