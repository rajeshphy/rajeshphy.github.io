  <h2 class="act-section-heading"><span>23</span>Reading Cricket Scorecards</h2>
  <p class="act-essence-box">A cricket scorecard is a compressed story. It shows innings shape, batting roles, bowling control, wickets, and match result.</p>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Why This Chapter Matters</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>A cricket scorecard is a compressed story. It shows innings shape, batting roles, bowling control, wickets, and match result.</p>
    <p>This chapter gives you the background that childhood sports followers usually collect slowly through repeated matches, arguments, highlights, and newspaper reports. Read it as a foundation, then attach the ideas to real sports news using the source links below.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Core Material</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>Start with team totals and result. Then read batting: runs, balls, fours, sixes, strike rate, and dismissal. Then read bowling: overs, maidens, runs, wickets, and economy.</p>
    <p>Fall of wickets shows collapse or partnership timing. Extras show discipline. Required rate and run rate show chase pressure. A scorecard can tell the match story even before you read the report.</p>
    <p>Use a three-line summary: top scorer and role, best bowler and method, turning point in the innings.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Worked Example</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>Suppose a headline uses this topic but gives very little background. Do not begin by arguing. First identify the event and competition. Second identify the role of the main player, team, number, or condition. Third explain the consequence in one calm sentence. In this chapter, the first words to watch are not out, extras, maidens.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Common Mistakes</h3>
  <section class="investment-box investment-box-event">
    <ul>
      <li>Reading only the headline and missing the match context.</li>
      <li>Using a term in conversation before connecting it to a real score, table, player, or event.</li>
      <li>Treating one clip, one statistic, or one fan opinion as the full story.</li>
    </ul>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Vocabulary Bank</h3>
  <section class="investment-box investment-box-definition">
    <ul>
      <li><strong>not out</strong>: batter remained undismissed</li>
      <li><strong>extras</strong>: runs not credited to batter</li>
      <li><strong>maidens</strong>: overs with no runs off the bat or extras</li>
      <li><strong>fall of wickets</strong>: score at each dismissal</li>
      <li><strong>run rate</strong>: runs per over scored</li>
      <li><strong>economy rate</strong>: runs per over conceded</li>
    </ul>
  </section>
