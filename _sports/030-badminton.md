  <h2 class="act-section-heading"><span>30</span>Badminton</h2>
  <p class="act-essence-box">Badminton is a fast rally sport where court control, rhythm change, and errors matter as much as powerful smashes.</p>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Why This Chapter Matters</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>Badminton is a fast rally sport where court control, rhythm change, and errors matter as much as powerful smashes.</p>
    <p>This chapter gives you the background that childhood sports followers usually collect slowly through repeated matches, arguments, highlights, and newspaper reports. Read it as a foundation, then attach the ideas to real sports news using the source links below.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Core Material</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>A game is usually played to 21 points with rally scoring. Players score whether serving or receiving. Matches are best of three games.</p>
    <p>Shots include serve, clear, drop, smash, drive, lift, and net shot. Good players move the opponent front-back and side-side before attacking.</p>
    <p>In Indian sports news, badminton often appears through BWF rankings, draws, Super Series or World Tour events, Olympic qualification, and head-to-head records.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Vocabulary Bank</h3>
  <section class="investment-box investment-box-definition">
    <ul>
      <li><strong>rally</strong>: continuous exchange before a point ends</li>
      <li><strong>smash</strong>: hard downward attacking shot</li>
      <li><strong>drop</strong>: soft shot falling near net</li>
      <li><strong>clear</strong>: high deep shot to back court</li>
      <li><strong>lift</strong>: defensive shot upward to back court</li>
      <li><strong>net play</strong>: shots near the net</li>
    </ul>
  </section>
