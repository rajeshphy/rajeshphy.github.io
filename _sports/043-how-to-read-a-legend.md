  <h2 class="act-section-heading"><span>43</span>How to Read a Legend</h2>
  <p class="act-essence-box">A legend is not only a record. A legend is a career with role, era, rivals, signature moments, and cultural meaning.</p>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Why This Chapter Matters</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>A legend is not only a record. A legend is a career with role, era, rivals, signature moments, and cultural meaning.</p>
    <p>This chapter gives you the background that childhood sports followers usually collect slowly through repeated matches, arguments, highlights, and newspaper reports. Read it as a foundation, then attach the ideas to real sports news using the source links below.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Core Material</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>Read a legend in six boxes: sport, role, era, signature skill, biggest stage, and legacy. This method works for Dhoni, Deepika, Pele, Serena, Bolt, or any athlete.</p>
    <p>Compare fairly. Different eras have different rules, equipment, fitness, media pressure, and tournament structures. Numbers must be read with context.</p>
    <p>Look for signature moments: final performance, comeback, record, rivalry win, or historic first. These moments become shorthand in sports conversation.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Worked Example</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>Suppose a headline uses this topic but gives very little background. Do not begin by arguing. First identify the event and competition. Second identify the role of the main player, team, number, or condition. Third explain the consequence in one calm sentence. In this chapter, the first words to watch are era context, signature moment, legacy debate.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Common Mistakes</h3>
  <section class="investment-box investment-box-event">
    <ul>
      <li>Reading only the headline and missing the match context.</li>
      <li>Using a term in conversation before connecting it to a real score, table, player, or event.</li>
      <li>Treating one clip, one statistic, or one fan opinion as the full story.</li>
    </ul>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Vocabulary Bank</h3>
  <section class="investment-box investment-box-definition">
    <ul>
      <li><strong>era context</strong>: conditions of the period in which athlete played</li>
      <li><strong>signature moment</strong>: famous decisive event</li>
      <li><strong>legacy debate</strong>: argument about long-term importance</li>
      <li><strong>peak</strong>: best period of performance</li>
      <li><strong>consistency</strong>: repeated performance level</li>
      <li><strong>influence</strong>: effect on sport or society</li>
    </ul>
  </section>
