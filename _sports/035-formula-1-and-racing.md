  <h2 class="act-section-heading"><span>35</span>Formula 1 and Racing</h2>
  <p class="act-essence-box">F1 is a racing championship where driver skill, car performance, tyre strategy, pit stops, and team decisions combine.</p>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Why This Chapter Matters</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>F1 is a racing championship where driver skill, car performance, tyre strategy, pit stops, and team decisions combine.</p>
    <p>This chapter gives you the background that childhood sports followers usually collect slowly through repeated matches, arguments, highlights, and newspaper reports. Read it as a foundation, then attach the ideas to real sports news using the source links below.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Core Material</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>A race weekend includes practice, qualifying, and race. Qualifying decides grid position. Pole position is first place on the starting grid.</p>
    <p>Tyres and pit stops shape strategy. A faster car can lose through poor timing, safety car interruptions, or tyre degradation. Teams compete for constructor points while drivers compete for driver standings.</p>
    <p>When reading F1, separate pace, reliability, strategy, and driver error. A result rarely comes from only one cause.</p>
  </section>
