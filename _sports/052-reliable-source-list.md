  <p class="act-chapter-kicker">Sports Knowledge Chapter 52</p>
  <h2 class="act-section-heading"><span>52</span>Reliable Source List</h2>
  <p class="act-essence-box">Good learning depends on good sources. Use official sources for facts, score sources for results, and reports for explanation.</p>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Why This Chapter Matters</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>Good learning depends on good sources. Use official sources for facts, score sources for results, and reports for explanation.</p>
    <p>This chapter gives you the background that childhood sports followers usually collect slowly through repeated matches, arguments, highlights, and newspaper reports. Read it as a foundation, then attach the ideas to real sports news using the source links below.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Core Material</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>For cricket, start with ICC, BCCI, and ESPNcricinfo. For football, start with FIFA, league websites, and UEFA for European competitions. For Olympic sports, start with Olympics, World Athletics, World Archery, BWF, FIH, and sport federations.</p>
    <p>Use news reports for narrative but verify big claims with official schedules, scorecards, or federation announcements. Social media is useful for clips, not for final truth.</p>
    <p>Keep a source ladder: official page first, live score second, match report third, opinion fourth. The lower you go, the more careful you become.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Worked Example</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>Suppose a headline uses this topic but gives very little background. Do not begin by arguing. First identify the event and competition. Second identify the role of the main player, team, number, or condition. Third explain the consequence in one calm sentence. In this chapter, the first words to watch are primary source, secondary source, verification.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Common Mistakes</h3>
  <section class="investment-box investment-box-event">
    <ul>
      <li>Reading only the headline and missing the match context.</li>
      <li>Using a term in conversation before connecting it to a real score, table, player, or event.</li>
      <li>Treating one clip, one statistic, or one fan opinion as the full story.</li>
    </ul>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Vocabulary Bank</h3>
  <section class="investment-box investment-box-definition">
    <ul>
      <li><strong>primary source</strong>: official body or direct data source</li>
      <li><strong>secondary source</strong>: reporting or analysis based on event</li>
      <li><strong>verification</strong>: checking claim against reliable source</li>
      <li><strong>score archive</strong>: stored past results</li>
      <li><strong>official profile</strong>: athlete page from governing body</li>
      <li><strong>source ladder</strong>: order of trust for information</li>
    </ul>
  </section>

  <section class="investment-box investment-box-law">
    <ul>
      <li><a href="https://olympics.com/en/">Olympics</a>: Olympic sports, athlete explainers, schedules, and event history.</li>
      <li><a href="https://www.bbc.com/sport">BBC Sport</a>: clear international match reports and broad sports context.</li>
      <li><a href="https://www.espn.com/">ESPN</a>: global sports news, standings, schedules, and professional league coverage.</li>
    </ul>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Study Path</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>Read this chapter once for meaning, then open one source below and find a real example of the topic. Add three notes to your sports notebook: one vocabulary word, one sentence of context, and one question for later.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Practice</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>Make bookmarks for ICC, ESPNcricinfo, FIFA, Olympics, BWF, World Athletics, and World Archery.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Chapter Takeaway</h3>
  <section class="investment-box investment-box-theory">
    <p>The chapter is complete when you can explain the topic in your own words and connect it to one real match, athlete, table, ranking, or news report.</p>
