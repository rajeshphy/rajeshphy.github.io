  <h2 class="act-section-heading"><span>51</span>Personal Sports Dictionary</h2>
  <p class="act-essence-box">Your dictionary should come from real reading. It should contain words you actually meet in headlines and commentary.</p>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Why This Chapter Matters</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>Your dictionary should come from real reading. It should contain words you actually meet in headlines and commentary.</p>
    <p>This chapter gives you the background that childhood sports followers usually collect slowly through repeated matches, arguments, highlights, and newspaper reports. Read it as a foundation, then attach the ideas to real sports news using the source links below.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Core Material</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>Divide the dictionary by sport and by function. Sport sections: cricket, football, tennis, badminton, hockey, athletics, archery, kabaddi, F1. Function sections: scoring, roles, tactics, statistics, tournaments, injuries, transfers, and rankings.</p>
    <p>Every entry needs meaning and sentence. Example: &#x27;seed: a player placed in a draw according to ranking. Sentence: The top seed avoided the strongest opponent in the first round.&#x27;</p>
    <p>Review by deleting or merging. If two words belong together, connect them. For example, ranking, seed, draw, and qualification belong in one family.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Worked Example</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>Suppose a headline uses this topic but gives very little background. Do not begin by arguing. First identify the event and competition. Second identify the role of the main player, team, number, or condition. Third explain the consequence in one calm sentence. In this chapter, the first words to watch are entry, word family, definition.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Common Mistakes</h3>
  <section class="investment-box investment-box-event">
    <ul>
      <li>Reading only the headline and missing the match context.</li>
      <li>Using a term in conversation before connecting it to a real score, table, player, or event.</li>
      <li>Treating one clip, one statistic, or one fan opinion as the full story.</li>
    </ul>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Vocabulary Bank</h3>
  <section class="investment-box investment-box-definition">
    <ul>
      <li><strong>entry</strong>: one dictionary item</li>
      <li><strong>word family</strong>: related vocabulary group</li>
      <li><strong>definition</strong>: meaning</li>
      <li><strong>usage</strong>: how the word appears in a sentence</li>
      <li><strong>revision</strong>: reviewing and improving notes</li>
      <li><strong>cross-reference</strong>: link between related terms</li>
    </ul>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Study Path</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>Read this chapter once for meaning, then open one source below and find a real example of the topic. Add three notes to your sports notebook: one vocabulary word, one sentence of context, and one question for later.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Practice</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>Create twenty entries: five cricket, five football, five Olympic sports, and five general news words.</p>
  </section>

  <section class="investment-box investment-box-theory">
