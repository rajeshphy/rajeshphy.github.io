  <h2 class="act-section-heading"><span>15</span>Rankings, Qualification, and Seeding</h2>
  <p class="act-essence-box">Ranking tells position, qualification tells entry, and seeding controls draw advantage.</p>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Why This Chapter Matters</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>Ranking tells position, qualification tells entry, and seeding controls draw advantage.</p>
    <p>This chapter gives you the background that childhood sports followers usually collect slowly through repeated matches, arguments, highlights, and newspaper reports. Read it as a foundation, then attach the ideas to real sports news using the source links below.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Core Material</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>Ranking systems differ. Cricket team rankings use match results over time. Tennis and badminton rankings use tournament points. Archery and athletics rankings may combine event results and qualification standards.</p>
    <p>Qualification is the gate. A team or athlete may need ranking points, qualifying marks, continental quota, tournament position, or selection trial performance.</p>
    <p>Seeding protects stronger players or teams from meeting too early. A bad seed can create a harder path even for a strong athlete.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Vocabulary Bank</h3>
  <section class="investment-box investment-box-definition">
    <ul>
      <li><strong>ranking points</strong>: points used to order players or teams</li>
      <li><strong>quota</strong>: limited entry place for a country or athlete</li>
      <li><strong>seed</strong>: draw position based on ranking or status</li>
      <li><strong>draw</strong>: bracket or fixture path</li>
      <li><strong>qualification mark</strong>: required time, distance, score, or result</li>
      <li><strong>wild card</strong>: special entry not earned by normal ranking</li>
    </ul>
  </section>
