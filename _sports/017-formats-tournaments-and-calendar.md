  <h2 class="act-section-heading"><span>17</span>Formats, Tournaments, and Calendar</h2>
  <p class="act-essence-box">Cricket vocabulary changes by format. Test, ODI, T20I, domestic cricket, and franchise cricket reward different skills.</p>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Why This Chapter Matters</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>Cricket vocabulary changes by format. Test, ODI, T20I, domestic cricket, and franchise cricket reward different skills.</p>
    <p>This chapter gives you the background that childhood sports followers usually collect slowly through repeated matches, arguments, highlights, and newspaper reports. Read it as a foundation, then attach the ideas to real sports news using the source links below.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Core Material</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>Test cricket is long-form cricket played over days. It rewards patience, technique, bowling spells, field plans, and changing pitch conditions. ODI cricket is 50 overs per side and balances patience with scoring rate. T20 cricket is 20 overs per side and rewards power, match-ups, and high-risk decision making.</p>
    <p>International tournaments are run by ICC and boards; domestic tournaments develop players; IPL and other franchise leagues mix domestic and overseas players in a commercial league format.</p>
    <p>Calendar awareness matters. A player may move from Test series to IPL to World Cup preparation. Form in one format does not automatically transfer to another.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Worked Example</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>Suppose a headline uses this topic but gives very little background. Do not begin by arguing. First identify the event and competition. Second identify the role of the main player, team, number, or condition. Third explain the consequence in one calm sentence. In this chapter, the first words to watch are Test, ODI, T20I.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Common Mistakes</h3>
  <section class="investment-box investment-box-event">
    <ul>
      <li>Reading only the headline and missing the match context.</li>
      <li>Using a term in conversation before connecting it to a real score, table, player, or event.</li>
      <li>Treating one clip, one statistic, or one fan opinion as the full story.</li>
    </ul>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Vocabulary Bank</h3>
  <section class="investment-box investment-box-definition">
    <ul>
      <li><strong>Test</strong>: longest international cricket format</li>
      <li><strong>ODI</strong>: 50-over one-day international</li>
      <li><strong>T20I</strong>: 20-over international</li>
      <li><strong>Ranji Trophy</strong>: major Indian first-class domestic tournament</li>
      <li><strong>IPL</strong>: Indian Premier League franchise tournament</li>
      <li><strong>bilateral series</strong>: series between two countries</li>
    </ul>
  </section>
