  <h2 class="act-section-heading"><span>29</span>Tennis</h2>
  <p class="act-essence-box">Tennis is an individual duel built around serve, return, break points, surface, ranking, and tournament draw.</p>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Why This Chapter Matters</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>Tennis is an individual duel built around serve, return, break points, surface, ranking, and tournament draw.</p>
    <p>This chapter gives you the background that childhood sports followers usually collect slowly through repeated matches, arguments, highlights, and newspaper reports. Read it as a foundation, then attach the ideas to real sports news using the source links below.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Core Material</h3>
  <section class="sports-topic">
    <p>Scoring runs from points to games to sets to match. Love means zero, deuce means level at 40-40, advantage means one point from game. A tie-break decides a set when games are level by the tournament rule.</p>
    <p>Serve and return shape the match. Holding serve means winning your service game. Breaking serve means winning the opponent&#x27;s service game. Break points are pressure points.</p>
    <p>Surface changes style. Grass rewards serve and fast points, clay rewards rallies and patience, hard courts balance speed and bounce.</p>
  </section>

  <h3 class="investment-section-heading">Vocabulary Bank</h3>
  <section class="investment-box investment-box-definition">
    <ul>
      <li><strong>serve</strong>: shot that starts a point</li>
      <li><strong>break</strong>: winning opponent&#x27;s service game</li>
      <li><strong>hold</strong>: winning your own service game</li>
      <li><strong>deuce</strong>: 40-40 score</li>
      <li><strong>tie-break</strong>: special game to decide a set</li>
      <li><strong>Grand Slam</strong>: one of four biggest tennis tournaments</li>
    </ul>
  </section>
